Why calibration matters for LLMs

In seq2seq, sentence pairs come from a controlled corpus so the perplexity distribution is relatively homogeneous. For LLMs trained on web-scale data, a rare technical document and a common news article have very different baseline perplexities — a global threshold will falsely label all rare-domain text as members. Calibration approaches (e.g., Min-K%, zlib ratio, neighborhood comparisons) address this by normalizing perplexity against a reference distribution. This distinction is a central motivation for the LLM MIA literature in Lecture 5.
