01

Classifier Guidance

Bayes decomposition and the $\omega$-scaled reverse SDE

Why Conditional Generation

Real applications rarely want a sample from $P_{\mathbf{X}}$. They want a sample from $P_{\mathbf{X}\,|\,Y}$ for some signal $Y$:

  • Text-to-image: $Y$ is a prompt — "a cat in a spacesuit".
  • Class-conditional: $Y$ is a label — generate a "dog".
  • Inpainting / editing: $Y$ is a partial image, mask, or layout.
  • Inverse problems: $Y$ is a measurement (low-res, noisy, blurred).

Unconditional generation is the warm-up. Controllability is the product.

Two Approaches

Naive: train a separate conditional score $s_\theta(\mathbf{x}\,|\,Y=y)$ per condition.

Expensive; does not scale; needs paired $(X, Y)$ for every $Y$.

Classifier guidance: reuse a pretrained unconditional score.

Steer with a small auxiliary classifier on the noisy state.

Dhariwal & Nichol, "Diffusion Models Beat GANs on Image Synthesis", NeurIPS 2021.

Bayes Decomposition

Bayes' rule:

$$P_{\mathbf{X}\,|\,Y}(\mathbf{x}\,|\,y) = \frac{P_{Y\,|\,\mathbf{X}}(y\,|\,\mathbf{x})\,P_{\mathbf{X}}(\mathbf{x})}{P_Y(y)}.$$

Take $\nabla_{\mathbf{x}}\log$; the prior $P_Y(y)$ drops:

$$\nabla_{\mathbf{x}} \log P_{\mathbf{X}\,|\,Y}(\mathbf{x}\,|\,y) \;=\; \nabla_{\mathbf{x}} \log P_{\mathbf{X}}(\mathbf{x}) \;+\; \nabla_{\mathbf{x}} \log P_{Y\,|\,\mathbf{X}}(y\,|\,\mathbf{x}).$$

Conditional score = unconditional score + classifier gradient.

Classifier on Noisy Inputs

Sampling operates on noisy state $\mathbf{X}^{(n)}$.

Train a classifier matched to that noise level:

$$c_\phi(\mathbf{X}^{(n)}, y, n) \;\approx\; P_{Y\,|\,\mathbf{X}^{(n)}}(y\,|\,\mathbf{X}^{(n)}).$$

Standard classifier; noise index $n$ as extra input.

Trained on noisy images from the forward process.

Guided Reverse SDE

Replace unconditional score by the Bayes-decomposed conditional, classifier term amplified by $\omega > 1$:

$$d\bar{\mathbf{X}}_t = \Bigl(f - g^2\bigl(\nabla\log p_t(\bar{\mathbf{X}}_t) + \omega\,\nabla \log c_\phi(\bar{\mathbf{X}}_t, y, t)\bigr)\Bigr)dt + g\,d\bar{\mathbf{Z}}_t.$$

Larger $\omega$: sharper, more label-consistent samples.

Trades diversity for fidelity.

Inpainting

Conditioning on a fixed subset of the signal

What Is Inpainting?

Given an image with part of its pixels observed and the rest missing, fill in the missing region coherently with the rest.

  • Photo restoration (scratches, occlusions).
  • Object removal / replacement.
  • Consistent edit propagation around a mask.

Goal: $X^{(0)}$ with $\Omega(X^{(0)}) = Y$ (observed pixels match) and the rest sampled from $p_{\text{data}}$.

Setup

  • $\Omega$: pixel-subsampling operator (the mask).
  • $Y = \Omega(X^{(0)})$: observed pixels, fixed throughout.
  • $\bar{\Omega}$: complementary pixels — the unknowns.
  • $Z^{(t)} = \bar{\Omega}(X^{(t)})$ is the part we still diffuse.

Brownian motion decomposes coordinate-wise; the complement obeys its own SDE:

$$dZ^{(t)} = \bar{\Omega}(f)\bigl(Z^{(t)}, t\bigr)\,dt + \bar{\Omega}(g)(t)\,d\bar{W}^{(t)}.$$

The Ideal Conditional Score

For Anderson reverse on the unknowns, we need the conditional score:

$$\nabla_{Z^{(t)}}\,\log p_t\bigl(Z^{(t)}\,|\,\Omega(X^{(0)}) = Y\bigr).$$

Conditions on $Y$ at the clean end of the chain ($t = 0$).

Score network only saw fully-noisy training pairs $(X^{(t)}, t)$. Mismatch.

Need a way to inject $Y$ that the existing score net can consume.

Approximating the Conditional Score

Replace the clean observation with a noised version at level $t$:

$$p_t\bigl(Z^{(t)}\,|\,\Omega(X^{(0)}) = Y\bigr) \;\approx\; p_t\bigl(Z^{(t)}\,|\,\hat{\Omega}(X^{(t)}) = \zeta_t\bigr),$$

where $\zeta_t$ is the forward process applied to $Y$ up to time $t$:

$$\zeta_t \;=\; \sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_t}\,Y \;+\; \sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_t}\,\eta_t,\qquad \eta_t \sim \mathcal{N}(0,I).$$

Both sides now live at noise level $t$ — score net is in-domain.

Why It Works

  • Forward kernel is a known Gaussian — the noised $\zeta_t$ has the law of $\Omega(X^{(t)})$ for a true clean observation.
  • Score net sees the same input distribution it was trained on.
  • At $t = 0$: $\zeta_0 = Y$ — boundary recovers the true condition.

At each step, paste $\zeta_t$ into observed pixels, sample unknowns with the score net.

Inpainting Sampler

Score-Guided Inpainting
  1. Sample $X^{(N)} \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I)$ on the full image.
  2. For $n = N, N{-}1, \ldots, 1$:
    1. Sample $\epsilon_n \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I)$.
    2. Form noised observation $\zeta_n = \beta_n\,Y + \gamma_n\,\epsilon_n$.
    3. One reverse-SDE step using $\bar{\Omega}\bigl(s_\theta([\bar{Z}^{(n)}; \zeta_n], n)\bigr)$.
03

Classifier-Free Guidance

Guidance without an extra classifier

Why Drop the Classifier?

Classifier guidance: a second network trained on noisy inputs.

Evaluated at every sampling step.

Hard to train in the high-noise regime.

Goal: one model providing both conditional and unconditional scores.

Ho & Salimans, "Classifier-Free Diffusion Guidance", NeurIPS Workshop 2021.

The CFG Identity

Recall the Bayes decomposition:

$$\nabla \log p_t(y\,|\,\mathbf{x}) \;=\; \nabla \log p_t(\mathbf{x}\,|\,y) - \nabla \log p_t(\mathbf{x}).$$

Substitute into the classifier-guided score:

$$\nabla \log p_t(\mathbf{x}) + \omega\,\nabla \log p_t(y\,|\,\mathbf{x}) \;=\; \omega\,\nabla \log p_t(\mathbf{x}\,|\,y) + (1-\omega)\,\nabla \log p_t(\mathbf{x}).$$

Exercise. Verify the substitution.

A Dual-Role Network

Train $\epsilon_\theta(\mathbf{X}^{(n)}, y, n)$ where $y$ is the label or null token $\varnothing$:

  • $\epsilon_\theta(\mathbf{X}^{(n)}, y, n)$: conditional noise under label $y$.
  • $\epsilon_\theta(\mathbf{X}^{(n)}, \varnothing, n)$: unconditional noise.

Training trick. Replace $y$ by $\varnothing$ with probability $p_\text{drop} \approx 0.1$–$0.2$.

Network learns both modes in a single pass.

CFG Sampling Rule

At inference, combine two forward passes:

$$\tilde{\epsilon}(\mathbf{X}^{(n)}, y, n) \;=\; \omega\,\epsilon_\theta(\mathbf{X}^{(n)}, y, n) - (\omega - 1)\,\epsilon_\theta(\mathbf{X}^{(n)}, \varnothing, n),\qquad \omega > 1.$$

Use $\tilde{\epsilon}$ in place of $\epsilon_\theta$ in any DDPM / DDIM sampler.

Push toward label-consistent mode (weight $\omega$); pull back with unconditional (weight $\omega-1$).

CFG in Practice

Design levers

  • Drop probability $p_\text{drop}$
  • Guidance scale $\omega$
  • Null-token embedding (learnable)

Trade-offs

  • Larger $\omega$: sharper, less diverse
  • Two passes per step: $2\times$ cost
  • No extra model to train

Discrete Diffusion

When data lives on $\{1, \ldots, K\}$ instead of $\mathbb{R}^d$

What Breaks in the Discrete Case

Continuous diffusion adds Gaussian noise.

Discrete data (tokens, categories) does not admit addition.

Substitute: random transitions.

Switch $x$ to a different value with some probability.

Additive noise replaced by stochastic transitions.

Forward, reverse, score, loss: same recipe.

Forward: Transition Matrices

Let $p_t \in [0,1]^K$ be the law of $X_t$.

Transition matrix $Q_t \in \mathbb{R}^{K\times K}$ with entry $Q_t(y, x) = \Pr(X_{t+1} = y\,|\,X_t = x)$:

$$p_{t+1} = Q_t\,p_t.$$

Valid noising: marginals converge $p_t \to \pi$ regardless of $p_0$.

Two standard choices: uniform and absorbing (masking).

Uniform vs. Absorbing

Uniform

Stay with prob $1-\epsilon$.

Otherwise jump uniformly to another token.

Stationary: uniform on $\{1,\ldots,K\}$.

Absorbing (mask)

Replace by $[\text{M}]$ at rate $\epsilon$.

Mask stays mask.

Stationary: all mass on $[\text{M}]$.

Exercise. Compute mixing time for both choices.

Continuous-Time Limit

Take $\Delta t \to 0$. Discrete updates become a linear ODE:

$$\frac{dp_t}{dt} = Q_t\,p_t,\qquad p_0 = p_\text{data}.$$

$Q_t$ now plays the role of a generator (rate matrix).

Reverse Rate Matrix

For $x \ne y$, the reverse generator:

$$\overline{Q}_t(y, x) \;=\; \frac{p_t(y)}{p_t(x)}\,Q_t(x, y).$$

Diagonal fixed by mass conservation:

$$\overline{Q}_t(x, x) \;=\; -\sum_{y \ne x}\overline{Q}_t(y, x).$$

Novel quantity: the marginal ratio $p_t(y)/p_t(x)$, the discrete analog of the score.

The Discrete Score Is a Ratio

Continuous analog of the score $\nabla_{\mathbf{x}} \log p_t(\mathbf{x})$ becomes the marginal-ratio vector:

$$s(x, t) = \bigl(p_t(1)/p_t(x),\; p_t(2)/p_t(x),\; \ldots,\; p_t(K)/p_t(x)\bigr)^\top \in [0, \infty)^K.$$

Learned by a neural network $s_\theta(x, t)$.

Continuous: gradient of log density.

Discrete: ratio of densities between states.

Squared Loss Fails

Naive: regress $s_\theta(x, t)_y$ onto the target ratio:

$$\mathcal{L}_\text{sq}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}\!\left[\bigl(s_\theta(x, t)_y - \tfrac{p_t(y)}{p_t(x)}\bigr)^2\right].$$

Minimizer can be negative.

Ratios are non-negative; the loss does not encode that constraint.

Need a loss whose minimizer is automatically positive.

Score Entropy Loss

Lou et al. (2024): the score entropy loss:

$$\mathcal{L}_\text{SE}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}_{x_t \sim p_t}\!\left[\sum_{y \ne x_t} w_{x_t, y}\!\left(s_\theta(x_t, t)_y - \tfrac{p_t(y)}{p_t(x_t)}\,\log s_\theta(x_t, t)_y + C\!\bigl(\tfrac{p_t(y)}{p_t(x_t)}\bigr)\right)\right].$$

Constant $C(a) = a(\log a - 1)$ makes the minimum zero.

The $\log s_\theta$ term forces positivity.

Exercise. Show the minimizer satisfies $s_{\theta^\star}(x, t)_y = p_t(y)/p_t(x)$ when $w_{x,y} > 0$.

Lou, Meng & Ermon, "Discrete Diffusion by Estimating Ratios of the Data Distribution", ICML 2024.

Sequence Space

Real text: $\mathcal{X} = \{1, \ldots, K\}^L$.

Direct transition matrix has $K^L \times K^L$ entries. Intractable.

Factored assumption: each token perturbed independently.

Implies the Hamming-sparsity condition:

$$Q_t(\mathbf{y}, \mathbf{x}) = 0\quad\text{whenever}\quad d_\text{H}(\mathbf{y}, \mathbf{x}) \geq 2.$$

Reverse process needs $s_\theta$ only on Hamming-distance-1 pairs.
