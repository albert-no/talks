01

Forward (Noising) Process

A fixed Markov chain that turns data into Gaussian noise

The forward chain

Fix a variance schedule $\{\beta_t\}_{t=1}^{T}$ with $0<\beta_t<1$. Define $\alpha_t\coloneqq 1-\beta_t$, $\bar\alpha_t\coloneqq\prod_{s=1}^{t}\alpha_s$.

$$q(x_{1:T}\mid x_0)\;=\;\prod_{t=1}^{T}q(x_t\mid x_{t-1}),\qquad q(x_t\mid x_{t-1})=\mathcal{N}\bigl(\sqrt{\alpha_t}\,x_{t-1},\;\beta_t I\bigr).$$

Each step contracts toward zero by $\sqrt{\alpha_t}$ and adds Gaussian noise of variance $\beta_t$. Variance-preserving: if $x_{t-1}$ has unit variance, so does $x_t$.

Closed form: $q(x_t\mid x_0)$

Lemma
$$q(x_t\mid x_0)\;=\;\mathcal{N}\bigl(\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0,\;(1-\bar\alpha_t)\,I\bigr).$$

Proof by induction. True for $t=1$ since $\bar\alpha_1=\alpha_1$. Assume $q(x_{t-1}\mid x_0)=\mathcal{N}(\sqrt{\bar\alpha_{t-1}}x_0,(1-\bar\alpha_{t-1})I)$. Then $x_t=\sqrt{\alpha_t}x_{t-1}+\sqrt{\beta_t}\,\eta$ with $\eta\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I)$ independent. Mean: $\sqrt{\alpha_t}\sqrt{\bar\alpha_{t-1}}x_0=\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}x_0$. Variance: $\alpha_t(1-\bar\alpha_{t-1})+\beta_t=\alpha_t-\alpha_t\bar\alpha_{t-1}+1-\alpha_t=1-\bar\alpha_t$. $\;\blacksquare$

The master reparameterization

Equivalently: for $\varepsilon\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I)$,

$$x_t\;=\;\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0\;+\;\sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\varepsilon.$$

Given any $(x_0,t,\varepsilon)$, generate $x_t$ directly without simulating the chain. Training never simulates more than one step at a time.

As $t\to T$ with $\bar\alpha_T\approx 0$:

$$x_T\;\approx\;\mathcal{N}(0,I).$$

The chain ends in (essentially) standard Gaussian noise — the prior we will sample from at generation time.

02

Reverse (Denoising) Process

Learned Gaussian kernels run the chain backwards

The reverse chain

Generative model:

$$p_\theta(x_{0:T})\;=\;p(x_T)\,\prod_{t=1}^{T}p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t),\qquad p(x_T)=\mathcal{N}(0,I).$$

Choose Gaussian reverse kernels:

$$p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)=\mathcal{N}\bigl(\mu_\theta(x_t,t),\,\Sigma_t\bigr).$$

$\Sigma_t$ is fixed (or separately learned); $\mu_\theta$ is the learned object — usually a neural network.

Generation: sample $x_T\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I)$, then $x_{T-1}\sim p_\theta,\ldots,x_0\sim p_\theta$.

Hierarchical VAE structure

Identify $X_0$ with the data, $(X_1,\ldots,X_T)$ with hierarchical latents. Then

  • $q(x_{1:T}\mid x_0)$: the encoder — fixed, not learned.
  • $p_\theta(x_{0:T})$: the decoder — learned.
  • $p(x_T)=\mathcal{N}(0,I)$: the prior over the deepest latent.

Diffusion is a hierarchical VAE with a frozen Gaussian-Markov encoder. Posterior collapse is impossible — the encoder cannot trivialize.

03

ELBO Decomposition

$L_0$ + $L_T$ + $\sum L_t$ — reading off the training signal

The diffusion ELBO

From Lecture 1, applied to this hierarchical VAE:

$$\log p_\theta(x_0)\;\ge\;\mathbb{E}_{q(x_{1:T}\mid x_0)}\!\left[\log p_\theta(x_{0:T})-\log q(x_{1:T}\mid x_0)\right].$$

Standard expansion gives

$$-\log p_\theta(x_0)\;\le\;L_0+L_T+\sum_{t=2}^{T}L_{t-1},$$

where each $L$ is interpreted below.

The boundary terms $L_0$ and $L_T$

$L_0$ — final-step reconstruction

$$L_0=-\mathbb{E}_{q(x_1\mid x_0)}\!\bigl[\log p_\theta(x_0\mid x_1)\bigr].$$

Decoder output likelihood at the cleanest step. Negligible for image-pixel models with discrete output (replaced by a one-step decoder).

$L_T$ — prior-matching

$$L_T=D_{\mathrm{KL}}\bigl(q(x_T\mid x_0)\,\Vert\,p(x_T)\bigr).$$

Constant w.r.t. $\theta$. Zero asymptotically (with $\bar\alpha_T\to 0$) since both sides become $\mathcal{N}(0,I)$.

The interior terms $L_t$ — learning signal

For $t=1,\ldots,T-1$:

$$L_t=\mathbb{E}_{q(x_t\mid x_0)}\!\bigl[D_{\mathrm{KL}}\bigl(q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0)\,\Vert\,p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)\bigr)\bigr].$$

The model's reverse kernel must approximate the true reverse conditional $q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0)$. This is where all the learning happens.

Crucial fact: $q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0)$ is Gaussian with closed form — we derive it next. Comparing two Gaussians, $L_t$ becomes a clean MSE loss.

04

The True Reverse Conditional

Closed form via completing the square — the heart of training

Setup — what we want to compute

Want: $q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0)$, i.e. the conditional law of $x_{t-1}$ given both the noisy state $x_t$ and the clean data $x_0$.

Bayes:

$$q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0)\;\propto\;\underbrace{q(x_t\mid x_{t-1})}_{\text{forward step}}\cdot\underbrace{q(x_{t-1}\mid x_0)}_{\text{closed form}}.$$

Both factors are Gaussian in $x_{t-1}$. Their product is Gaussian. Compute the mean and variance.

Closed form by completing the square

Derivation

Plug in the two Gaussian densities and collect terms quadratic in $x_{t-1}$:

$$q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0)\;\propto\;\exp\!\left(-\tfrac{(x_t-\sqrt{\alpha_t}x_{t-1})^2}{2\beta_t}-\tfrac{(x_{t-1}-\sqrt{\bar\alpha_{t-1}}x_0)^2}{2(1-\bar\alpha_{t-1})}\right).$$

Expand and complete the square in $x_{t-1}$. Collect $1/(2\sigma^2)$ coefficient: $\frac{\alpha_t}{\beta_t}+\frac{1}{1-\bar\alpha_{t-1}}=\frac{1-\bar\alpha_t}{\beta_t(1-\bar\alpha_{t-1})}$. So

$$\widetilde\beta_t\;\coloneqq\;\dfrac{1-\bar\alpha_{t-1}}{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\beta_t.$$

Mean of the true reverse conditional

Continuing the derivation

Collect the linear-in-$x_{t-1}$ coefficient: $\sqrt{\alpha_t}\,x_t/\beta_t+\sqrt{\bar\alpha_{t-1}}x_0/(1-\bar\alpha_{t-1})$. Divide by $1/\widetilde\beta_t$ to get the mean:

$$\mu_q(x_t,x_0)\;=\;\dfrac{\sqrt{\bar\alpha_{t-1}}\,\beta_t}{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0\;+\;\dfrac{\sqrt{\alpha_t}\,(1-\bar\alpha_{t-1})}{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,x_t.$$

Final form:

$$q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0)\;=\;\mathcal{N}\bigl(\mu_q(x_t,x_0),\,\widetilde\beta_t I\bigr).\;\blacksquare$$

$L_t$ becomes a Gaussian-Gaussian KL = MSE

Both $q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0)$ and $p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)$ are Gaussian with the same fixed variance (by convention, $\Sigma_t=\widetilde\beta_t I$). KL between equal-covariance Gaussians:

$$D_{\mathrm{KL}}\bigl(\mathcal{N}(\mu_q,\Sigma)\,\Vert\,\mathcal{N}(\mu_\theta,\Sigma)\bigr)\;=\;\dfrac{1}{2}\,(\mu_q-\mu_\theta)^\top\Sigma^{-1}(\mu_q-\mu_\theta).$$

For $\Sigma=\widetilde\beta_t I$:

$$L_t\;\propto\;\mathbb{E}_q\!\left[\,\|\mu_q(x_t,x_0)-\mu_\theta(x_t,t)\|^2\,\right].$$

Training reduces to MSE regression of $\mu_\theta(x_t,t)$ onto $\mu_q(x_t,x_0)$.

What's left: parameterizing $\mu_\theta$

Three equivalent parameterizations of the reverse mean:

  • Predict $x_0$ directly — train against the clean data.
  • Predict the noise $\varepsilon$ — the empirical workhorse.
  • Predict the score $\nabla_x\log p_t(x)$ — the SDE perspective.

All three are linked by the master reparameterization $x_t=\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}x_0+\sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\varepsilon$ and Tweedie's formula.

Lecture 3 derives all three views, the equivalences between them, and the information-theoretic interpretation of each forward step as a noisy channel.

Recap of Lecture 2

  • Forward: $q(x_t\mid x_{t-1})=\mathcal{N}(\sqrt{\alpha_t}x_{t-1},\beta_t I)$. Closed form $q(x_t\mid x_0)=\mathcal{N}(\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}x_0,(1-\bar\alpha_t)I)$.
  • Reverse: $p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)=\mathcal{N}(\mu_\theta(x_t,t),\Sigma_t)$. Generative chain runs backward.
  • Hierarchical VAE: latents $X_1,\ldots,X_T$, fixed encoder $q$, learned decoder $p_\theta$, no posterior collapse.
  • ELBO decomposition: $-\log p_\theta(x_0)\le L_0+L_T+\sum_{t=2}^{T}L_{t-1}$.
  • True reverse conditional: Gaussian, closed form via completing the square.
  • $L_t$ becomes MSE on the reverse-mean prediction.
