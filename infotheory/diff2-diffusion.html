01

Forward Process

Fixed Markov chain turning data into Gaussian noise

The Forward Chain

Schedule $\{\beta_t\}_{t=1}^T$, $0<\beta_t<1$. Define $\alpha_t\coloneqq 1-\beta_t$, $\bar\alpha_t\coloneqq\prod_{s=1}^t\alpha_s$.

$$q(x_t\mid x_{t-1}) = \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(\sqrt{\alpha_t}\,x_{t-1},\;\beta_t I\bigr).$$

Each step contracts toward zero, adds $\mathcal{N}(0,\beta_t I)$ noise.

Variance-preserving: unit-variance input stays unit-variance.

Closed Form: $q(x_t\mid x_0)$

Lemma.

$$q(x_t\mid x_0) = \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0,\;(1-\bar\alpha_t)I\bigr).$$
Proof by induction

Base: $t=1$, $\bar\alpha_1=\alpha_1$. Inductive step: $x_t=\sqrt{\alpha_t}x_{t-1}+\sqrt{\beta_t}\eta$. Mean: $\sqrt{\alpha_t\bar\alpha_{t-1}}\,x_0=\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0$. Variance: $\alpha_t(1-\bar\alpha_{t-1})+\beta_t=1-\bar\alpha_t$. $\;\blacksquare$

Master Reparameterization

For $\varepsilon\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I)$:

$$x_t = \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0 + \sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\varepsilon.$$

Given $(x_0,t,\varepsilon)$: jump to $x_t$ directly, no chain simulation.

As $t\to T$, $\bar\alpha_T\approx 0$:

$$x_T \approx \mathcal{N}(0,I).$$
02

Reverse Process

Learned Gaussian kernels run the chain backwards

The Reverse Chain

Generative model: $p(x_T)=\mathcal{N}(0,I)$, then:

$$p_\theta(x_{0:T}) = p(x_T)\prod_{t=1}^T p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t).$$

Learned Gaussian kernels:

$$p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t) = \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(\mu_\theta(x_t,t),\,\Sigma_t\bigr).$$

Generation: sample $x_T$, then run $x_{T-1},\ldots,x_0$ via $p_\theta$.

Hierarchical VAE Structure

Identify $X_0$ as data, $(X_1,\ldots,X_T)$ as hierarchical latents:

  • $q(x_{1:T}\mid x_0)$: encoder. Fixed, not learned.
  • $p_\theta(x_{0:T})$: decoder. Learned.
  • $p(x_T)=\mathcal{N}(0,I)$: prior over deepest latent.
Frozen Gaussian-Markov encoder: posterior collapse impossible.
03

ELBO Decomposition

$L_0$ + $L_T$ + $\sum L_t$: the training signal

The Diffusion ELBO

From Lecture 1, applied to this hierarchical VAE:

$$\log p_\theta(x_0) \ge \mathbb{E}_{q(x_{1:T}\mid x_0)}\!\bigl[\log p_\theta(x_{0:T}) - \log q(x_{1:T}\mid x_0)\bigr].$$

Expanding gives three types of terms:

$$-\log p_\theta(x_0) \le L_0 + L_T + \sum_{t=2}^T L_{t-1}.$$

Boundary Terms: $L_0$ and $L_T$

$L_0$: final reconstruction

$-\mathbb{E}_{q(x_1\mid x_0)}[\log p_\theta(x_0\mid x_1)]$.

Negligible for discrete pixel models.

$L_T$: prior matching

$D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(q(x_T\mid x_0)\Vert p(x_T)\bigr)$.

Constant in $\theta$. Vanishes as $\bar\alpha_T\to 0$.

Interior Terms: $L_t$ (Learning Signal)

For $t=1,\ldots,T-1$:

$$L_t = \mathbb{E}_{q(x_t\mid x_0)}\!\bigl[D_{\mathrm{KL}}\bigl(q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0)\,\Vert\,p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)\bigr)\bigr].$$

Model must approximate the true reverse conditional.

$q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0)$ is Gaussian with closed form. Comparing two Gaussians makes $L_t$ an MSE.
04

True Reverse Conditional

Closed form via completing the square

Setup: What We Want

Target: $q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0)$, the law of $x_{t-1}$ given $x_t$ and $x_0$.

By Bayes:

$$q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0) \propto \underbrace{q(x_t\mid x_{t-1})}_{\text{forward step}}\cdot\underbrace{q(x_{t-1}\mid x_0)}_{\text{closed form}}.$$

Both factors are Gaussian in $x_{t-1}$. Product is Gaussian.

Completing the Square (1 of 2)

Derivation

Plug in both Gaussians and collect the exponent:

$$\propto \exp\!\left(-\frac{(x_t-\sqrt{\alpha_t}x_{t-1})^2}{2\beta_t}-\frac{(x_{t-1}-\sqrt{\bar\alpha_{t-1}}x_0)^2}{2(1-\bar\alpha_{t-1})}\right).$$

Coefficient of $x_{t-1}^2$: $\dfrac{\alpha_t}{\beta_t}+\dfrac{1}{1-\bar\alpha_{t-1}} = \dfrac{1-\bar\alpha_t}{\beta_t(1-\bar\alpha_{t-1})}$.

Posterior variance:

$$\tilde\beta_t \coloneqq \dfrac{1-\bar\alpha_{t-1}}{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\beta_t.$$

Completing the Square (2 of 2)

Continuing

Collect linear coefficient; divide by $1/\tilde\beta_t$ for posterior mean:

$$\mu_q(x_t,x_0) = \frac{\sqrt{\bar\alpha_{t-1}}\,\beta_t}{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0 + \frac{\sqrt{\alpha_t}(1-\bar\alpha_{t-1})}{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,x_t.$$

Closed form:

$$q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0) = \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(\mu_q(x_t,x_0),\,\tilde\beta_t I\bigr). \;\blacksquare$$

$L_t$ Becomes MSE

Both $q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0)$ and $p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)$ are Gaussian with equal covariance $\tilde\beta_t I$:

$$D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(\mathcal{N}(\mu_q,\Sigma)\Vert\mathcal{N}(\mu_\theta,\Sigma)\bigr) = \tfrac{1}{2}(\mu_q-\mu_\theta)^\top\Sigma^{-1}(\mu_q-\mu_\theta).$$
Training objective: $L_t \propto \mathbb{E}_q\!\bigl[\,\|\mu_q(x_t,x_0)-\mu_\theta(x_t,t)\|^2\,\bigr]$.

Parameterizing $\mu_\theta$

Three equivalent choices:

  • Predict $x_0$: train against clean data
  • Predict noise $\varepsilon$: the DDPM default
  • Predict score $\nabla_x\log p_t(x)$: the SDE view
Lecture 3 derives all three views and the Tweedie links between them.

Recap of Lecture 2

  • Forward: $q(x_t\mid x_0)=\mathcal{N}(\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}x_0,(1-\bar\alpha_t)I)$
  • Reverse: learned Gaussian kernels $p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)$
  • Hierarchical VAE: fixed encoder, no posterior collapse
  • ELBO: $-\log p_\theta(x_0)\le L_0+L_T+\sum L_{t-1}$
  • True reverse: Gaussian, closed form via completing the square
  • $L_t$: MSE on reverse-mean prediction
