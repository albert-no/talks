Differential Entropy 3 — Companion Notes

MI — Why It "Just Works" Continuously

For any joint distribution $P_{XY}$ (continuous, discrete, or mixed), the MI

$$I(X;Y) = D(P_{XY}\|P_X\otimes P_Y)$$

is well-defined whenever $P_{XY}\ll P_X\otimes P_Y$. KL is invariant under measure-preserving maps and absolutely-continuous reparameterizations.

Operationally: given $n$ samples from $P_{XY}$ versus $P_X\otimes P_Y$, the optimal hypothesis-test exponent is exactly $I(X;Y)$ (Stein's lemma).

AWGN Capacity — Full Proof Outline

The single-letter calculation gives $\max_{p_X} I(X; X+Z) = \tfrac{1}{2}\log(1 + P/\sigma^2)$ when $\mathbb{E}[X^2]\le P$. To extend to a coding theorem requires:

  1. Random coding. Generate a codebook of $2^{nR}$ codewords i.i.d. from $\mathcal{N}(0, P)^n$. Decode by joint typicality.
  2. Power constraint handling. Truncate codewords with norm $> n(P+\delta)$; loss is $o(1)$.
  3. Achievability: for $R < C$, error probability $\to 0$ as $n\to\infty$.
  4. Converse: Fano + per-letter MI bound $I(X_i; Y_i)\le \tfrac{1}{2}\log(1 + P_i/\sigma^2)$, then concavity of $\log$.

Detailed proof in Cover & Thomas, Chapter 9.

Bandwidth-Limited Capacity — Continuous-Time Form

For a channel with bandwidth $W$ Hz, the Nyquist sampling theorem gives $2W$ orthogonal samples per second. Each sample is an AWGN channel with power $P/(2W)$ and noise variance $N_0/2 \cdot (2W) = N_0 W$ (after scaling).

Capacity per sample: $\tfrac{1}{2}\log(1 + (P/2W)/((N_0/2))) = \tfrac{1}{2}\log(1 + P/(N_0 W))$. Times $2W$ samples per second:

$$C = W\log\!\left(1 + \frac{P}{N_0 W}\right) \text{ bits/sec}.$$

$W\to\infty$ limit: $\lim W\log(1 + P/(N_0 W)) = P/N_0 \cdot \log e \approx 1.443\,P/N_0$ bits/sec. This is the Shannon limit on energy efficiency.

Water-Filling — KKT Derivation

Lagrangian for parallel-AWGN capacity:

$$\mathcal{L}(\{P_i\}, \nu) = \sum_i \tfrac{1}{2}\log\!\left(1 + \tfrac{P_i}{\sigma_i^2}\right) - \nu\!\left(\sum_i P_i - P\right).$$

Stationarity: $\frac{1}{2(P_i + \sigma_i^2)\ln 2} = \nu \Rightarrow P_i = (1/(2\nu\ln 2)) - \sigma_i^2$.

With non-negativity constraint $P_i \ge 0$, solution is $P_i = (\nu' - \sigma_i^2)^+$ where $\nu' = 1/(2\nu\ln 2)$. Sum-power constraint determines $\nu'$.

I-MMSE — Sketch of Proof

Differentiate $I(X; Y_t) = h(Y_t) - h(Y_t\mid X) = h(Y_t) - h(Z)$ in $t$ where $Y_t = \sqrt{t}X + Z$, $Z\sim\mathcal{N}(0,1)$. By de Bruijn,

$$\frac{d}{dt}h(Y_t) = \tfrac{1}{2}J(Y_t),$$

where $J$ is Fisher information. By the Cramér–Rao identity (or direct computation), $J(Y_t) = 1 - t\,\mathrm{mmse}(t)$ when $Z\sim\mathcal{N}(0,1)$. After change of variables to $\mathrm{snr} = t$ (well, $t = \mathrm{snr}$ in this scaling), one gets

$$\frac{dI}{d\,\mathrm{snr}} = \tfrac{1}{2}\,\mathrm{mmse}(\mathrm{snr}).$$

Detailed derivation in Guo, Shamai, Verdú (2005).

de Bruijn — Heat-Equation View

$Y_t = X + \sqrt{t}Z$ has density $f_{Y_t}(y) = (f_X * \varphi_t)(y)$ where $\varphi_t$ is the Gaussian pdf with variance $t$. Then $f_{Y_t}$ satisfies the heat equation

$$\partial_t f_{Y_t}(y) = \tfrac{1}{2}\partial_y^2 f_{Y_t}(y).$$

Differentiating $h(Y_t) = -\int f_{Y_t}\log f_{Y_t}$ in $t$ and integrating by parts:

$$\partial_t h(Y_t) = -\int (\partial_t f_{Y_t})(\log f_{Y_t} + 1) = \tfrac{1}{2}\int \frac{(\partial_y f_{Y_t})^2}{f_{Y_t}} = \tfrac{1}{2}J(Y_t).$$

This is the de Bruijn identity in its cleanest form.

Diffusion Models — Information-Theoretic Loss

For a diffusion model with forward $X^{(t)} = \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}X^{(0)} + \sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}Z$, the ELBO can be expressed as

$$-\log p(X^{(0)}) \le \sum_t \tfrac{1}{2}\,\mathrm{mmse}(\mathrm{snr}_t)\,d\mathrm{snr}_t + \text{const}$$

(Kingma et al., 2021). The integrand is exactly the I-MMSE derivative; the ELBO accumulates the loss in mutual information across noise levels.

This connects diffusion training to Bayesian estimation under additive-Gaussian noise — see privacy/diffusion/diffusion3-sde-score.html and infotheory/diffusion/diff3-parameterizations.html.
