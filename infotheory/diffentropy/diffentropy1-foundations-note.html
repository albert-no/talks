Differential Entropy 1 — Companion Notes

Discretization Bridge — Detail

Let $f$ be Riemann-integrable. By the mean-value theorem, for each bin $[i\Delta, (i+1)\Delta)$ there exists $x_i$ such that $\Pr(X^\Delta = i) = \Delta f(x_i)$. Then

$$H(X^\Delta) = -\sum_i \Delta f(x_i) \log(\Delta f(x_i)) = -\Delta\sum_i f(x_i)\log f(x_i) - \log\Delta\sum_i \Delta f(x_i).$$

The first sum approaches $\int f\log f\,dx = -h(X)$ as $\Delta\to 0$ (Riemann sum); the second sum approaches $\log(1/\Delta)$ since $\sum \Delta f(x_i) \to 1$. Hence $H(X^\Delta) = h(X) + \log(1/\Delta) + o(1)$.

This justifies the slogan "differential entropy is discrete entropy minus the resolution".

Why $h$ Can Be Negative — Reading the Bridge

Both $h(X)$ and $\log(1/\Delta)$ contribute to $H(X^\Delta)$. The discrete $H \ge 0$ always, but the split is not constrained — $h$ alone can be very negative as long as $\log(1/\Delta)$ is large enough to keep $H \ge 0$.

Example: $\mathrm{Unif}[0, 2^{-100}]$ has $h = -100$ bits. Discretizing to $\Delta = 2^{-200}$ gives $H(X^\Delta) = -100 + 200 = 100$ bits — perfectly reasonable: 100 bits of resolution about a tiny number.

Cauchy Distribution — Computation

$f(x) = \tfrac{1}{\pi}\tfrac{\gamma}{x^2+\gamma^2}$. Differential entropy:

$$h(X) = \int f(x)\log\bigl(\pi(x^2+\gamma^2)/\gamma\bigr)\,dx.$$

Substitute $u = x/\gamma$: $f = (1/(\pi\gamma)) \cdot 1/(1+u^2)$, $dx = \gamma\,du$. The integral becomes

$$h(X) = \log(\pi\gamma) + \frac{1}{\pi}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\frac{\log(1 + u^2)}{1 + u^2}\,du = \log(\pi\gamma) + \log 4 = \log(4\pi\gamma).$$

The integral $\int (\log(1+u^2))/(1+u^2)\,du = 2\pi\log 2$ is a standard result (residues, or differentiation under the integral).

Multivariate Gaussian Entropy — Derivation

For $\mathbf{X}\sim\mathcal{N}(\boldsymbol{\mu}, K)$, density

$$f(\mathbf{x}) = \frac{1}{(2\pi)^{d/2}\sqrt{\det K}}\exp\!\left(-\tfrac{1}{2}(\mathbf{x}-\boldsymbol{\mu})^\top K^{-1}(\mathbf{x}-\boldsymbol{\mu})\right).$$

Entropy:

$$\begin{aligned} h(\mathbf{X}) &= -\mathbb{E}[\log f(\mathbf{X})] \\ &= \tfrac{1}{2}\log((2\pi)^d\det K) + \tfrac{1}{2}\,\mathbb{E}[(\mathbf{X}-\boldsymbol{\mu})^\top K^{-1}(\mathbf{X}-\boldsymbol{\mu})]\log e \\ &= \tfrac{1}{2}\log((2\pi)^d\det K) + \tfrac{d}{2}\log e \\ &= \tfrac{1}{2}\log((2\pi e)^d \det K). \end{aligned}$$

Used $\mathbb{E}[(\mathbf{X}-\boldsymbol{\mu})^\top K^{-1}(\mathbf{X}-\boldsymbol{\mu})] = \mathrm{tr}(K^{-1} K) = d$.

Linear Transformation — General Form

For $Y = AX + b$ with invertible $A$, density transforms as $f_Y(y) = f_X(A^{-1}(y-b))/|\det A|$. Then

$$h(Y) = -\int f_Y(y)\log f_Y(y)\,dy = h(X) + \log|\det A|.$$

For singular $A$, $h(AX) = -\infty$ (the distribution lives on a lower-dimensional subspace, density is degenerate).

KL Continuous — Convexity, Pinsker, etc.

Every property from the discrete case carries over verbatim:

  • Gibbs: $D(f\|g)\ge 0$, equality iff $f=g$ a.e.
  • Joint convexity: $(f, g)\mapsto D(f\|g)$ jointly convex.
  • Pinsker: $D(f\|g)\ge 2(\ln 2)\|f-g\|_{TV}^2$, where $\|f-g\|_{TV} = \tfrac{1}{2}\int|f-g|$.
  • Data processing: $D(P_X\circ T\,\|\,Q_X\circ T)\le D(P_X\,\|\,Q_X)$ for any (measurable) $T$.

Continuous KL is also insensitive to reparameterization: change of variables on both $f$ and $g$ leaves $D$ unchanged.

Conditional Differential Entropy — Mixed Case

If $Y$ is discrete and $X$ is continuous (e.g., a label and a feature), $h(X\mid Y) = \sum_y p_Y(y) h(X\mid Y=y)$ is well-defined and behaves like the continuous version: chain rule, conditioning reduces entropy, etc.

Conversely, $H(Y\mid X) = -\int f_X(x)\sum_y p_{Y\mid X}(y\mid x)\log p_{Y\mid X}(y\mid x)\,dx$ stays bounded.

Mixed MI: $I(X; Y) = h(X) - h(X\mid Y) = H(Y) - H(Y\mid X)$ — both forms valid simultaneously.
