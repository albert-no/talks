01

Continuous Mutual Information

Why $I$ "just works" in the continuous domain

Recap — Definition

Definition.

For $(X, Y)$ with joint density $f_{X,Y}$,

$$I(X; Y) \;=\; D(f_{X,Y}\,\|\,f_X f_Y) \;=\; \mathbb{E}\!\left[\log\frac{f_{X,Y}(X,Y)}{f_X(X) f_Y(Y)}\right].$$

Equivalent forms:

$$I(X;Y) \;=\; h(X) - h(X\mid Y) \;=\; h(Y) - h(Y\mid X).$$

Discretization Bridge for MI

Bin both $X$ and $Y$ to width $\Delta$:

$$\begin{aligned} I(X^\Delta; Y^\Delta) &\;\approx\; \mathbb{E}\!\left[\log\frac{\Delta^2 f_{X,Y}(X,Y)}{\Delta f_X(X) \cdot \Delta f_Y(Y)}\right] \\ &\;=\; \mathbb{E}\!\left[\log\frac{f_{X,Y}}{f_X f_Y}\right] \;=\; I(X; Y). \end{aligned}$$

The $\log\Delta$ factors cancel. Hence $I(X^\Delta; Y^\Delta) \to I(X; Y)$ without a $\log(1/\Delta)$ divergence.

$I$ is a true continuous-domain quantity.

Properties — All Inherited

  • Non-negative: $I(X;Y)\ge 0$ from $D\ge 0$. Equality iff $X\perp Y$.
  • Symmetric: $I(X;Y) = I(Y;X)$.
  • Scale-invariant: $I(aX; bY) = I(X; Y)$.
  • Diffeo-invariant: $I(g(X); h(Y)) = I(X;Y)$ for invertible smooth $g, h$.
  • DPI: $X\to Y\to Z\Rightarrow I(X;Z)\le I(X;Y)$.
  • Chain rule: $I(X^n; Y) = \sum_i I(X_i; Y\mid X^{i-1})$.

Example — Bivariate Gaussian

$(X, Y)$ jointly Gaussian, correlation $\rho$. From Lecture 1:

$$I(X;Y) \;=\; -\tfrac{1}{2}\log(1 - \rho^2).$$

Example — Additive Gaussian Noise

$Y = X + Z$, $Z\sim\mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2)$ independent of $X$. Pose $\mathbb{E}[X^2] \le P$. Then:

$$h(Y\mid X) \;=\; h(X + Z\mid X) \;=\; h(Z) \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\log(2\pi e\sigma^2).$$

And $h(Y)$ is maximized when $Y$ is Gaussian, hence when $X$ is Gaussian:

$$h(Y) \;\le\; \tfrac{1}{2}\log(2\pi e (P + \sigma^2)).$$

Combining:

$$I(X;Y) \;\le\; \tfrac{1}{2}\log\!\left(1 + \tfrac{P}{\sigma^2}\right).$$
02

The AWGN Channel

$Y = X + Z$, the canonical continuous channel

Setup

Continuous-input, continuous-output channel:

$$Y_t \;=\; X_t + Z_t, \qquad Z_t \;\stackrel{\mathrm{iid}}{\sim}\; \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2).$$
  • $n$ uses of the channel: $Y^n = X^n + Z^n$.
  • Power constraint: $\tfrac{1}{n}\sum_i X_i^2 \le P$.
  • SNR $= P/\sigma^2$.

Goal: compute the maximum mutual information per channel use.

Theorem — Shannon–Hartley

Theorem (Shannon, 1948).

The capacity of the AWGN channel with input power $P$ and noise variance $\sigma^2$ is

$$C \;=\; \max_{p_X:\,\mathbb{E}[X^2]\le P} I(X; X + Z) \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\log\!\left(1 + \tfrac{P}{\sigma^2}\right) \quad\text{bits/channel use}.$$

Achieved by $X\sim\mathcal{N}(0, P)$.

Proof — Achievability (Single Letter)

Choose $X\sim\mathcal{N}(0, P)$. Then $Y = X + Z\sim\mathcal{N}(0, P + \sigma^2)$.

$$\begin{aligned} I(X; Y) &= h(Y) - h(Y\mid X) \\ &= \tfrac{1}{2}\log(2\pi e (P + \sigma^2)) - \tfrac{1}{2}\log(2\pi e\sigma^2) \\ &= \tfrac{1}{2}\log\!\left(1 + \tfrac{P}{\sigma^2}\right). \end{aligned}$$

This is the single-letter rate. The full coding theorem extends to block codes via random Gaussian codebooks — details in companion notes. $\square$

Proof — Converse (Single Letter)

For any input distribution with $\mathbb{E}[X^2]\le P$:

$$\begin{aligned} \mathrm{Var}(Y) &= \mathbb{E}[X^2] + \sigma^2 \;\le\; P + \sigma^2 \\ h(Y) &\;\le\; \tfrac{1}{2}\log(2\pi e (P + \sigma^2)) \quad\text{(Gaussian MaxEnt)} \\ h(Y\mid X) &= h(Z) \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\log(2\pi e \sigma^2) \\ I(X;Y) &\;\le\; \tfrac{1}{2}\log\!\left(1 + \tfrac{P}{\sigma^2}\right). \end{aligned}$$

Equality iff $Y$ is Gaussian iff $X$ is Gaussian. $\square$

At high SNR: $C \approx \tfrac{1}{2}\log(\mathrm{SNR})$ · each $6$ dB $\to$ $1$ extra bit.

At low SNR: $C \approx \tfrac{P}{2\sigma^2 \ln 2}$ · linear in SNR.

Bandwidth-Limited Form

For a continuous-time AWGN channel of bandwidth $W$ Hz and noise PSD $N_0/2$:

$$C \;=\; W\,\log_2\!\left(1 + \frac{P}{N_0 W}\right) \quad \text{bits/sec}.$$

Limits:

  • Power-limited ($W\to\infty$): $C\to (P/N_0)\log_2 e \approx 1.443\,P/N_0$ bits/sec.
  • Bandwidth-limited ($W$ small): linear in $W$.

Foundational result for cellular networks, satellite, optical fiber.

03

Parallel Channels

Power allocation across $d$ independent AWGN sub-channels

Setup — Parallel AWGN

$d$ independent sub-channels:

$$Y_i \;=\; X_i + Z_i, \quad Z_i\sim\mathcal{N}(0, \sigma_i^2), \quad i = 1, \dots, d.$$

Total power constraint: $\sum_i \mathbb{E}[X_i^2] \le P$.

Question: how to allocate $P_i = \mathbb{E}[X_i^2]$ to maximize $\sum_i I(X_i; Y_i)$?

Optimization Problem

By single-letter capacity, optimal $X_i\sim\mathcal{N}(0, P_i)$:

$$\max_{P_1, \dots, P_d \ge 0}\; \sum_{i=1}^d \tfrac{1}{2}\log\!\left(1 + \tfrac{P_i}{\sigma_i^2}\right) \quad\text{s.t. } \sum_i P_i \le P.$$

Concave in $P_i$ · subject to linear constraint · KKT gives the solution.

Theorem — Water-Filling

Theorem.

The optimal power allocation is

$$P_i^\star \;=\; \bigl(\nu - \sigma_i^2\bigr)^+,$$

where $\nu$ is chosen so $\sum_i P_i^\star = P$.

  • Sub-channels with $\sigma_i^2 < \nu$ get power $\nu - \sigma_i^2$.
  • Sub-channels with $\sigma_i^2 \ge \nu$ get zero power — "too noisy to use".

Water-Filling — Picture

Plot $\sigma_i^2$ as bars. Pour "water" to level $\nu$. The power on each sub-channel is the depth above the bar.

Sub-channels with bar $\ge\nu$: dry — no power.

$\nu$ is the "water level" set by the total budget $P$.

Example — Three Sub-Channels

$\sigma_1^2 = 1$, $\sigma_2^2 = 2$, $\sigma_3^2 = 5$. Total $P = 6$.

Try $\nu = 4$: $P_1 = 3, P_2 = 2, P_3 = 0$. Sum $= 5\ne 6$.

Try $\nu = 4.5$: $P_1 = 3.5, P_2 = 2.5, P_3 = 0$. Sum $= 6$. $\;\checkmark$

Capacity:

$$C \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\log(4.5) + \tfrac{1}{2}\log(2.25) + 0 \;\approx\; 1.085 + 0.585 \;=\; 1.670 \text{ bits/use}.$$

Application — Frequency-Selective Channels

OFDM: split bandwidth $W$ into $d$ narrow sub-bands. Each sub-band has its own SNR (frequency-selective fading). Apply water-filling.

Used in WiFi, LTE, 5G, DSL. Modern adaptive coding pushes near capacity per sub-band.

Same idea also appears in: SVD on a MIMO channel (parallel singular-value sub-channels), eigen-decomposition of correlated noise.

Connections

MI and estimation

MMSE

For $X\sim p_X$ and observation $Y$, the minimum mean-square error estimator is the conditional expectation:

$$\hat X(Y) \;=\; \mathbb{E}[X\mid Y], \qquad \mathrm{mmse}(X\mid Y) \;=\; \mathbb{E}\bigl[(X - \mathbb{E}[X\mid Y])^2\bigr].$$

For AWGN $Y = \sqrt{\mathrm{snr}}\,X + Z$, $Z\sim\mathcal{N}(0, 1)$:

$$\mathrm{mmse}(\mathrm{snr}) \;=\; \mathbb{E}\bigl[(X - \hat X(Y))^2\bigr].$$

Theorem — I-MMSE

Theorem (Guo, Shamai, Verdú, 2005).

For $Y = \sqrt{\mathrm{snr}}\,X + Z$ with $Z\sim\mathcal{N}(0,1)$ independent of $X$:

$$\frac{d}{d\,\mathrm{snr}}\;I(X; Y) \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\,\mathrm{mmse}(\mathrm{snr}).$$
Guo, Shamai, Verdú, "Mutual Information and Minimum Mean-Square Error in Gaussian Channels", IEEE Trans. Inf. Theory 2005.

Mutual information increases at rate equal to half the MMSE.

Sanity Check — Gaussian Input

$X\sim\mathcal{N}(0, 1)$, $Y = \sqrt{\mathrm{snr}}\, X + Z$.

  • $I(X; Y) = \tfrac{1}{2}\log(1 + \mathrm{snr})$.
  • $\frac{d}{d\,\mathrm{snr}} I = \frac{1}{2(1 + \mathrm{snr})\ln 2}\cdot \ln 2 = \frac{1}{2(1 + \mathrm{snr})}$ (in nats per snr).
  • $\mathrm{mmse}(\mathrm{snr}) = \frac{1}{1 + \mathrm{snr}}$ (linear MMSE = exact MMSE for Gaussian).
$$\tfrac{1}{2}\,\mathrm{mmse} \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2(1 + \mathrm{snr})} \;=\; \tfrac{d I}{d\,\mathrm{snr}}. \;\checkmark$$

Theorem — de Bruijn's Identity

Theorem.

Let $Y_t = X + \sqrt{t}\,Z$ with $Z\sim\mathcal{N}(0, 1)$ independent of $X$. Then

$$\frac{d}{dt}\,h(Y_t) \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\,J(Y_t),$$

where $J(Y) = \mathbb{E}[(\partial_y \log f_Y(Y))^2]$ is the Fisher information of $Y$ at $0$ shift.

Connects entropy, MMSE, and Fisher information — the core triad of information-theoretic estimation.

Application — Diffusion Models

Diffusion adds Gaussian noise: $X^{(t)} = \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\, X^{(0)} + \sqrt{1 - \bar\alpha_t}\, Z$.

Score function $s(x, t) = \nabla_x\log f_{X^{(t)}}(x)$ relates to MMSE via Tweedie:

$$\mathbb{E}[X^{(0)}\mid X^{(t)} = x] \;=\; \frac{x + (1 - \bar\alpha_t)\, s(x, t)}{\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}}.$$

I-MMSE / de Bruijn give the entropy and KL of the diffusion at any time.

See infotheory/diffusion/ and privacy/diffusion/ for the full story.

Recap — Continuous Information Theory

  • $h$ is relative. Use $D, I$ for invariant operational meaning.
  • Gaussian = MaxEnt for fixed variance · cornerstone inequality.
  • EPI: entropy power is super-additive under convolution.
  • AWGN: $C = \tfrac{1}{2}\log(1 + \mathrm{SNR})$ · achieved by Gaussian input.
  • Water-filling: optimal power allocation across parallel sub-channels.
  • I-MMSE / de Bruijn: derivative of entropy is MMSE (or Fisher information).

Where the Series Connects

  • Lossy compression: Gaussian R(D) = $\tfrac{1}{2}\log(\sigma^2/D)$ — uses Shannon LB and MaxEnt.
  • MI estimation: variational bounds (Donsker–Varadhan, MINE) — rely on the KL definition of MI.
  • Diffusion models: de Bruijn / I-MMSE compute the ELBO.
  • Privacy (DP): AWGN is the exact channel of Gaussian DP.
