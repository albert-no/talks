01

Master Reparameterization

One identity that drives every parameterization choice

The Core Identity

From Lecture 2:

$$x_t = \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0 + \sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\varepsilon, \qquad \varepsilon\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I).$$

Knowing any two of $(x_t,x_0,\varepsilon)$ recovers the third:

$$x_0 = \frac{x_t - \sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\varepsilon}{\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}}, \qquad \varepsilon = \frac{x_t - \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0}{\sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}}.$$
02

Three Equivalent Parameterizations

$x_0$, $\varepsilon$, score: pick your favorite

Parameterization 1: Predict $x_0$

Network $x_{0,\theta}(x_t,t)$ predicts clean data:

$$\mathcal{L}_{x_0} = \mathbb{E}_{(x_0,t,\varepsilon)}\!\bigl[\,\|x_0 - x_{0,\theta}(x_t,t)\|^2\,\bigr].$$

Optimal: $x_{0,\theta}^\star(x_t,t) = \mathbb{E}[X_0\mid X_t=x_t]$ (posterior mean).

Pro

Directly interpretable target.

Con

High-variance loss at large $t$ (noisy input, clean target).

Parameterization 2: Predict Noise $\varepsilon$

Network $\varepsilon_\theta(x_t,t)$ predicts the noise that produced $x_t$:

$$\mathcal{L}_\varepsilon = \mathbb{E}_{(x_0,t,\varepsilon)}\!\bigl[\,\|\varepsilon - \varepsilon_\theta(x_t,t)\|^2\,\bigr].$$

Equivalent to $\mathcal{L}_{x_0}$ up to a $t$-dependent rescaling. DDPM default (Ho et al. 2020).

Unit-variance target at every $t$: well-conditioned gradients across noise levels.
Ho, Jain, Abbeel, "Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models", NeurIPS 2020.

Parameterization 3: Predict the Score

Marginal density at level $t$: $p_t(x) = q(x_t=x)$. Score function:

$$\nabla_x\log p_t(x).$$

Train score network $s_\theta(x_t,t)\approx\nabla_{x_t}\log p_t(x_t)$.

Score determines the reverse-time SDE drift. Connection to $\varepsilon_\theta$ derived next via Tweedie.
Song, Ermon, "Generative Modeling by Estimating Gradients of the Data Distribution", NeurIPS 2019.
03

Tweedie's Formula

Posterior mean of clean data = rescaled score

Tweedie's Formula

Theorem (Robbins, 1956).

$X_t = \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}X_0 + \sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\varepsilon$. Then:

$$\mathbb{E}[X_0\mid X_t=x_t] = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}}\Bigl(x_t + (1-\bar\alpha_t)\,\nabla_{x_t}\log p_t(x_t)\Bigr).$$

Posterior mean of clean $X_0$ = noisy $x_t$ + score correction, rescaled.

Robbins, "An empirical Bayes approach to statistics", Berkeley Symp. 1956.

Tweedie Proof (1 of 2)

Proof

Marginal by mixing:

$$p_t(x_t) = \int p_0(x_0)\,\mathcal{N}(x_t;\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}x_0,(1-\bar\alpha_t)I)\,dx_0.$$

Differentiate $\log p_t$ in $x_t$. Gaussian score in $x_t$:

$$-\frac{x_t - \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0}{1-\bar\alpha_t}.$$

Tweedie Proof (2 of 2)

Proof (continued)

Exchange differentiation and integration:

$$\nabla_{x_t}\log p_t(x_t) = -\frac{x_t - \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,\mathbb{E}[X_0\mid x_t]}{1-\bar\alpha_t}.$$

Solve for $\mathbb{E}[X_0\mid x_t]$: recover the stated formula. $\;\blacksquare$

Three-Way Conversion

Combine Tweedie and the master reparameterization:

$$\mathbb{E}[\varepsilon\mid x_t] = \frac{x_t - \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,\mathbb{E}[X_0\mid x_t]}{\sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}} = -\sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\nabla_{x_t}\log p_t(x_t).$$
Optimal noise predictor = score, rescaled: three names for one object.

Pick the parameterization that conditions the loss best at your noise level.

Information-Theoretic View

Noisy channels, approximate inversion, successive refinement

Forward Chain as Noisy Channels

Each step $X_{t-1}\to X_t$ is an additive Gaussian channel:

$$X_t = \sqrt{\alpha_t}\,X_{t-1} + \sqrt{\beta_t}\,\eta_t, \qquad \eta_t\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I).$$

Data-processing inequality: mutual information is non-increasing:

$$I(X_0;X_T) \le \cdots \le I(X_0;X_1) \le I(X_0;X_0).$$

As $\bar\alpha_T\to 0$: $I(X_0;X_T)\to 0$. Noise erases all source information.

Reverse Process as Channel Inversion

$p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)$ approximates $p(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)$: the optimal channel inverter.

Perfect inversion would recover $X_0$ exactly.

Each step accumulates a per-letter KL gap: total error = ELBO slack over $T$ steps.

Diffusion training is approximate universal channel decoding at $T$ noise levels.

Successive Refinement Analogy

Rate-distortion successive refinement: describe a source at decreasing distortion via nested codebooks.

Diffusion matches this structure:

  • $X_T$: coarsest description (pure noise)
  • $X_{T-1},\ldots,X_1$: intermediate refinements
  • $X_0$: finest reconstruction

Reverse process learns refinements jointly via shared parameters across $t$.
