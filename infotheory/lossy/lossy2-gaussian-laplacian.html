01

Gaussian $R(D)$

$\tfrac12\log(\sigma^2/D)$, achievability and converse

Statement

$X\sim\mathcal{N}(0,\sigma^2)$, squared error.

$$R(D) \;=\; \begin{cases}\tfrac12\log\dfrac{\sigma^2}{D}, & 0<D\le\sigma^2,\\[2mm] 0, & D\ge\sigma^2.\end{cases}$$

Below the variance: bits per coordinate scale logarithmically with the SNR $\sigma^2/D$.

Achievability — Backward Gaussian Channel

Test channel for $0<D<\sigma^2$, independent components:

$$\widehat X\sim\mathcal{N}(0,\sigma^2-D),\qquad Z\sim\mathcal{N}(0,D),\qquad X = \widehat X + Z.$$

Marginal: $X\sim\mathcal{N}(0,\sigma^2)$.

Distortion: $\mathbb{E}[(X-\widehat X)^2] = D$.

Mutual information via $Z\perp\widehat X$, so $h(X\mid\widehat X) = h(Z)$:

$$I(X;\widehat X) \;=\; h(X) - h(Z) \;=\; \tfrac12\log\dfrac{\sigma^2}{D}.$$

Achievability — Shell-Cover Picture

Outer shell: source $X^n$, radius $\sqrt{n\sigma^2}$.

Inner shell: codebook $\widehat X^n(m) \sim \mathcal{N}(0,\sigma^2{-}D)$,

radius $\sqrt{n(\sigma^2{-}D)}$.

Cover: some $m$ within $\sqrt{nD}$ of $X^n$.

$$X^n \approx \widehat X^n(m) + Z^n,\; Z^n\sim\mathcal{N}(0, D\,I).$$

Backward test channel realized geometrically.

$\sqrt{n\sigma^2}$ $\sqrt{n(\sigma^2 {-} D)}$ $X^n$ $\sqrt{nD}$ codebook $\widehat X^n(m)$

Forward Form — Linear Shrinkage

Same joint $P_{X,\widehat X}$, now conditioned on $X$:

$$\widehat X\mid X = x \;\sim\; \mathcal{N}\!\left(\dfrac{\sigma^2 - D}{\sigma^2}\,x,\;\dfrac{D(\sigma^2 - D)}{\sigma^2}\right).$$

Shrinkage factor: $(\sigma^2 - D)/\sigma^2 < 1$.

Reconstruction: shrunken copy of the source.

Energy gap: lossy code cannot keep all source energy.

Converse — Gaussian Maximizes Entropy

Let $E^n = X^n - \widehat X^n$. Standard chain:

$$nR \;\ge\; I(X^n;\widehat X^n) \;=\; h(X^n) - h(X^n\mid\widehat X^n) \;\ge\; h(X^n) - h(E^n).$$

Among $\mathbb{R}^n$ vectors with $\mathbb{E}\|E^n\|^2\le nD$, the isotropic Gaussian $\mathcal{N}(0,DI)$ maximizes $h$:

$$h(E^n) \le \tfrac{n}{2}\log(2\pi e D), \quad h(X^n) = \tfrac{n}{2}\log(2\pi e\sigma^2) \;\Longrightarrow\; R \ge \tfrac12\log\dfrac{\sigma^2}{D}.$$

Converse — Sphere Cover

Typical shell: radius $\sqrt{n\sigma^2}$.

Each codeword: ball of radius $\sqrt{nD}$.

Cover the shell with $M$ balls:

$$\begin{aligned} M &\;\gtrsim\; \!\left(\dfrac{\sigma^2}{D}\right)^{n/2}, \\[1mm] R = \tfrac{1}{n}\log M &\;\ge\; \tfrac12\log\dfrac{\sigma^2}{D}. \end{aligned}$$

Bits per coordinate, not per block.

$\sqrt{n\sigma^2}$ balls of radius $\sqrt{nD}$
02

Shannon Lower Bound

Gaussian is the hardest source at fixed variance

Statement (Squared Error)

For real $X$ with differential entropy $h(X)$:

$$R_X(D) \;\ge\; h(X) - \tfrac{1}{2}\log(2\pi e D).$$

Vector $X\in\mathbb{R}^d$ at per-coordinate distortion $D$:

$$R_X(D) \;\ge\; h(X) - \tfrac{d}{2}\log(2\pi e D).$$

Same flavor as the Gaussian converse, applied to any source.

Proof — Maximize Entropy of the Error

Error vector $E = X - \widehat X$, second-moment budget $\mathbb{E}\|E\|^2 = \sum_i \mathbb{E}(X_i - \widehat X_i)^2 \le d\,D$.

Mutual-information chain.

$$I(X;\widehat X) \;=\; h(X) - h(E\mid\widehat X) \;\ge\; h(X) - h(E).$$

Max-entropy bound. Gaussian wins at fixed variance.

$$h(E) \;\le\; \tfrac{d}{2}\log(2\pi e D) \;\Longrightarrow\; R \ge h(X) - \tfrac{d}{2}\log(2\pi e D).$$

Vector case: AM–GM on covariance eigenvalues.

Gaussian Hits the Bound — Hardest Source

Plug in $X\sim\mathcal{N}(0,\sigma^2)$: $h(X) = \tfrac12\log(2\pi e\sigma^2)$.

$$R_X(D) \;\ge\; \tfrac12\log(2\pi e\sigma^2) - \tfrac12\log(2\pi e D) \;=\; \tfrac12\log\dfrac{\sigma^2}{D}.$$

SLB tight at the Gaussian.

Non-Gaussian sources: extra structure, lower rate.

Code designed for Gaussian: universal MSE compressor at fixed variance.
03

Laplacian $R(D)$

Atom at zero in the optimal reproduction

Setup and Statement

$X\sim\mathrm{Lap}(\lambda)$, $f_X(x)=\tfrac\lambda 2 e^{-\lambda|x|}$.

$\mathbb{E}|X|=1/\lambda$. Distortion $|x-\hat x|$.

$$R(D) = \begin{cases}-\log(\lambda D), & 0<D\le 1/\lambda,\\ 0, & D\ge 1/\lambda.\end{cases}$$

Pretrained NN weights: approximately Laplacian.

Optimal reproduction $P^*_{\widehat X}$

$\lambda^2 D^2$ $\mathrm{Lap}(\lambda)$ tail $\hat x = 0$

Atom at $0$ + Laplacian tail.

Converse — Auxiliary Density Trick

Auxiliary $q_{X\mid\widehat X}(x\mid\hat x) = \tfrac{1}{2D}\exp(-|x-\hat x|/D)$, Laplacian centered at $\hat x$.

$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p_{X\mid\widehat X}\,\Vert\,q_{X\mid\widehat X})\ge 0$ gives

$$I(X;\widehat X) \;\ge\; \mathbb{E}\log\dfrac{q_{X\mid\widehat X}(X\mid\widehat X)}{p_X(X)} \;=\; -\log(\lambda D) - \tfrac{\mathbb{E}|X-\widehat X|}{D} + \lambda\,\mathbb{E}|X|.$$

$\mathbb{E}|X|=1/\lambda$ and $\mathbb{E}|X-\widehat X|\le D$: last two terms $\ge 0$.

Conclusion: $R(D)\ge -\log(\lambda D)$.

Achievability — Sparse Mixture (Backward Channel)

Backward Laplacian channel: independent

$$N\sim\mathrm{Lap}(1/D), \qquad V \;=\; \lambda^2 D^2\,\delta_0 \;+\; (1-\lambda^2 D^2)\,\mathrm{Lap}(\lambda),\qquad X = V + N.$$

Atom at zero in $V$ with mass $\lambda^2 D^2$. Set $\widehat X = V$.

Distortion: $\mathbb{E}|X-\widehat X| = \mathbb{E}|N| = D$.

Mutual information: $I(X;\widehat X) = -\log(\lambda D)$.

Optimal Laplacian reproduction has a Dirac atom at zero.
Isik, No, Weissman, "An Information-Theoretic Justification for Model Pruning", AISTATS 2022 (arXiv:2102.08329).
04

Pruning From Rate–Distortion

Why optimal NN compression is naturally sparse

Network and Compressed Weights

$d$-layer net with 1-Lipschitz $\sigma$, $\sigma(0)=0$:

$$f(x;w) \;=\; W^{(d)}\sigma\!\bigl(W^{(d-1)}\sigma(\cdots\sigma(W^{(1)}x))\bigr).$$

Compress $W^{(\ell)}\to\widehat W^{(\ell)}$ with $\|\widehat W^{(\ell)}\|_1\le\|W^{(\ell)}\|_1$.

Question: how does weight distortion bound output distortion?

Weight Distortion Bounds Output Distortion

Theorem (layer-wise telescoping).

$$\sup_{\|x\|_1\le 1}\!\|f(x;w)-f(x;\widehat w)\|_1 \;\le\; \!\Bigl(\prod_k\|W^{(k)}\|_1\Bigr)\sum_{\ell}\dfrac{\|W^{(\ell)}-\widehat W^{(\ell)}\|_1}{\|W^{(\ell)}\|_1}.$$

Per layer, this reduces to a normalized $\ell_1$ distortion: the Laplacian $R(D)$ problem from Part 03.

Trained Weights Are Laplacian-Like

Empirical histogram of pretrained weights:

  • Peaked at zero, heavy two-sided tails.
  • Matches $\tfrac{\lambda}{2}e^{-\lambda|w|}$ within layer.
  • Few large weights, many near-zero.

Source model for compression: $W \sim \mathrm{Lap}(\lambda)$ per layer (after normalization).

Heavy-tailed, sparse-friendly — not Gaussian.
Isik, No, Weissman, "An Information-Theoretic Justification for Model Pruning", AISTATS 2022 (arXiv:2102.08329).

Optimal Laplacian Code Is a Sparsifier

Optimal reproduction $P^*_{\widehat W}$ at rate $R = -\log(\lambda D)$:

$$P^*_{\widehat W} \;=\; \underbrace{\lambda^2 D^2}_{\text{atom mass}}\;\delta_0 \;+\; (1-\lambda^2 D^2)\,\mathrm{Lap}(\lambda).$$

Most reproductions land exactly at zero.

An information-theoretically optimal compressor of $\mathrm{Lap}(\lambda)$ weights is a sparsifier — pruning emerges, not imposed.

Isik, No, Weissman, "An Information-Theoretic Justification for Model Pruning", AISTATS 2022 (arXiv:2102.08329).

Pruning Is Optimal

Three pieces from this lecture:

  • Output distortion $\le$ normalized $\ell_1$ weight distortion.
  • Weights $\approx$ Laplacian.
  • Optimal Laplacian reproduction has a Dirac atom at zero.
Pruning is not a heuristic: it is part of the rate–distortion optimum for Laplacian-like weights.
05

Extreme-Value Theory and CROM

Rateless lossy compression by communicating extremes

Extremes Are Almost Predictable

Exponential maxima. $Y_1,\ldots,Y_n\stackrel{\mathrm{iid}}{\sim}\mathrm{Exp}(\lambda)$:

$$M_n \;=\; \max_i Y_i \;=\; \tfrac{\log n}{\lambda} + O_p(1) \quad\text{(Gumbel limit).}$$

Gaussian rule of thumb.

$$\max_i X_i \;\approx\; \sigma\sqrt{2\log n} + O_p(1).$$
Value: predictable from $n$ alone. Index: the only thing to send.

CROM: Send the Index, Reconstruct the Spike

Source: $X^n\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I_n)$.

Encoder: $m=\arg\max_i X_i$. Rate $\log n / n \to 0$.

Decoder reconstruction:

$$\widehat X_i \;=\; \begin{cases}\alpha_n, & i = m,\\ -\alpha_n/(n-1), & i\ne m,\end{cases}\qquad \alpha_n\approx\sqrt{2\log n}.$$

Distortion slope matches Gaussian $R(D)$ at origin: $D_G'(0) = -2\sigma^2$ vs CROM $-2$.

Random Rotations Gaussianize

For Haar-random orthogonal $A\in\mathrm{O}(d)$ and any deterministic $v\in\mathbb{R}^d$:

$$Av \;\sim\; \mathrm{Unif}\bigl(\|v\|\cdot S^{d-1}\bigr).$$

Each coordinate, $d\to\infty$:

$$(Av)_j \;\xrightarrow{d}\; \mathcal{N}\!\left(0,\;\tfrac{\|v\|^2}{d}\right).$$

Sparse, peaked, heavy-tailed — any source becomes Gaussian per coordinate after one rotation.

Incoherence step: makes per-coordinate / extremes-based encoding effective.

Iterate with Random Rotations

After one sparse subtraction, the residual is correlated and non-Gaussian. Apply a fresh Haar rotation each round to restore Gaussian marginals.

  1. Find indices $m^{(t)}$ of the $k_n$ largest residual coordinates.
  2. Encode the index set ($\log\binom{n}{k_n}$ nats).
  3. Subtract sparse $\alpha_t U^{(t)}$ on $m^{(t)}$.
  4. Rotate: $X^{(t+1)} = A_{t+1}\bigl(X^{(t)} - \alpha_t U^{(t)}\bigr)$.
Rateless: decoder can stop after any prefix; every prefix lies near $D_G(R)=\sigma^2 e^{-2R}$.

Recap

  • Gaussian: $R(D) = \tfrac12\log(\sigma^2/D)$ via backward channel and entropy converse.
  • SLB: $R_X(D)\ge h(X) - \tfrac{d}{2}\log(2\pi e D)$ for any source.
  • Gaussian = worst case at fixed variance.
  • Laplacian: $R(D) = -\log(\lambda D)$. Optimal reproduction has an atom at zero.
  • Pruning: Laplacian-like weights + $\ell_1$ distortion $\Rightarrow$ sparsity is information-theoretically optimal.
  • CROM: sparse-extreme communication after rotation matches the Gaussian D–R curve.
