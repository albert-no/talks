What Is the KV Cache?

Transformer attention $\Rightarrow$ each layer stores past Keys and Values per token.

At decode, every new query attends to all past $K, V$. Reuse, don't recompute.

Tokens $1{:}t$ $\to$ Cache $\{K_s, V_s\}_{s\le t}$ $\to$ Decode $t{+}1$

Memory grows linearly in sequence length, per layer, per head.

Why Compress the KV Cache?

Long context $32\text{K}, 128\text{K}, 1\text{M}$ tokens $\Rightarrow$ KV dominates GPU memory.

Cheap, online, data-oblivious compression buys context length.

  • Online: quantize each $K, V$ as it arrives.
  • Data-oblivious: no per-model calibration.
  • Lossy: exact recovery unnecessary — attention only needs inner products.
Lossy quantization is the natural lever. TURBOQUANT is the principled answer.
01

Setup and the Rotation Idea

Rotation alone produces a known coordinate distribution

Why Scalar Quantization Fails

  • Round each coordinate independently.
  • Implicit assumption: energy spread evenly.
  • Reality (KV, embeddings): a few outliers dominate.
  • Low-bit SQ burns resolution on outliers.
Random rotation flattens energy — every coordinate $\sim 1/\sqrt{d}$, same law.

Random Rotation Creates a Known Law

Haar-uniform $\Pi \in \mathbb{R}^{d\times d}$. Any fixed $x \in S^{d-1}$:

$$Y \;=\; \Pi x \;\sim\; \mathrm{Unif}(S^{d-1}), \qquad \sqrt{d}\,Y_j \;\Rightarrow\; \mathcal{N}(0, 1).$$

Why Gaussian-like. $\Pi$ Haar $\Rightarrow$ $\Pi x$ uniform on the sphere.

Marginal of one coordinate $=$ classical sphere–Gaussian duality (Poincaré's lemma).

Law depends only on $\|x\| = 1$. Algorithm is data-oblivious.

Lemma — Coordinate of a Sphere Point

Lemma 1.

$Y \sim \mathrm{Unif}(S^{d-1})$. Marginal density of each $Y_j$:

$$f_d(t) \;=\; \dfrac{\Gamma(d/2)}{\sqrt{\pi}\,\Gamma((d-1)/2)}\,(1 - t^2)^{(d-3)/2}, \quad t \in [-1, 1].$$

Large $d$: $\;\sqrt{d}\,Y_j \;\Rightarrow\; \mathcal{N}(0, 1)$.

(Slice argument; Gaussian limit derived in the note file.)

Two Distortions

MSE distortion

$$D_{\mathrm{mse}} = \mathbb{E}\|x - \widetilde x\|_2^2.$$

Reconstruction quality. Target $\sim 4^{-b}$.

Inner-product distortion

$$D_{\mathrm{prod}} = \mathbb{E}|\langle y, x\rangle - \langle y, \widetilde x\rangle|^2.$$

Attention, NN search. Want unbiased.

Where $4^{-b}$ comes from. Gaussian R(D): $D(R) = \sigma^2 \cdot 2^{-2R}$.

At $R = b$ bits/sample: $\;D \propto 4^{-b}$. Each rotated coordinate $\sim \mathcal{N}(0, 1/d) \Rightarrow$ Gaussian rate is the right benchmark.

Bias warning. $1$-bit reconstruction has magnitude $\sigma \sqrt{2/\pi} < \sigma$ — shrinks projections. Fixed in Part 5.

02

MSE TURBOQUANT

Algorithm, anchor, theorem via Panter–Dite

Algorithm: TURBOQUANT$_{\mathrm{mse}}$

Public state: rotation $\Pi$, scalar codebook $\{c_1, \ldots, c_K\}$ for $f_d$, $K = 2^b$.

TURBOQUANT$_{\mathrm{mse}}$
  1. Encode$(x)$: rotate $y = \Pi \cdot x$.
  2. For each $j$: $\mathrm{idx}[j] = \arg\min_i |y_j - c_i|$.
  3. Return $\mathrm{idx}$   $\bigl(d \cdot b$ bits$\bigr)$.
  4. Decode$(\mathrm{idx})$: $\widetilde y_j = c_{\mathrm{idx}[j]}$, then $\widetilde x = \Pi^\top \widetilde y$.

Codebook offline via Lloyd–Max on $f_d$. No data-dependent training.

One-Bit Anchor: $1 - 2/\pi$

For $Y \sim \mathcal{N}(0, 1/d)$ and sign quantizer $\widehat Y = a \cdot \operatorname{sign}(Y)$:

$$\mathbb{E}[(Y - a \cdot \operatorname{sign} Y)^2] = \mathbb{E} Y^2 - 2 a \cdot \mathbb{E}|Y| + a^2, \qquad a^\star = \mathbb{E}|Y| = \sqrt{\tfrac{2}{\pi d}}.$$

At the optimum: $\mathbb{E}[(Y - a^\star \cdot \operatorname{sign} Y)^2] = \tfrac{1}{d}(1 - \tfrac{2}{\pi})$.

$$\text{total MSE at } b = 1 \;=\; 1 - \tfrac{2}{\pi} \;\approx\; 0.363.$$

Theorem and Proof Outline

Theorem (TURBOQUANT$_{\mathrm{mse}}$).

Every unit $x \in S^{d-1}$:

$$\mathbb{E}\|x - \widetilde x\|^2 \;\le\; \tfrac{\sqrt{3}\,\pi}{2} \cdot 4^{-b} \;\approx\; 2.72 \cdot 4^{-b}.$$

Step 1. Orthogonal invariance: $\|x - \widetilde x\|^2 = \|Y - \widetilde Y\|^2 = d \cdot C(f_d, b)$.

Step 2. Panter–Dite, evaluated on $f_d$:

$$\begin{aligned} C(f, b) &\;\le\; \tfrac{1}{12}\Bigl(\!\int f^{1/3}\Bigr)^{\!3} \cdot 4^{-b}, \\ \Bigl(\!\int f_d^{1/3}\Bigr)^{\!3} &\;=\; \tfrac{6\sqrt{3}\,\pi}{d} \;\;\Rightarrow\;\; \text{constant } \tfrac{\sqrt{3}\,\pi}{2}. \end{aligned}$$
03

Lower Bound

Sphere SLB + Yao $\;\Rightarrow\;$ exponent $4^{-b}$ unbeatable

Sphere SLB

Why this slide. Upper bound says we can hit $4^{-b}$.

We need a matching lower bound to know no algorithm beats it — SLB on the sphere distribution gives that benchmark.

$$D \;\ge\; \dfrac{d}{2\pi e} \cdot 2^{2(h(X) - B)/d}.$$

For $X \sim \mathrm{Unif}(S^{d-1})$: $h(X) = \log_2 A_d$, Stirling $\Rightarrow A_d^{2/d} \to 2\pi e / d$.

So $D(B) \ge 2^{-2B/d}$. At $B = b d$: $\;D \ge 4^{-b}$ on the sphere.

Yao + Constant Gap

Yao's principle. Worst-input randomized cost $\ge$ best deterministic cost on hardest input distribution.

Apply with $\mathrm{Unif}(S^{d-1})$: some $x$ forces $\mathbb{E}\|x - \widehat x\|^2 \ge 4^{-b}$ for every $bd$-bit quantizer.

$$4^{-b} \;\le\; D_{\mathrm{mse}}^\star(b) \;\le\; \tfrac{\sqrt{3}\,\pi}{2} \cdot 4^{-b}.$$
Constant gap $\Rightarrow$ exponent $4^{-b}$ is optimal.
04

Inner-Product Bias

Good MSE doesn't imply good linear-functional preservation

One-Bit MSE Shrinks Projections

Sign quantizer $\widetilde Y_j = a \cdot \operatorname{sign}(Y_j)$, $a = \sqrt{2/(\pi d)}$.

Rotational symmetry $\Rightarrow \mathbb{E}[\widetilde x \mid x] = \alpha \cdot x$.

$$\alpha \;=\; \mathbb{E}\langle x, \widetilde x\rangle \;=\; d \cdot a \cdot \mathbb{E}|Y_1| \;=\; d \cdot \dfrac{2}{\pi d} \;=\; \dfrac{2}{\pi}.$$

Hence $\mathbb{E}\langle y, \widetilde x\rangle = \tfrac{2}{\pi} \cdot \langle y, x\rangle$ — biased low.

Geometric Reason — Shrunken Norm

One-bit reconstruction has constant magnitude per rotated coordinate. Squared norm:

$$\|\widetilde Y\|^2 \;=\; d \cdot a^2 \;=\; \dfrac{2}{\pi}.$$

So $\|\widetilde x\| = \sqrt{2/\pi} < 1$.

Good representative of its sign cell, but every projection multiplicatively shrunk.

Need an unbiased estimator — enter QJL.
05

QJL and Inner-Product TURBOQUANT

One-bit unbiased sketch, applied to the residual

QJL Definition

Let $S \in \mathbb{R}^{d\times d}$ have i.i.d. $\mathcal{N}(0, 1)$ entries. For $u \in S^{d-1}$:

$$Q_{\mathrm{qjl}}(u) \;=\; \operatorname{sign}(S u) \;\in\; \{-1, +1\}^d, \qquad Q_{\mathrm{qjl}}^{-1}(z) \;=\; \dfrac{\sqrt{\pi/2}}{d} \cdot S^\top z.$$

One bit per coordinate. Unbiased estimator of $u$.

Lemma — Unbiased with $1/d$ Variance

Lemma (QJL).

$$\mathbb{E}_S\langle y,Q_{\mathrm{qjl}}^{-1}(Q_{\mathrm{qjl}}(u))\rangle = \langle y,u\rangle, \qquad \operatorname{Var}_S(\cdot)\;\le\;\dfrac{\pi}{2d}\|y\|^2.$$

Unbiasedness. Gaussian identity $\mathbb{E}\bigl[s\operatorname{sign}(s^\top u)\bigr] = \sqrt{2/\pi}\,u$ for $s\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I)$.

Variance. Each summand $\le\tfrac{\pi}{2}\|y\|^2$ in second moment; $d$-fold averaging gives $\tfrac{\pi}{2d}\|y\|^2$.

Two-Stage Decomposition

Split $x = \widetilde x_{\mathrm{mse}} + r$, with $\widetilde x_{\mathrm{mse}}$ MSE-quantized:

$$\langle y, x\rangle \;=\; \langle y, \widetilde x_{\mathrm{mse}}\rangle \;+\; \langle y, r\rangle.$$

First term. Read from the MSE-quantized vector.

Second term. QJL on residual $r$.

QJL variance scales with $\|r\|^2$. MSE stage shrinks $\|r\|$ — QJL accurate when MSE works.

Algorithm and Theorem

TURBOQUANT$_{\mathrm{prod}}$
  1. Encode$(x, b)$: $\mathrm{idx} = \mathrm{TQ}_{\mathrm{mse}}(x, b{-}1)$   $\bigl((b{-}1) \cdot d$ bits$\bigr)$.
  2. $r = x - \mathrm{TQ}_{\mathrm{mse}}^{-1}(\mathrm{idx})$,   $\gamma = \|r\|$.
  3. $q = \operatorname{sign}(S \cdot r) \in \{-1, +1\}^d$   $\bigl(d$ bits$\bigr)$. Return $(\mathrm{idx}, q, \gamma)$.
  4. Decode: $\widetilde x = \mathrm{TQ}_{\mathrm{mse}}^{-1}(\mathrm{idx}) + \gamma \cdot \tfrac{\sqrt{\pi/2}}{d} \cdot S^\top q$.

Theorem (TURBOQUANT$_{\mathrm{prod}}$).

$$\mathbb{E}\langle y, \widetilde x\rangle = \langle y, x\rangle, \qquad D_{\mathrm{prod}} \;\le\; \dfrac{\sqrt{3}\,\pi^2\,\|y\|^2}{d} \cdot 4^{-b}.$$
06

KV Cache Quantization

Online, data-oblivious, attention-preserving

Why TURBOQUANT Fits the KV Cache

Decoder-only transformer: each new token uses inner products $\langle q_t, k_s\rangle$ between query and past keys.

Attention preservation $\Leftrightarrow$ inner-product preservation.

  • Online: each new key quantized as it arrives.
  • No calibration: codebook universal, not dataset-specific.
  • Inner-product control: unbiased, $\mathcal{O}(1/d)$ variance.
Quality-neutral around $3.5$ bits/channel; gradual degradation near $2.5$.
Daliri et al., "TurboQuant: Online Vector Quantization with Optimal Distortion", arXiv:2504.19874 (2025).

Recap

  • Rotation: turns any unit vector into a sphere point with known coordinate density $f_d$.
  • MSE TURBOQUANT: Lloyd–Max on $f_d$ achieves $D_{\mathrm{mse}}\le\tfrac{\sqrt 3\pi}{2}\cdot 4^{-b}$.
  • Lower bound: sphere SLB + Yao $\Rightarrow$ exponent $4^{-b}$ optimal.
  • Bias: MSE shrinks projections by $2/\pi$ at one bit.
  • QJL: one-bit unbiased linear-functional estimator with $\mathcal{O}(1/d)$ variance.
  • IP TURBOQUANT: two-stage; unbiased, $D_{\mathrm{prod}} \le \tfrac{\sqrt 3\pi^2\|y\|^2}{d}\cdot 4^{-b}$.
