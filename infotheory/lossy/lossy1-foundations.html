01

Problem Setup

Trade bits for fidelity on real-valued sources

Lossless Is Not Enough

Discrete source: $H(X)$ bits per symbol suffices.

Continuous source: $H(X) = \infty$. Perfect reconstruction needs infinitely many bits.

Lossy compression: trade rate against fidelity, measured by a distortion $d(x,\hat x)\ge 0$.

Central question: how few bits per sample at distortion $\le D$?

Three Distortion Functions

Squared error

$$d(x,\hat x)=(x-\hat x)^2$$

Default for continuous sources.

Hamming

$$d_H(x,\hat x)=\mathbb{1}\{x\ne\hat x\}$$

Default for discrete sources.

Absolute error

$$d_1(x,\hat x)=|x-\hat x|$$

Sparse, robust.

Block Code and Per-Letter Distortion

An $(n,R)$ lossy code:

$$f:\mathcal{X}^n\to\{1,\ldots,2^{nR}\},\qquad g:\{1,\ldots,2^{nR}\}\to\widehat{\mathcal{X}}^n.$$

Reconstruction $\widehat X^n = g(f(X^n))$. Per-letter average distortion:

$$d^{(n)}(x^n,\hat x^n) \;=\; \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n d(x_i,\hat x_i).$$

Achievable Region and $R(D)$

$(R,D)$ achievable: for every $\varepsilon>0$, $\exists$ $(n,R+\varepsilon)$ code with $\mathbb{E}\,d^{(n)}\le D+\varepsilon$ for large $n$.

$$R(D) \;=\; \inf\{R : (R,D)\text{ achievable}\}.$$

Non-increasing, convex, continuous on $(D_{\min}, D_{\max})$.

02

Scalar Quantization

$R\in\{0,1,2\}$ on a Gaussian, Lloyd–Max convergence

Toy: $X\sim\mathcal{N}(\mu,\sigma^2)$

Squared error. Send $R$ bits per symbol; pick partition + reconstruction levels.

Three cases: $R=0$ (no bits), $R=1$ (one threshold), $R=2$ (Lloyd–Max).

$R=0$: send nothing. Decoder outputs a constant $c$. Optimal $c$?

$R=0$: $c^\star = \mu$, $D(0)=\sigma^2$

Add and subtract $\mu$:

$$\mathbb{E}[(X-c)^2] \;=\; \sigma^2 + \underbrace{2(\mu-c)\,\mathbb{E}[X-\mu]}_{=\,0} + (\mu-c)^2.$$

So $\mathbb{E}[(X-c)^2] = \sigma^2 + (\mu-c)^2 \ge \sigma^2$, with equality iff $c=\mu$.

Same argument generalizes: conditional mean minimizes squared error inside any cell.

$R=1$: One Threshold, Two Cells

Symmetry: threshold at $\mu$. Reps are conditional means of the half-lines.

Standardize $X = \mu + \sigma Z$, $Z\sim\mathcal{N}(0,1)$. Half-Gaussian mean:

$$\mathbb{E}[Z\mid Z>0] \;=\; \dfrac{1/\sqrt{2\pi}}{1/2} \;=\; \sqrt{\tfrac{2}{\pi}}.$$

By symmetry $\mathbb{E}[Z^2\mid Z>0]=1$, so the cell variance is $1 - 2/\pi$:

$$D(1) \;=\; \sigma^2\bigl(1 - \tfrac{2}{\pi}\bigr) \;\approx\; 0.363\,\sigma^2.$$

$R=2$: Lloyd–Max Coupling

Naive thresholds at $\mu\pm\sigma$? Not optimal.

Coupled fixed point: best rep is the cell's conditional mean; best threshold is the midpoint of adjacent reps.

① Centroid

$$\hat x_i \leftarrow \mathbb{E}[X\mid \tau_i\le X<\tau_{i+1}].$$

② Midpoint

$$\tau_{i+1} \leftarrow \tfrac{1}{2}(\hat x_i + \hat x_{i+1}).$$

Lloyd–Max Converges Monotonically

Total distortion $D(\tau,\hat x) = \sum_i \int_{\tau_i}^{\tau_{i+1}} (x-\hat x_i)^2 p(x)\,dx$.

Step ① decreases $D$ at fixed $\tau$: each integral is minimized over $\hat x_i$ by the conditional mean.

Step ② decreases $D$ at fixed $\hat x$: the integrand at each $x$ is the smaller of $(x-\hat x_i)^2$ and $(x-\hat x_{i+1})^2$; the minimum switches at the midpoint.

$$D_0 \ge D_1 \ge D_2 \ge \cdots \ge 0 \;\Longrightarrow\; D_t \text{ converges.}$$
03

Why Blocks Win

Even on i.i.d. components, vector quantization beats scalar

Vector Quantization

Quantize an $n$-block jointly:

$$Q^{(n)}: \mathbb{R}^n \to \{1,\ldots,2^{nR}\}.$$

Codebook of $2^{nR}$ representatives. Encoder picks the nearest; decoder looks it up.

Cells partition $\mathbb{R}^n$ — their shape is what scalar quantization can't control.

Round Cells Beat Rectangles

Scalar (2D)

Vector (hex)

Why Round Wins: Typical-Shell Concentration

Cell second moment under uniform mass: $M(\mathcal{C}) = \frac{1}{|\mathcal{C}|}\int_\mathcal{C}\|x-c\|^2 dx$.

At fixed area, the disk minimizes $M$. Square: 8% worse; rectangle: much worse.

High dimension: mass concentrates on a thin shell of radius $\sqrt{n\sigma^2}$. Spherical cells cover the shell efficiently; cubic cells waste budget on empty corners.
04

Shannon's Theorem

$R(D) = \inf I(X;\widehat X)$ subject to $\mathbb{E}\,d\le D$

Information $R(D)$ and the Theorem

Single-letter optimization over test channels $P_{\widehat X\mid X}$:

$$R^{(I)}(D) \;=\; \inf_{\substack{P_{\widehat X\mid X}\\ \mathbb{E}\,d(X,\widehat X)\le D}}\; I(X;\widehat X).$$

Theorem (Shannon, 1959).

For a memoryless source under single-letter distortion: $\;R(D) = R^{(I)}(D).$

Two halves: achievability via random coding, converse via data processing.

Why Mutual Information Appears

Index $M = f(X^n)$ takes at most $2^{nR}$ values. Reconstruction $\widehat X^n = g(M)$ is a deterministic function of $M$.

$$nR \;\ge\; H(M) \;\ge\; I(X^n; M) \;\ge\; I(X^n; \widehat X^n).$$
Smallest information $\widehat X$ must carry about $X$ at distortion $\le D$ is exactly $\inf I(X;\widehat X)$.

Achievability — Random Codebook

Fix $P_{\widehat X\mid X}$ with $\mathbb{E}\,d \le D-\delta$ and $I(X;\widehat X)=I$. Marginal $P_{\widehat X}$.

Generate $M = 2^{nR}$ codewords i.i.d. from $P_{\widehat X}^{\otimes n}$.

Encoder: find any $m$ with $(X^n, \widehat X^n(m)) \in T_\varepsilon^{(n)}$. Send $m$.

Decoder: output $\widehat X^n(m)$.

Achievability — Joint AEP and Encoder Error

Joint AEP: each random codeword is jointly typical with $x^n$ with probability $\ge 2^{-n(I+3\varepsilon)}$.

Codewords independent, so:

$$\Pr[\text{no match}\mid X^n] \;\le\; \bigl(1 - 2^{-n(I+3\varepsilon)}\bigr)^M \;\le\; \exp\!\bigl(-2^{n(R-I-3\varepsilon)}\bigr).$$

For $R > I+3\varepsilon$: doubly-exponentially small. On the match event, joint typicality forces $\frac{1}{n}\sum d \le \mathbb{E}\,d + \varepsilon$.

Every $R > R^{(I)}(D)$ is achievable.

Converse — Three Steps

Assume $(R,D)$ achievable; let $E^n = X^n - \widehat X^n$.

  1. Rate dominates info: $nR \ge H(M) \ge I(X^n;\widehat X^n)$.
  2. Memoryless source: $I(X^n;\widehat X^n) \ge \sum_i I(X_i;\widehat X_i)$ (chain rule + conditioning reduces entropy).
  3. Definition + convexity: $\sum_i I(X_i;\widehat X_i) \ge n\,R^{(I)}\bigl(\tfrac{1}{n}\sum_i D_i\bigr) \ge n\,R^{(I)}(D)$.
$$\Longrightarrow\; R \ge R^{(I)}(D).$$

Binary Toy: $n=24,\ D=1/4$

Bit strings, Hamming balls, the threshold rate

Bernoulli Source, Hamming Distortion

$X_i \stackrel{\mathrm{iid}}{\sim} \mathrm{Bern}(1/2)$, $d_H(x,\hat x) = \mathbb{1}\{x\ne\hat x\}$. Test channel:

$$X = \widehat X \oplus Z, \qquad \widehat X\sim\mathrm{Bern}(1/2),\; Z\sim\mathrm{Bern}(D),\; \widehat X\perp Z.$$

Then $\Pr[X\ne\widehat X] = D$. Compute mutual information:

$$I(X;\widehat X) \;=\; H(X) - H(X\mid\widehat X) \;=\; 1 - H_b(D).$$

The matching converse (Lecture exercise) gives $R(D) = 1 - H_b(D)$ for $0\le D\le 1/2$.

One Source, Many Candidates

Source draw, $n=24$:

Goal: find a codeword within $nD = 6$ Hamming flips.

A random Bernoulli$(1/2)^{24}$ codeword typically differs in $\approx 12$ positions. Far from the ball.

Generate many candidates; eventually one lands inside.

Threshold Rate from Ball Volume

Hamming ball volume bound: $|B(0,nD)| \le 2^{nH_b(D)}$ for $D\le 1/2$ (sum of binomial pmf weights).

A random codeword falls in $B(X^n, nD)$ with probability:

$$\dfrac{|B(0,nD)|}{2^n} \;\le\; 2^{-n(1 - H_b(D))}.$$

$M = 2^{nR}$ candidates contain a match w.h.p. iff $R > 1 - H_b(D) = R(D)$.

For $D=1/4$: $R(D) \approx 0.189$ bits/symbol. The 64-codeword run is well past threshold.

Recap

  • R–D problem: trade rate for fidelity under fixed $d$.
  • Scalar: $D(1) = \sigma^2(1 - 2/\pi)$; Lloyd–Max alternates conditional mean and midpoint.
  • Vector wins: round cells cover the typical shell.
  • Shannon: $R(D) = \inf I(X;\widehat X)$ s.t. $\mathbb{E}\,d \le D$.
  • Achievability: random codebook + joint typicality.
  • Converse: DPI + chain rule + convexity.
  • Binary toy: $R(D) = 1 - H_b(D)$, ball-volume threshold.
