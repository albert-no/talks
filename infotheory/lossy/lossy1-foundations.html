Problem Setup

Trade bits for fidelity on real-valued sources

Lossless Is Not Enough

Discrete source: $H(X)$ bits per symbol suffices.

Continuous source: $H(X) = \infty$. Perfect reconstruction needs infinitely many bits.

Lossy compression: trade rate against fidelity, measured by a distortion $d(x,\hat x)\ge 0$.

Central question: how few bits per sample at distortion $\le D$?

Three Distortion Functions

Squared error

$$d(x,\hat x)=(x-\hat x)^2$$

Default for continuous sources.

Hamming

$$d_H(x,\hat x)=\mathbb{1}\{x\ne\hat x\}$$

Default for discrete sources.

Absolute error

$$d_1(x,\hat x)=|x-\hat x|$$

Sparse, robust.

Block Code and Per-Letter Distortion

An $(n,R)$ lossy code:

$$f:\mathcal{X}^n\to\{1,\ldots,2^{nR}\},\qquad g:\{1,\ldots,2^{nR}\}\to\widehat{\mathcal{X}}^n.$$

Reconstruction $\widehat X^n = g(f(X^n))$. Per-letter average distortion:

$$d^{(n)}(x^n,\hat x^n) \;=\; \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n d(x_i,\hat x_i).$$

Achievable Region and $R(D)$

$(R,D)$ achievable: for every $\varepsilon > 0$ and large $n$, there exists an $(n, R+\varepsilon)$ code with

$$\mathbb{E}\bigl[d^{(n)}(X^n,\, g(f(X^n)))\bigr] \;\le\; D + \varepsilon.$$
$$R(D) \;=\; \inf\bigl\{R : (R,D)\text{ achievable}\bigr\}.$$

Non-increasing · convex · continuous.

Scalar Quantization

$R\in\{0,1,2\}$ on a Gaussian, Lloyd–Max convergence

Toy: $X\sim\mathcal{N}(\mu,\sigma^2)$

  • Squared error: $d(x,\hat x) = (x-\hat x)^2$
  • Send $R$ bits per symbol · pick partition + reconstruction levels
$$\begin{aligned} R = 0 \;&: \;\text{no bits — output a constant} \\ R = 1 \;&: \;\text{one threshold, two cells} \\ R = 2 \;&: \;\text{Lloyd–Max (4 cells)} \end{aligned}$$
$R=0$: send nothing. Optimal constant $c$?

$R=0$: $c^\star = \mu$, distortion $= \sigma^2$

Add and subtract $\mu$:

$$\begin{aligned} \mathbb{E}[(X-c)^2] &= \sigma^2 + \underbrace{2(\mu-c)\,\mathbb{E}[X-\mu]}_{=\,0} + (\mu-c)^2 \\ &= \sigma^2 + (\mu-c)^2 \\ &\ge \sigma^2 \qquad\text{(equality iff } c=\mu\text{).} \end{aligned}$$

Best constant achieves variance $\sigma^2$ · conditional mean minimizes squared error inside any cell.

$R=1$: One Threshold, Two Cells

Symmetry: threshold at $\mu$. Reps are conditional means of the half-lines.

Standardize $X = \mu + \sigma Z$, $Z\sim\mathcal{N}(0,1)$. Half-Gaussian mean:

$$\mathbb{E}[Z\mid Z>0] \;=\; \dfrac{1/\sqrt{2\pi}}{1/2} \;=\; \sqrt{\tfrac{2}{\pi}}.$$

By symmetry $\mathbb{E}[Z^2\mid Z>0]=1$, so the cell variance is $1 - 2/\pi$. Scalar quantizer achieves:

$$\mathbb{E}\bigl[(X-\widehat X)^2\bigr] \;=\; \sigma^2\bigl(1 - \tfrac{2}{\pi}\bigr) \;\approx\; 0.363\,\sigma^2.$$

$R=2$: Lloyd–Max Coupling

Naive thresholds at $\mu\pm\sigma$? Not optimal.

Coupled fixed point: best rep is the cell's conditional mean;
best threshold is the midpoint of adjacent reps.

① Centroid

$$\hat x_i \leftarrow \mathbb{E}[X\mid \tau_i\le X<\tau_{i+1}].$$

② Midpoint

$$\tau_{i+1} \leftarrow \tfrac{1}{2}(\hat x_i + \hat x_{i+1}).$$

Lloyd–Max Converges Monotonically

Total distortion $D(\tau,\hat x) = \sum_i \int_{\tau_i}^{\tau_{i+1}} (x-\hat x_i)^2 p(x)\,dx$.

Step ① at fixed $\tau$: conditional mean minimizes each cell.

Step ② at fixed $\hat x$: nearest-rep partition, switching at the midpoint.

$$D_0 \ge D_1 \ge D_2 \ge \cdots \ge 0 \;\Longrightarrow\; D_t \text{ converges.}$$

Why Blocks Win

Even on i.i.d. components, vector quantization beats scalar

Vector Quantization

Quantize an $n$-block jointly:

$$Q^{(n)}: \mathbb{R}^n \to \{1,\ldots,2^{nR}\}.$$

Codebook of $2^{nR}$ representatives. Encoder picks the nearest; decoder looks it up.

Cells partition $\mathbb{R}^n$ — their shape is what scalar quantization can't control.

Worst-case distortion $\sim$ corner-to-center distance · fix the cell's area.

Shannon's Theorem

$R(D) = \inf I(X;\widehat X)$ · converse, binary toy, achievability

Information $R(D)$ and the Theorem

Single-letter optimization over test channels $P_{\widehat X\mid X}$:

$$R^{(I)}(D) \;=\; \inf_{\substack{P_{\widehat X\mid X}\\ \mathbb{E}\,d(X,\widehat X)\le D}}\; I(X;\widehat X).$$

Theorem (Shannon, 1959).

For a memoryless source under single-letter distortion: $\;R(D) = R^{(I)}(D).$

Two halves: achievability via random coding, converse via data processing.

Why Mutual Information Appears

Encoder + decoder = noisy channel from $X^n$ to $\widehat X^n$.

$X^n$ $\xrightarrow{\;f\;}$ $M$ $\xrightarrow{\;g\;}$ $\widehat X^n$

Markov chain · $M\in\{1,\ldots,2^{nR}\}$ · $\widehat X^n = g(M)$

$$nR \;\ge\; H(M) \;\ge\; I(X^n; M) \;\ge\; I(X^n; \widehat X^n).$$

Rate = bandwidth of the $X\to\widehat X$ channel.

Minimum bandwidth at distortion $\le D$: $\;\inf I(X;\widehat X)$.

Converse — Outline

Assume $(R,D)$ achievable. Goal: $\;R \ge R^{(I)}(D)$.

  1. Rate dominates information:  $nR \ge I(X^n;\widehat X^n)$
  2. Single-letterize:  $I(X^n;\widehat X^n) \ge \sum_i I(X_i;\widehat X_i)$
  3. Convexity + non-increasing:  $\sum_i I(X_i;\widehat X_i) \ge n\,R^{(I)}(D)$
$$nR \;\ge\; I(X^n;\widehat X^n) \;\ge\; \sum_i I(X_i;\widehat X_i) \;\ge\; n\,R^{(I)}(D).$$
Each step lower-bounds the previous.

Step 1 — Rate Dominates Information

Index $M = f(X^n) \in \{1,\ldots,2^{nR}\}$ · $\widehat X^n = g(M)$ deterministic.

$$\begin{aligned} nR &\;\ge\; H(M) &&\text{(uniform bound on log range)} \\ &\;\ge\; I(X^n; M) &&\text{(}H \ge I\text{)} \\ &\;\ge\; I(X^n; \widehat X^n) &&\text{(DPI: }\widehat X^n = g(M)\text{)} \end{aligned}$$
$nR \;\ge\; I(X^n; \widehat X^n)$.

Step 2 — Single-Letterize

Memoryless source $\Rightarrow H(X^n) = \sum_i H(X_i)$. Conditioning reduces entropy.

$$\begin{aligned} I(X^n; \widehat X^n) &= H(X^n) - H(X^n \mid \widehat X^n) \\ &= \sum_i H(X_i) - \sum_i H(X_i \mid X_{<i}, \widehat X^n) \\ &\ge \sum_i H(X_i) - \sum_i H(X_i \mid \widehat X_i) \\ &= \sum_i I(X_i; \widehat X_i). \end{aligned}$$

Step 3 — Convexity

Per-coordinate distortion $D_i = \mathbb{E}[d(X_i, \widehat X_i)]$ · achievable $\Rightarrow$ $\tfrac{1}{n}\sum_i D_i \le D$.

By definition: $\;I(X_i; \widehat X_i) \;\ge\; R^{(I)}(D_i).$

$$\begin{aligned} \tfrac{1}{n}\sum_i I(X_i; \widehat X_i) &\;\ge\; \tfrac{1}{n}\sum_i R^{(I)}(D_i) \\ &\;\ge\; R^{(I)}\!\Bigl(\tfrac{1}{n}\sum_i D_i\Bigr) &&\text{(convex)} \\ &\;\ge\; R^{(I)}(D) &&\text{(non-increasing)} \end{aligned}$$

Converse — Summary

$$\begin{aligned} nR &\;\stackrel{(1)}{\ge}\; I(X^n; \widehat X^n) \\ &\;\stackrel{(2)}{\ge}\; \sum_i I(X_i; \widehat X_i) \\ &\;\stackrel{(3)}{\ge}\; n\,R^{(I)}\!\Bigl(\tfrac{1}{n}\sum_i D_i\Bigr) \\ &\;\stackrel{(3)}{\ge}\; n\,R^{(I)}(D) \end{aligned}$$
$R \;\ge\; R^{(I)}(D).$

Rate–distortion function:

$$R(D) \;=\; 1 - H_b(D), \qquad R(\tfrac{1}{4}) \;=\; 1 - H_b(\tfrac{1}{4}) \;\approx\; 0.189 \text{ bits}.$$

Ball Volume = Typical-Set Size

Recall (typical set). $T_\varepsilon^{(n)}(P) = \{z^n : |\tfrac{1}{n}\!\sum_i \mathbb{1}\{z_i=a\} - P(a)| < \varepsilon \;\forall a\}$. AEP: $|T_\varepsilon^{(n)}(P)| \doteq 2^{nH(P)}$.

Hamming ball $B(0, nD) = \{z\in\{0,1\}^n : \text{wt}(z) \le nD\}$ · each $z$ is an error pattern.

Typical $\mathrm{Bern}(D)$ pattern has $\approx nD$ ones · so

$$|T_\varepsilon^{(n)}(\mathrm{Bern}(D))| \;\doteq\; 2^{n H_b(D)}.$$
Ball volume $\;\asymp\;$ size of the strongly-typical set under the error law.

Threshold Rate from Ball Volume

Hence $|B(0, nD)| \le 2^{n H_b(D)}$ · a random codeword lands in $B(X^n, nD)$ with prob.

$$\dfrac{|B(0,nD)|}{2^n} \;\le\; 2^{-n(1 - H_b(D))}.$$

$M = 2^{nR}$ candidates · expected hits $= M \cdot 2^{-n(1-H_b(D))}$.

Match w.h.p. iff $R > 1 - H_b(D) = R(D)$.

$D=1/4$: $R(D) \approx 0.189$ · $M=16$ ($R\approx 0.167$) just under · $M=32$ above.

From Binary Toy to General Recipe

Binary case:

  • Drew codewords $\widehat X^n(m) \stackrel{\mathrm{iid}}{\sim} \mathrm{Bern}(1/2)^n$.
  • Found a candidate $\widehat X^n(14)$ within $nD$ Hamming flips of $X^n$.
  • Empirical joint $(X^n, \widehat X^n(14))$ matched the test-channel $P_{X,\widehat X}$ at $D=1/4$.
Three ingredients: codeword law, match criterion, typicality.

Generalization: the Optimal Test Channel

  • Identify the optimizer for source $P_X$ at distortion $D$:
$$P^\star_{X,\widehat X} \;=\; \arg\inf\!\bigl\{ I(X;\widehat X) : \mathbb{E}\,d(X,\widehat X) \le D \bigr\}.$$
  • Draw codewords iid from the marginal $P^\star_{\widehat X}$
  • Pick any $m$ with $(X^n, \widehat X^n(m))$ jointly typical
  • $M = 2^{nR}$ codewords with $R > I(X;\widehat X^\star)$  $\Rightarrow$  match w.h.p.
Open question: how to find $P^\star_{X,\widehat X}$ for a given source. Lecture 2: Gaussian, Laplacian.

Achievability — Random Codebook

Formal recipe for any source.

Test channel $P_{\widehat X\mid X}$:  $\mathbb{E}[d(X,\widehat X)] \le D-\delta$,  $I(X;\widehat X) = I$.

Codebook $\{\widehat X^n(m)\}_{m=1}^{2^{nR}}$ drawn iid from $P_{\widehat X}^{\otimes n}$.

Encoder:  send any $m$ with $(X^n, \widehat X^n(m)) \in T_\varepsilon^{(n)}$.

Decoder:  output $\widehat X^n(m)$.

Achievability — Joint AEP

Joint AEP:  per codeword, $\Pr[(X^n, \widehat X^n(m))\in T_\varepsilon^{(n)}] \ge 2^{-n(I+3\varepsilon)}$. Codewords iid:

$$\Pr[\text{no match}\mid X^n] \;\le\; \bigl(1 - 2^{-n(I+3\varepsilon)}\bigr)^{2^{nR}} \;\le\; \exp\!\bigl(-2^{n(R-I-3\varepsilon)}\bigr).$$

$R > I + 3\varepsilon$: encoder error doubly-exponentially small.

On the match event:  $\tfrac{1}{n}\sum d \le \mathbb{E}\,d + \varepsilon \le D - \delta + \varepsilon$.

Every $R > R^{(I)}(D)$ achievable.

Recap

  • R–D problem: trade rate for fidelity under fixed $d$.
  • Scalar: 1-bit quantizer gives $\sigma^2(1 - 2/\pi)$; Lloyd–Max alternates conditional mean and midpoint.
  • Vector wins: round cells cover the typical shell.
  • Shannon: $R(D) = \inf I(X;\widehat X)$ s.t. $\mathbb{E}\,d \le D$.
  • Achievability: random codebook + joint typicality.
  • Converse: DPI + chain rule + convexity.
  • Binary toy: $R(D) = 1 - H_b(D)$, ball-volume threshold.
