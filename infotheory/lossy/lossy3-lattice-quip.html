01

Why Structure

Optimal VQ is rate-distortion right and operationally dead.

What a Real Quantizer Must Do

Production setting: $10^{10}$+ LLM weights, every layer, every forward pass.

  • Encode in $\mathcal{O}(d)$ per block, not $\mathcal{O}(2^{dR})$.
  • No codebook to store or fetch from RAM.
  • Embarrassingly parallel across blocks.
  • MSE close to the rate-distortion optimum.
Compression is not enough. The quantizer has to be cheap.

Standard VQ Recap

Group $d$ scalars into $\mathbf{w}\in\mathbb{R}^d$; map to nearest of $K=2^{dR}$ centroids.

$$\mathcal{C} = \{\mathbf{c}_1,\ldots,\mathbf{c}_K\}\subset\mathbb{R}^d.$$

Larger $d$ buys rounder cells, lower distortion at fixed rate. Shannon's theorem says push $d \to \infty$.

The Complexity Wall

Storage

$d \cdot 2^{dR}$ floats in the codebook.

$d=8$, $R=4$: $\approx 34$ billion floats.

Search

$\mathcal{O}(d \cdot 2^{dR})$ per nearest-neighbor query.

Per-weight, per-forward-pass: infeasible.

Both grow exponentially in $d$. Optimal VQ is operationally dead at LLM scale.

Uniform Quantization Buys Speed

Fix a step $\Delta$, round each coordinate: $\hat w_i = \Delta\cdot\lfloor w_i/\Delta + \tfrac12\rfloor$.

  • $\mathcal{O}(d)$ encode: arithmetic, no search.
  • Zero codebook: only the rule $(\Delta)$ is stored.
  • Parallel: coordinates are independent.
  • Penalty: small constant factor of MSE vs. optimal VQ at high $d$.
Lattices generalize this: fast and structured, with smaller MSE penalty.
02

Lattice Codes

A regular grid: uniform Voronoi cells, algebraic rounding

Definition

Lattice $\Lambda\subset\mathbb{R}^d$: additive subgroup, integer combinations of a basis.

$$\Lambda \;=\; B\mathbb{Z}^d, \qquad B = [\,b_1\,|\,b_2\,|\,\cdots\,|\,b_d\,].$$

Voronoi region $\mathcal{V}(\lambda) = \{x : \|x-\lambda\|\le\|x-\lambda'\|\;\forall\lambda'\in\Lambda\}$.

Translation symmetry: every Voronoi region is congruent ŌĆö one cell shape, repeated.

Packing Gain ŌĆö Regularity Buys Distortion

Normalized second moment of $\Lambda$:

$$G(\Lambda) \;=\; \dfrac{1}{V^{1+2/d}}\int_\mathcal{V}\|x\|^2\,dx, \qquad V = |\mathcal{V}|.$$

Smaller $G$ means rounder cells, less MSE per unit volume:

  • $\mathbb{Z}^d$ (cube): $G = 1/12$, any $d$.
  • $E_8$ ($d=8$): $G \approx 0.0717$, near-optimal.
  • Sphere bound: $G\to 1/(2\pi e)\approx 0.0585$.

Fast Rounding ŌĆö $\mathcal{O}(d)$ via Algebra

Lattice nearest-neighbor uses the algebra of $\Lambda$, not a list search.

  • $\mathbb{Z}^d$: $\lfloor x + \tfrac12\rfloor$ coordinate-wise.
  • $D_d$ (even-sum integers): round, then fix parity.
  • $E_8$: $D_8$ rounding plus a half-integer shift; pick the closer.
Codebook size $2^{dR}$ in principle; encoding $\mathcal{O}(d)$ in practice.
03

Finite Codebook

Base lattice $\Lambda$, scaling $\Delta$, spherical shaping

Three Components

  1. Base lattice $\Lambda$ (e.g., $E_8$). Stored implicitly as basis $B$.
  2. Scaling $\Delta$: $\Lambda_\Delta = \Delta\cdot\Lambda$. Distortion variance $\propto \Delta^2 G(\Lambda)$.
  3. Shaping: intersect with a region $\mathcal{S}$. For Gaussian-like data, take $\mathcal{S}$ a $d$-ball.
$$\mathcal{C}_{\text{lattice}} \;=\; \Lambda_\Delta \cap \mathcal{S}(r).$$

Why a Sphere ŌĆö Match the Typical Set

For $X\sim\mathcal{N}(0, I_d)$, $\|X\|^2$ concentrates at $\sqrt d$:

$$\Pr\bigl[\,|\|X\|^2 - d|\ge\varepsilon d\,\bigr] \;\le\; 2 e^{-\Omega(\varepsilon^2 d)}.$$

A spherical $\mathcal{S}$ of radius $\sqrt d + O(1)$ covers nearly all probability with no wasted codepoints.

Cubic shaping wastes a $(d^{-1/2})^d$ fraction near the typical sphere.
04

QUIP: Incoherence Processing

Chee, Cai, Kuleshov, De Sa — NeurIPS 2023

The Proxy Loss

Per-layer post-training quantization: replace $W \in \mathbb{R}^{m\times n}$ by $\widehat W$.

Calibration data gives $H = \mathbb{E}_x[xx^\top]$. Output error becomes

$$\ell(\widehat W) \;=\; \mathbb{E}_x\bigl\|(\widehat W - W)x\bigr\|^2 \;=\; \mathrm{tr}\bigl((\widehat W - W)\,H\,(\widehat W - W)^\top\bigr).$$

Hessian-weighted MSE on the weights, decoupled across rows.

Chee et al., "QuIP: 2-Bit Quantization of Large Language Models With Guarantees", NeurIPS 2023 (arXiv:2307.13304).

Incoherence ŌĆö A Spectral Outlier Bound

$H = Q\Lambda Q^\top$ is $\mu$-incoherent if

$$\max_{i,j} |Q_{ij}| \;\le\; \mu/\sqrt{n}.$$

$W$ is $\mu$-incoherent if $\max_{i,j} |W_{ij}| \le \mu \|W\|_F / \sqrt{mn}$.

Incoherent = no entry much larger than the typical scale. A principled outlier suppression.

Make Things Incoherent ŌĆö Random Rotations

Conjugate by random orthogonal $U\in\mathbb{R}^{m\times m}$, $V\in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}$:

$$\widetilde W \;=\; U W V^\top, \qquad \widetilde H \;=\; V H V^\top.$$

The proxy is invariant: $\mathrm{tr}(\widetilde W\,\widetilde H\,\widetilde W^\top) = \mathrm{tr}(W H W^\top)$.

QUIP uses a Kronecker product $U = U_1 \otimes U_2$ (and same for $V$): $\widetilde\mathcal{O}(1)$-incoherent w.h.p., multiply in $\Theta(n\sqrt n)$.

LDLQ ŌĆö Adaptive Per-Coordinate Rounding

After rotation, round one column at a time with linear feedback from past errors:

$$\widehat W_k \;=\; Q\bigl(W_k + (W_{1:k-1} - \widehat W_{1:k-1})\,a_k\bigr).$$

$Q$ is per-coordinate (nearest or stochastic). Choose $a_k$ from the $LDL^\top$ decomposition $H = (\bar U + I)\,D\,(\bar U + I)^\top$.

Worst- and average-case optimal among rounding methods with linear feedback (Theorem 1).

QUIP ŌĆö End-to-End

  1. Rotate: $\widetilde W = UWV^\top$, $\widetilde H = VHV^\top$ via Kronecker random orthogonals.
  2. Round: per-coordinate LDLQ on $\widetilde W$ using $\widetilde H$.
  3. Inference: undo the rotation around the quantized linear layer.
First viable 2-bit LLM quantization with theoretical guarantees.
05

QUIP#: Hadamard + Lattice

Tseng, Chee, Sun, Kuleshov, De Sa — ICML 2024

Two Upgrades Over QUIP

Rotation

Kronecker $\to$ Randomized Hadamard Transform.

$\Theta(n \log n)$ time, sign-only multiplies, sharper incoherence bound.

Quantizer

Per-coordinate $\to$ $E_8$ lattice on blocks of 8.

Ball-shaped codebook matches the post-rotation Gaussian.

Tseng et al., "QuIP\#: Even Better LLM Quantization with Hadamard Incoherence and Lattice Codebooks", ICML 2024 (arXiv:2402.04396).

Step 1: Randomized Hadamard Transform

$H_n$: scaled Hadamard ($\pm 1$ entries, orthogonal). $S_U, S_V$: diagonals of independent $\pm 1$ signs.

$$\widetilde W \;=\; (H_m S_U)\,W\,(H_n S_V)^\top.$$

Each entry is a sign-randomized linear combination of all of $W$ $\Rightarrow$ marginally Gaussian (CLT):

$$\mu_W \;=\; \mathcal{O}\bigl(\sqrt{\log(mn/\delta)}\bigr), \qquad \widetilde W_{ij} \;\approx\; \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(0,\tfrac{\|W\|_F^2}{mn}\bigr).$$

Multiply via Fast Walsh-Hadamard: $\Theta(n\log n)$, no float multiplies.

Step 2: $E_8$ Lattice Codebook

$E_8$: integer or half-integer 8-vectors with even sum. Best sphere packing in $\mathbb{R}^8$ (Viazovska 2017).

$$E_8 \;=\; \bigl(\mathbb{Z}^8 \cup (\mathbb{Z}^8 + \tfrac12)\bigr) \cap \{x : \mathbf{1}^\top x \in 2\mathbb{Z}\}.$$

QUIP\# groups rotated weights into 8-vectors; each block quantized to a 2-bit per weight $E_8$ codebook (E8P).

$E_8$ near-spherical Voronoi $\Rightarrow$ minimal MSE for ball-shaped post-RHT data.

BlockLDLQ ŌĆö Adaptive Block Rounding

LDLQ rounds one column; vector quantization rounds $g$ columns at once. Generalize:

$$\widehat W_k \;=\; Q_{E_8}\bigl(W_k + (W_{1:k-g} - \widehat W_{1:k-g})\,A_k\bigr).$$

$A_k$ from a $g$-block $LDL^\top$ of $H$. Theorem 4.1: error scales as $\frac{g m \mu^2}{n}\sigma^2 \mathrm{tr}(H^{1/2})^2$.

Same $\mathrm{tr}(H) \to \mathrm{tr}(H^{1/2})^2/n$ improvement as scalar QUIP, now with VQ.

The Two-Faced Effective Codebook

To the data

Pseudo-random scattering matched to incoherent Gaussian weights.

Approaches Shannon-optimal random Gaussian codebook.

To the hardware

A rigid $E_8$ grid. 1 KiB codebook, fits in L1.

$\mathcal{O}(d)$ rounding, $\mathcal{O}(n\log n)$ rotation.

Rate-distortion benefits without the exponential cost of unstructured VQ.

Recap

  • Wall: optimal VQ at high $d$ needs $2^{dR}$ codewords; encoding cost matches.
  • Lattices: congruent Voronoi, $\mathcal{O}(d)$ rounding; $E_8$ near-spherical.
  • Shaping: base lattice + scaling + spherical $\mathcal{S}$.
  • QUIP: Kronecker random rotation $+$ per-coordinate LDLQ.
  • QUIP\#: Randomized Hadamard rotation $+$ $E_8$ BlockLDLQ. Sub-2-bit/weight.
