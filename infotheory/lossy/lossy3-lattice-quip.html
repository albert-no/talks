01

Why Structure

Shannon says go to high $d$. Cost says you can't.

Standard VQ Recap

Group $d$ scalars into $\mathbf{w}\in\mathbb{R}^d$; map to nearest of $K=2^{dR}$ centroids.

$$\mathcal{C} = \{\mathbf{c}_1,\ldots,\mathbf{c}_K\}\subset\mathbb{R}^d.$$

Larger $d$ buys rounder cells, lower distortion at fixed rate. So why not take $d$ very large?

The Complexity Wall

Storage

$d\cdot K = d\cdot 2^{dR}$ floats.

$d=8$, $R=4$: $\approx 34$ billion floats.

Search

$\mathcal{O}(d\cdot 2^{dR})$ per nearest-neighbor query.

$10^{10}$ LLM weights $\times$ that: infeasible.

Direct VQ is theoretically optimal but operationally dead at LLM scale.

What We Need

  • Implicit codebook: described by a rule, not a list.
  • Fast nearest-neighbor: $\mathcal{O}(d)$ per query via algebra.
  • Match to data: LLM weights are spiky; the lattice is rigid.
Lattice + rotation buys all three.
02

Lattice Codes

A regular grid: uniform Voronoi cells, algebraic rounding

Definition

Lattice $\Lambda\subset\mathbb{R}^d$: integer combinations of basis vectors.

$$\Lambda \;=\; B\mathbb{Z}^d, \qquad B = [\,b_1\,|\,b_2\,|\,\cdots\,|\,b_d\,].$$

Voronoi region $\mathcal{V}(\lambda) = \{x : \|x-\lambda\|\le\|x-\lambda'\|\;\forall\lambda'\in\Lambda\}$.

By translation symmetry, all Voronoi regions are congruent — one cell shape, repeated.

Packing Gain — Regularity Buys Distortion

Normalized second moment of $\Lambda$:

$$G(\Lambda) \;=\; \dfrac{1}{V^{1+2/d}}\int_\mathcal{V}\|x\|^2\,dx, \qquad V = |\mathcal{V}|.$$
  • $\mathbb{Z}^d$: $G = 1/12$, any $d$. Worst of practical interest.
  • $E_8$: $G \approx 0.0717$ (8-D). 14% better than $\mathbb{Z}^8$.
  • Leech ($d=24$): $G \approx 0.0658$. Near the sphere bound.
  • Sphere bound: $G\to 1/(2\pi e)\approx 0.0585$.

Fast Rounding — $\mathcal{O}(d)$ via Algebra

$\mathbb{Z}^d$: nearest point is $\lfloor x + \tfrac12\rfloor$ coordinate-wise.

$D_d$ (even-sum integer lattice): round each coordinate; if sum odd, flip the worst-rounded coord.

$E_8$: combine $D_8$ rounding with a half-integer translate; pick the closer.

Codebook size $2^{dR}$ in principle; search $\mathcal{O}(d)$ via the algebraic structure.
03

Finite Codebook

Base lattice $\Lambda$, scaling $\Delta$, spherical shaping

Three Components

  1. Base lattice $\Lambda$ (e.g., $E_8$). Implicit: stored as basis $B$.
  2. Scaling $\Delta$: $\Lambda_\Delta = \Delta\cdot\Lambda$. Distortion variance $\propto\Delta^2 G(\Lambda)$.
  3. Shaping: intersect with a region $\mathcal{S}$. For Gaussian-like data, $\mathcal{S}$ is a $d$-ball.
$$\mathcal{C}_{\text{lattice}} \;=\; \Lambda_\Delta \cap \mathcal{S}(r).$$

Why a Sphere — Match the Typical Set

For $X\sim\mathcal{N}(0, I_d)$, $\|X\|^2\sim\chi^2_d$ has mean $d$, std $\sqrt{2d}$:

$$\Pr\bigl[\,|\|X\|^2 - d|\ge\varepsilon d\,\bigr] \;\le\; 2 e^{-\Omega(\varepsilon^2 d)}.$$

Mass concentrates on a thin shell of radius $\sqrt{d}$. A spherical $\mathcal{S}$ of radius $\sqrt{d} + O(1)$ contains nearly all of the probability with no wasted codepoints.

Cubic shaping $[-A,A]^d$ wastes a $(d^{-1/2})^d$ fraction near the typical sphere — catastrophic in high $d$.

04

QUIP#: Lattice for LLM Weights

Quantization with incoherence processing (Tseng et al. 2024)

Source–Channel Mismatch

Lattice cells are uniform and isotropic. LLM weights are not: a few outliers dominate; the typical set is heavy-tailed and skewed.

Drop a spherical $\mathcal{C}_{\text{lattice}}$ on raw weights: most of the codebook is empty; most data sits far from the nearest lattice point.

Two options: bend the lattice to the weights (kills fast rounding) or bend the weights to the lattice. QUIP# does the latter.

Step 1: Hadamard Rotation

Pick orthogonal $\mathbf{U}\in\mathbb{R}^{m\times m}$, $\mathbf{V}\in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}$ — random Hadamard transforms, $\mathcal{O}(d\log d)$.

$$\widetilde{\mathbf{W}} \;=\; \mathbf{U}\,\mathbf{W}\,\mathbf{V}^\top.$$

Orthogonality preserves Frobenius norm, so $\widetilde{\mathbf{W}}$ encodes the same model.

Each entry of $\widetilde{\mathbf{W}}$ is a sum of many sign-flipped products $\Rightarrow$ CLT $\Rightarrow$ marginally Gaussian:

$$\widetilde W_{ij}\;\approx\;\mathcal{N}\!\bigl(0,\tfrac{1}{mn}\|\mathbf{W}\|_F^2\bigr).$$

Step 2: Lattice Quantize and Code

Block the entries of $\widetilde{\mathbf{W}}$ into $d$-vectors (e.g. $E_8$, $d=8$). Lattice round:

$$\widehat{\mathbf{W}} \;=\; Q_{\Lambda_\Delta}(\widetilde{\mathbf{W}}).$$

Store integer indices $\mathbf{n}_b$ per block. Indices concentrate near the origin (spherically symmetric data) — entropy-code them.

At inference, undo the rotation: $\mathbf{W}_q = \mathbf{U}^\top\widehat{\mathbf{W}}\mathbf{V}$.

The Two-Faced Effective Codebook

To the data

Pseudo-random scattering contoured to the LLM weight distribution. Behaves like the Shannon-optimal random Gaussian codebook.

To the hardware

Underneath, a rigid grid $\Lambda$. Zero codebook storage; $\mathcal{O}(d)$ rounding per block.

QUIP# harvests the rate–distortion benefits of Shannon's theory without paying its exponential cost.
Tseng et al., "QuIP#: Even Better LLM Quantization with Hadamard Incoherence and Lattice Codebooks", ICML 2024.

Recap

  • Complexity wall: unstructured VQ needs $2^{dR}$ codewords. Dead at LLM scale.
  • Lattices: congruent Voronoi cells, near-spherical for $E_8$, Leech; rounding $\mathcal{O}(d)$.
  • Shaping: base lattice + scaling + spherical $\mathcal{S}$, all rule-based.
  • QUIP#: Hadamard rotation Gaussianizes weights; lattice quantize the rotated tensor; entropy code indices.
  • Effective codebook: pseudo-random in the original space, rigid in the rotated space.
