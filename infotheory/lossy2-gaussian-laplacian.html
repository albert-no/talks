01

Gaussian Source, Squared Error

$R(D)=\tfrac12\log(\sigma^2/D)$ — achievability and converse

The Gaussian rate–distortion function

Source $X\sim\mathcal{N}(0,\sigma^2)$, distortion $d(x,\hat x)=(x-\hat x)^2$.

$$R(D)=\begin{cases}\tfrac12\log\dfrac{\sigma^2}{D}, & 0<D\le\sigma^2,\\[2mm] 0, & D\ge\sigma^2.\end{cases}$$

($\log$ is natural; divide by $\log 2$ for bits/symbol.)

Two halves to prove:  achievability (build a code) and converse (no code beats this).

Achievability — the backward Gaussian channel

For $0<D<\sigma^2$, define independent

$$\widehat X\sim\mathcal{N}(0,\,\sigma^2-D),\qquad Z\sim\mathcal{N}(0,D),\qquad X\;\coloneqq\;\widehat X+Z.$$

Then $X\sim\mathcal{N}(0,\sigma^2)$ (variances add), so this is a valid joint with the right $X$ marginal.

Distortion check: $\;\mathbb{E}[(X-\widehat X)^2]=\mathbb{E}[Z^2]=D$. Constraint satisfied with equality.

Achievability — mutual-information calculation

$I(X;\widehat X)=\tfrac12\log(\sigma^2/D)$

Since $X=\widehat X+Z$ with $Z\perp\widehat X$, we have $h(X\mid\widehat X)=h(Z\mid\widehat X)=h(Z)$. Hence

$$I(X;\widehat X)=h(X)-h(Z)=\tfrac12\log(2\pi e\sigma^2)-\tfrac12\log(2\pi eD)=\tfrac12\log\dfrac{\sigma^2}{D}.$$

By Shannon's theorem, every rate $R>\tfrac12\log(\sigma^2/D)$ is achievable: a random codebook of $2^{nR}$ codewords drawn from $\mathcal{N}(0,\sigma^2-D)^{\otimes n}$ contains a jointly typical match for $X^n$ w.h.p. $\;\blacksquare$

Forward form: linear shrinkage of $X$

The same joint $P_{X,\widehat X}$ admits a "forward" view. By the Gaussian conditioning formula,

$$\widehat X\mid X=x\;\sim\;\mathcal{N}\!\left(\frac{\sigma^2-D}{\sigma^2}\,x,\;\frac{D(\sigma^2-D)}{\sigma^2}\right).$$

The conditional mean is the linear shrinkage $\frac{\sigma^2-D}{\sigma^2}x$: the optimal reconstruction has smaller variance than the source — a lossy description cannot reproduce all of the source energy.

Converse — setup and chain of inequalities

Let $f$, $g$ be any $(n,R)$ code with $\frac{1}{n}\mathbb{E}\|X^n-\widehat X^n\|^2\le D$. Let $E^n=X^n-\widehat X^n$ be the error vector.

Step A — rate dominates information
$$nR\;\ge\;H(M)\;\ge\;I(X^n;M)\;\ge\;I(X^n;\widehat X^n)\;=\;h(X^n)-h(X^n\mid\widehat X^n).$$

Replace conditioning by error: $h(X^n\mid\widehat X^n)=h(X^n-\widehat X^n\mid\widehat X^n)=h(E^n\mid\widehat X^n)\le h(E^n)$.

Converse — Gaussian maximizes entropy at fixed variance

Step B — max-entropy bound on $h(E^n)$

Among $\mathbb{R}^n$ vectors with $\mathbb{E}\|E^n\|^2\le nD$, the isotropic Gaussian $\mathcal{N}(0,DI_n)$ maximizes differential entropy:

$$h(E^n)\;\le\;\tfrac{n}{2}\log(2\pi e\,D),\qquad h(X^n)=\tfrac{n}{2}\log(2\pi e\,\sigma^2).$$

Combining with Step A:

$$nR\;\ge\;\tfrac{n}{2}\log(2\pi e\sigma^2)-\tfrac{n}{2}\log(2\pi eD)\;=\;\tfrac{n}{2}\log\dfrac{\sigma^2}{D}\;\Longrightarrow\;R\ge\tfrac12\log\dfrac{\sigma^2}{D}.\;\blacksquare$$

Geometric picture: cover a sphere by small balls

In $\mathbb{R}^n$, a typical Gaussian block sits on a shell of radius $\sqrt{n\sigma^2}$. A distortion-$D$ ball has radius $\sqrt{nD}$.

$$\frac{\text{shell volume}}{\text{ball volume}}\;\approx\;\Bigl(\dfrac{\sigma^2}{D}\Bigr)^{n/2}.$$

Logarithm per coordinate: $\tfrac12\log(\sigma^2/D)$ — matches Shannon's formula. The entropy proof above is the rigorous version.

02

Shannon Lower Bound

Gaussian is the worst-case source at fixed variance

Shannon lower bound (squared error)

For real-valued $X$ with finite differential entropy $h(X)$ and squared-error distortion,

$$R_X(D)\;\ge\;h(X)-\tfrac12\log(2\pi eD).$$

For $X\in\mathbb{R}^d$ with per-coordinate distortion $\frac1d\mathbb{E}\|X-\widehat X\|^2\le D$,

$$R_X(D)\;\ge\;h(X)-\tfrac{d}{2}\log(2\pi eD).$$

Same flavor as the Gaussian converse, but for any source.

SLB proof (scalar)

Proof

For any reconstruction $\widehat X$ with $\mathbb{E}(X-\widehat X)^2\le D$, let $E=X-\widehat X$. Then

$$I(X;\widehat X)=h(X)-h(X\mid\widehat X)=h(X)-h(E\mid\widehat X)\;\ge\;h(X)-h(E).$$

Among scalars with variance $\le D$, the Gaussian maximizes entropy: $h(E)\le\tfrac12\log(2\pi eD)$. Substituting gives the scalar SLB. $\;\blacksquare$

SLB proof (vector) via AM–GM

Proof (vector case)

Let $E^d=X^d-\widehat X^d$ with covariance $K_E$ and $\operatorname{tr}(K_E)\le dD$. The Gaussian-maxent bound gives

$$h(E^d)\le\tfrac12\log\bigl((2\pi e)^d\det K_E\bigr).$$

By the AM–GM inequality on the eigenvalues, $\det K_E\le\bigl(\tfrac{\operatorname{tr}(K_E)}{d}\bigr)^d\le D^d$. Hence $h(E^d)\le\tfrac{d}{2}\log(2\pi eD)$, and the vector SLB follows. $\;\blacksquare$

Gaussian achieves the SLB with equality

For $X\sim\mathcal{N}(0,\sigma^2)$, $h(X)=\tfrac12\log(2\pi e\sigma^2)$. Substituting:

$$R_X(D)\;\ge\;\tfrac12\log(2\pi e\sigma^2)-\tfrac12\log(2\pi eD)\;=\;\tfrac12\log\dfrac{\sigma^2}{D}.$$

The backward Gaussian test channel achieves equality. So the SLB is tight for Gaussian sources.

Gaussian is the hardest source at fixed variance

Among all sources with variance $\sigma^2$ under squared error, the Gaussian has the largest entropy — mass spread as diffusely as possible.

$$R_X(D)\;\le\;\tfrac12\log(\sigma^2/D)\quad\text{for any source with variance }\sigma^2,$$

and the Gaussian source attains equality. Non-Gaussian sources at the same variance have more structure and compress at lower rate.

Practical consequence. A code designed for Gaussians is a universal MSE compressor for any variance-bounded source.

03

Laplacian Source, $\ell_1$ Distortion

$R(D)=-\log(\lambda D)$ and the surprising atom at zero

Laplacian setup

Source: $X\sim\mathrm{Lap}(\lambda)$ with density $f_X(x)=\frac{\lambda}{2}e^{-\lambda|x|}$, so $\mathbb{E}|X|=1/\lambda$.

Distortion: absolute error $d_1(x,\hat x)=|x-\hat x|$. Then

$$R(D)=\begin{cases}-\log(\lambda D),& 0<D\le 1/\lambda,\\ 0,& D\ge 1/\lambda.\end{cases}$$

This will turn out to be central for neural-network pruning.

Converse — introduce an auxiliary density

Let $\widehat X$ be any reconstruction with $\mathbb{E}|X-\widehat X|\le D$. Define the auxiliary conditional

$$q_{X\mid\widehat X}(x\mid\hat x)\;\coloneqq\;\frac{1}{2D}\exp\!\left(-\frac{|x-\hat x|}{D}\right)\quad\text{(Lap}(1/D)\text{ centered at }\hat x).$$

Trick: KL divergence is non-negative, so

$$I(X;\widehat X)\;=\;\mathbb{E}\log\frac{p_{X\mid\widehat X}(X\mid\widehat X)}{p_X(X)}\;\ge\;\mathbb{E}\log\frac{q_{X\mid\widehat X}(X\mid\widehat X)}{p_X(X)}.$$

Converse — the expectation collapses to $-\log(\lambda D)$

Computation of the bound

Take logs of the densities and substitute:

$$\mathbb{E}\log\frac{(2D)^{-1}e^{-|X-\widehat X|/D}}{(\lambda/2)e^{-\lambda|X|}}\;=\;-\log(\lambda D)-\tfrac{1}{D}\,\mathbb{E}|X-\widehat X|+\lambda\,\mathbb{E}|X|.$$

Now $\mathbb{E}|X|=1/\lambda$ and $\mathbb{E}|X-\widehat X|\le D$, so the last two terms add up to at most $\lambda\cdot\frac{1}{\lambda}-\frac{1}{D}\cdot D+\bigl(\frac{D-\mathbb{E}|X-\widehat X|}{D}\bigr)\ge 0$:

$$I(X;\widehat X)\;\ge\;-\log(\lambda D)-1+1\;=\;-\log(\lambda D).\;\blacksquare$$

Achievability — the optimal reproduction is a mixture

Construct a test channel $X=V+N$ with independent

$$N\sim\mathrm{Lap}(1/D),\qquad V\sim P_V\;\coloneqq\;\lambda^2D^2\,\delta_0\;+\;(1-\lambda^2D^2)\,\mathrm{Lap}(\lambda).$$

$V$ is a mixture: with probability $\lambda^2D^2$ it equals zero exactly; otherwise it is Laplacian.

Set $\widehat X=V$. Then $\mathbb{E}|X-\widehat X|=\mathbb{E}|N|=D$ — the distortion constraint is met with equality.

Achievability — characteristic-function check

Verify $X=V+N\sim\mathrm{Lap}(\lambda)$

Characteristic functions: $\phi_N(t)=\frac{1}{1+D^2t^2}$, $\phi_X(t)=\frac{\lambda^2}{\lambda^2+t^2}$. The mixture's char. fn.:

$$\phi_V(t)=\lambda^2D^2+(1-\lambda^2D^2)\dfrac{\lambda^2}{\lambda^2+t^2},\qquad \phi_V(t)\,\phi_N(t)=\phi_X(t).$$

(Direct algebra; cross-multiply and simplify.) So $X=V+N$ is correctly distributed. By the converse computation, $I(X;V)=-\log(\lambda D)$, with equality. $\;\blacksquare$

04

Pruning From Rate–Distortion

Why optimal NN compression is naturally sparse

The optimal reproduction has an atom at zero

For the Laplacian source under $\ell_1$ distortion, the optimal reproduction marginal is

$$P_{\widehat X}\;=\;\underbrace{\lambda^2D^2}_{\text{prob. of exact }0}\,\delta_0\;+\;(1-\lambda^2D^2)\,\mathrm{Lap}(\lambda).$$

For $0<D\le 1/\lambda$, the atom weight $\lambda^2D^2$ is in $[0,1]$.

At the rate–distortion limit, a positive fraction of reconstructed coordinates are exactly zero.

One-sided version: exponential magnitudes

For $U=|X|\sim\mathrm{Exp}(\lambda)$ with one-sided distortion $d(u,v)=u-v$ if $u\ge v$, else $\infty$:

$$R(D)=-\log(\lambda D),\qquad P_{\widehat U}=\lambda D\,\delta_0+(1-\lambda D)\,\mathrm{Exp}(\lambda).$$

Same qualitative result — sparsity in the optimal reproduction. Useful in practice: protect the sign bits, compress the magnitudes via this scheme.

Compressing neural networks — setup

Network $f(x;w)=W^{(d)}\sigma(W^{(d-1)}\sigma(\cdots\sigma(W^{(1)}x)\cdots))$ with 1-Lipschitz $\sigma$, $\sigma(0)=0$ (e.g. ReLU).

Compress $W^{(\ell)}\to\widehat W^{(\ell)}$ with $\|\widehat W^{(\ell)}\|_1\le\|W^{(\ell)}\|_1$. Question: how does weight distortion control output distortion?

Weight distortion controls output distortion

Theorem
$$\sup_{\|x\|_1\le 1}\|f(x;w)-f(x;\widehat w)\|_1\;\le\;\Bigl(\prod_{k=1}^{d}\|W^{(k)}\|_1\Bigr)\,\sum_{\ell=1}^{d}\frac{\|W^{(\ell)}-\widehat W^{(\ell)}\|_1}{\|W^{(\ell)}\|_1}.$$

Proof idea. Telescope layer by layer:

$$\|W^{(d)}\sigma(h)-\widehat W^{(d)}\sigma(\widehat h)\|_1\le\|(W^{(d)}-\widehat W^{(d)})\sigma(h)\|_1+\|\widehat W^{(d)}(\sigma(h)-\sigma(\widehat h))\|_1.$$

First term controlled by $\|W^{(d)}-\widehat W^{(d)}\|_1$; second propagates to layer $d-1$ with multiplier $\|\widehat W^{(d)}\|_1\le\|W^{(d)}\|_1$. Iterate. $\;\blacksquare$

The induced distortion metric

Layer-normalized $\ell_1$ weight distortion is the natural per-layer metric:

$$\sum_{\ell=1}^{d}\frac{\|W^{(\ell)}-\widehat W^{(\ell)}\|_1}{\|W^{(\ell)}\|_1}.$$

Per layer, this reduces to the scalar problem with $u=(u_1,\ldots,u_n)$ being the normalized weights and

$$d(u,\widehat u)=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n|u_i-\widehat u_i|.$$

Squarely the Laplacian / $\ell_1$ rate–distortion problem from Part 03.

Pruning is information-theoretically optimal

Empirically, normalized pretrained NN weights look approximately Laplacian. Combine:

  • Output distortion $\le$ normalized $\ell_1$ weight distortion (theorem above).
  • Weights $\approx$ Laplacian.
  • Optimal reconstruction has a Dirac atom at zero.

For Laplacian-like weights under $\ell_1$ distortion, pruning is not a heuristic — it is part of the rate–distortion optimum itself.

Extreme-Value Theory and CROM

Rateless lossy compression by communicating residual extremes

Maximum of i.i.d. exponentials

Let $Y_1,\ldots,Y_n\stackrel{\text{i.i.d.}}{\sim}\mathrm{Exp}(\lambda)$ and $M_n=\max_i Y_i$. Then

$$M_n\;=\;\frac{\log n}{\lambda}+O_p(1),$$

and after centering by $\frac{1}{\lambda}\log n$, the fluctuations converge to a Gumbel distribution.

Rule of thumb for Gaussians: $\max_i X_i=\sigma\sqrt{2\log n}+O_p(1)$.

Take-away: the value of an extreme is almost predictable from $n$ alone — only its index needs to be sent.

CROM — zero-rate compression by the maximum

For $X^n\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I_n)$, $\max_i X_i\approx\sqrt{2\log n}$. Encoder sends only the index $m=\arg\max_i X_i$.

$$\text{rate}=\frac{\log n}{n}\to 0\text{ nats/symbol as }n\to\infty.$$

Decoder reconstructs:

$$\widehat X_i=\begin{cases}\alpha_n,& i=m,\\ -\alpha_n/(n-1),& i\ne m,\end{cases}\qquad \alpha_n\approx\sqrt{2\log n}.$$

The negative offset keeps the reconstruction roughly mean-zero.

CROM achieves the right zero-rate slope

Distortion reduction calculation

With the spike at coord $m$ removing $\alpha_n^2\approx 2\log n$ of squared error from the source, the per-coordinate distortion drops by $2\log n/n=2R_n$, so

$$D_{\text{after}}=\sigma^2-2R_n+o(R_n),\qquad D_G(R)=\sigma^2 e^{-2R}=\sigma^2-2R\sigma^2+o(R).$$

For unit-variance Gaussians, both expansions match at leading order (slope $-2$). The zero-rate descent is information-theoretically optimal. $\;\blacksquare$

CROM — why a rotation between iterations

After one sparse subtraction, the residual $X^n-\widehat X^n$ is no longer i.i.d. Gaussian. The largest coordinate has been partially removed and dependencies have been introduced.

Multiplying by a random orthogonal matrix $A$ uniformizes a fixed vector on its sphere of constant norm. In high dimension, that distribution has nearly i.i.d. Gaussian coordinates with variance $\|r^n\|_2^2/n$.

Rotation "Gaussianizes" the residual, restoring the extreme-value behavior we need.

Iterative CROM

At iteration $t$, given residual $X^{(t)}$:

  1. Find the indices $m^{(t)}$ of the $k_n$ largest coordinates.
  2. Encode only this index set ($\log\binom{n}{k_n}$ nats).
  3. Subtract a sparse vector $\alpha_t U^{(t)}$ supported on $m^{(t)}$.
  4. Rotate: $X^{(t+1)}=A_{t+1}\bigl(X^{(t)}-\alpha_t U^{(t)}\bigr)$.

Decoder undoes the rotations and sums sparse refinements:

$$\widehat X^{(L)}=\sum_{t=1}^{L}\alpha_t\,A_1^\top\!\cdots A_t^\top U^{(t)}.$$

CROM — ratelessness

Decoder can stop after any prefix of the message stream. Every prefix yields a reconstruction lying near the Gaussian distortion–rate curve $D_G(R)=\sigma^2 e^{-2R}$.

Encoder need not know the final rate in advance; the bitstream is progressively useful.

Analogous to rateless channel coding, except the decoder needs the message prefix, not an arbitrary subset.

Successive Refinement for Pruning (SuRP)

Initialize $U^{(1)}=u$, $\widehat U=0$. At iteration $t$ (Laplacian symmetric version), find indices with large positive and negative residual:

$$U_i^{(t)}\ge\tfrac{1}{\lambda_t}\log\dfrac{n}{2\beta},\qquad U_j^{(t)}\le-\tfrac{1}{\lambda_t}\log\dfrac{n}{2\beta}.$$

Send the pair $(i,j)$. Decoder adds $\pm\frac{1}{\lambda_t}\log\frac{n}{2\beta}$ at those positions; encoder subtracts. Update $\lambda_{t+1}$ for the new residual. Reconstruction starts at zero and grows sparse.

SuRP zero-rate optimality

Each iteration costs $\sim\frac{\log n}{n}\to 0$ nats per coordinate. For Laplacian sources under $\ell_1$ distortion,

$$D(R)=\frac{1}{\lambda}e^{-R},\qquad D'(0)=-\frac{1}{\lambda}.$$

One SuRP refinement reduces distortion at this optimal first-order slope. Repeated refinements build a sparse reconstruction progressively, matching the rate–distortion bound.

Recap of Lecture 2

  • Gaussian: $R(D)=\tfrac12\log(\sigma^2/D)$ via backward channel + entropy converse.
  • Shannon lower bound: $R_X(D)\ge h(X)-\tfrac{d}{2}\log(2\pi eD)$ for any source.
  • Gaussian = worst case at fixed variance under squared error.
  • Laplacian: $R(D)=-\log(\lambda D)$. Optimal reproduction has an atom at zero.
  • Pruning: Laplacian-like NN weights $+$ $\ell_1$ distortion $\Rightarrow$ sparsity is information-theoretically optimal.
  • EVT & CROM: sparse-extreme communication after random rotation matches Gaussian distortion–rate curve.
