Why this particular variance schedule

The choice $q(x_t\mid x_{t-1})=\mathcal{N}(\sqrt{\alpha_t}x_{t-1},\beta_t I)$ with $\alpha_t=1-\beta_t$ is called variance-preserving diffusion (Song & Ermon, Ho et al., DDPM). The contraction factor $\sqrt{\alpha_t}=\sqrt{1-\beta_t}$ ensures $\operatorname{Var}(X_t)=\operatorname{Var}(X_{t-1})$ when $X_{t-1}$ has unit marginal variance. Iterating, $\operatorname{Var}(X_t)$ stays bounded as $t$ grows.

Alternative: variance-exploding (VE) diffusion with $X_t=X_{t-1}+\sigma_t\eta$, no contraction. Used in NCSN-style models. Forward variance grows unboundedly. Both VP and VE work; VP is cleaner for ELBO derivations because the marginal variance stays in a small range.

Detailed expansion of the diffusion ELBO

Starting from $\log p_\theta(x_0)\ge\mathbb{E}_{q(x_{1:T}\mid x_0)}[\log p_\theta(x_{0:T})-\log q(x_{1:T}\mid x_0)]$, expand both products:

\[ \log p_\theta(x_{0:T})-\log q(x_{1:T}\mid x_0) =\log p(x_T)+\sum_{t=1}^T\log p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)-\sum_{t=1}^T\log q(x_t\mid x_{t-1}). \]

Use Bayes on the forward chain: $q(x_t\mid x_{t-1})=q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0)q(x_t\mid x_0)/q(x_{t-1}\mid x_0)$. Substitute, telescope, and rearrange. The final form (up to constants):

\[ -\log p_\theta(x_0)\le \underbrace{D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q(x_T|x_0)\Vert p(x_T))}_{L_T}+\sum_{t=2}^T \underbrace{\mathbb{E}_q D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q(x_{t-1}|x_t,x_0)\Vert p_\theta(x_{t-1}|x_t))}_{L_{t-1}}\underbrace{-\mathbb{E}_{q(x_1|x_0)}\log p_\theta(x_0|x_1)}_{L_0}. \]

Each $L_t$ is a per-step contribution. Training a single network $\mu_\theta(x_t,t)$ over all $t$ shares parameters across the chain.

Why the true reverse $q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0)$ is Gaussian

This is special to linear-Gaussian forward processes. In general, $p(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)$ (without conditioning on $x_0$) is not Gaussian for arbitrary data. But conditioning on $x_0$ makes both factors $q(x_t\mid x_{t-1})$ and $q(x_{t-1}\mid x_0)$ Gaussian in $x_{t-1}$, and the product of two Gaussians is Gaussian. The forward process being linear is what gives us a closed form.

For non-linear forward processes (e.g. discrete diffusion), the analogous derivation yields a categorical or other simple form, but the closed-form reverse conditional remains the centerpiece of the analysis.

Why $L_T$ vanishes asymptotically

$q(x_T\mid x_0)=\mathcal{N}(\sqrt{\bar\alpha_T}x_0,(1-\bar\alpha_T)I)$. As $T\to\infty$ with $\bar\alpha_T\to 0$, this converges in distribution to $\mathcal{N}(0,I)=p(x_T)$, and the KL goes to zero. In practice: pick the schedule so $\bar\alpha_T$ is very small (e.g. $\sim 10^{-5}$).

Since $L_T$ is constant in $\theta$, it cannot be optimized. It contributes a fixed offset to the bound; we ignore it during training.

Treatment of $L_0$ in practice

$L_0=-\mathbb{E}_{q(x_1\mid x_0)}\log p_\theta(x_0\mid x_1)$ is the final-step decoder likelihood. For continuous data, often replaced by a Gaussian likelihood with unit variance. For pixel data with discrete intensities $\{0,1,\ldots,255\}/255$, Ho et al. (2020) use a discretized Gaussian decoder that integrates the Gaussian density over each pixel-value bin.

$L_0$ is typically small relative to the sum $\sum L_t$ for large $T$, so a simple approximation is acceptable.

Numerical sanity check on the closed form

Take the simplest case: $T=2$, $\beta_1=\beta_2=\beta$, $\alpha=1-\beta$, $\bar\alpha_1=\alpha$, $\bar\alpha_2=\alpha^2$.

$\widetilde\beta_2=\frac{1-\bar\alpha_1}{1-\bar\alpha_2}\beta_2=\frac{1-\alpha}{1-\alpha^2}\beta=\frac{\beta}{1+\alpha}$. Sanity: at $\alpha\to 1$ (no noise added), $\widetilde\beta_2\to\beta/2$, smaller than $\beta$ — as expected, conditioning on $x_0$ tightens the variance.

Mean coefficient on $x_0$: $\frac{\sqrt{\bar\alpha_1}\beta_2}{1-\bar\alpha_2}=\frac{\sqrt{\alpha}\beta}{1-\alpha^2}=\frac{\sqrt\alpha}{1+\alpha}$. Sums to 1 with the coefficient on $x_t$: $\frac{\sqrt{\alpha}(1-\bar\alpha_1)}{1-\bar\alpha_2}=\frac{\sqrt\alpha(1-\alpha)}{1-\alpha^2}=\frac{\sqrt\alpha}{1+\alpha}$. They're equal in this case (symmetry from $T=2$); in general they differ.

Connection to information-theoretic rate–distortion

Each forward step is a noisy channel from $X_{t-1}$ to $X_t$. Mutual information $I(X_0;X_t)$ is monotone non-increasing in $t$ (data processing). The reverse process learns to invert these channels approximately.

This connects to successive refinement from rate–distortion theory: a single source described at multiple distortion levels, with each level a coarsening of the previous. Diffusion's reverse chain is essentially a learned successive-refinement decoder.
