01

Master Identity

One linear relation drives every parameterization

Three Variables, Two Free

From Lecture 2:

$$x_t \;=\; \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0 \;+\; \sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\varepsilon, \qquad \varepsilon\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I).$$

Knowing any two of $(x_t, x_0, \varepsilon)$ recovers the third:

$$x_0 \;=\; \frac{x_t - \sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\varepsilon}{\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}}, \qquad \varepsilon \;=\; \frac{x_t - \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0}{\sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}}.$$
Predicting $x_0$ and predicting $\varepsilon$ are the same network up to a $t$-dependent affine map.
02

Three Parameterizations

Same optimum, different conditioning

Predict $x_0$

Train $x_{0,\theta}(x_t,t)$ against the clean target:

$$\mathcal{L}_{x_0} \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{(x_0,t,\varepsilon)}\!\bigl[\,\|x_0 - x_{0,\theta}(x_t,t)\|^2\,\bigr].$$

Bayes-optimal predictor: $x_{0,\theta}^\star(x_t,t) = \mathbb{E}[X_0\mid X_t = x_t]$ — the posterior mean.

Pro: interpretable target. Con: very noisy regression at large $t$ (input nearly noise, target clean).

Predict Noise $\varepsilon$

Train $\varepsilon_\theta(x_t,t)$ against the noise that generated $x_t$:

$$\mathcal{L}_\varepsilon \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{(x_0,t,\varepsilon)}\!\bigl[\,\|\varepsilon - \varepsilon_\theta(x_t,t)\|^2\,\bigr].$$

Equivalent to $\mathcal{L}_{x_0}$ up to a $t$-dependent rescaling.

Unit-variance target at every $t$. Well-conditioned gradients across noise levels: the DDPM default.
Ho, Jain, Abbeel, "Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models", NeurIPS 2020.

Predict the Score

Marginal at noise level $t$: $p_t(x) = q(x_t = x)$. Score:

$$\nabla_x\log p_t(x).$$

Train $s_\theta(x_t, t) \approx \nabla_{x_t}\log p_t(x_t)$.

Score determines the reverse-time SDE drift. Bridge to $\varepsilon_\theta$: Tweedie.
Song, Ermon, "Generative Modeling by Estimating Gradients of the Data Distribution", NeurIPS 2019.
03

Tweedie's Bridge

Posterior mean of clean data = rescaled score

Tweedie's Formula

Theorem (Robbins, 1956 / Tweedie).

$X_t = \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}X_0 + \sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\varepsilon$:

$$\mathbb{E}[X_0\mid X_t=x_t] \;=\; \frac{1}{\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}}\bigl(x_t + (1-\bar\alpha_t)\,\nabla_{x_t}\log p_t(x_t)\bigr).$$

Posterior mean of clean $X_0$ = noisy $x_t$ + score correction, all rescaled.

Robbins, "An Empirical Bayes Approach to Statistics", Berkeley Symp. 1956.

Proof — Differentiate the Marginal

Marginal as a Gaussian mixture in $X_0$:

$$p_t(x_t) \;=\; \int p_0(x_0)\,\mathcal{N}\!\bigl(x_t;\,\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}x_0,\,(1-\bar\alpha_t)I\bigr)\,dx_0.$$

Differentiate $\log p_t$. Inside the integral, the Gaussian score in $x_t$ is:

$$-\,\dfrac{x_t - \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0}{1-\bar\alpha_t}.$$

Pulling the differentiation through gives an expectation over the posterior:

$$\nabla_{x_t}\log p_t(x_t) \;=\; -\,\dfrac{x_t - \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,\mathbb{E}[X_0\mid x_t]}{1-\bar\alpha_t}.$$

Solve for $\mathbb{E}[X_0\mid x_t]$ to recover the formula.

Three Names, One Object

Combine Tweedie with the master identity:

$$\mathbb{E}[\varepsilon\mid x_t] \;=\; \dfrac{x_t - \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,\mathbb{E}[X_0\mid x_t]}{\sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}} \;=\; -\sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\nabla_{x_t}\log p_t(x_t).$$
Optimal $x_0$-predictor, $\varepsilon$-predictor, and score are three rescalings of the same function.

Pick the one that conditions the loss best at your noise level.

04

Channel View

Forward as noise channel, reverse as decoding

Each Step Is an Additive Gaussian Channel

$$X_t \;=\; \sqrt{\alpha_t}\,X_{t-1} + \sqrt{\beta_t}\,\eta_t, \qquad \eta_t\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I).$$

Data-processing inequality:

$$I(X_0;X_T) \;\le\; \cdots \;\le\; I(X_0;X_1) \;\le\; H(X_0).$$

As $\bar\alpha_T\to 0$, $I(X_0;X_T)\to 0$. Information is fully erased.

Reverse = Approximate Decoding

The optimal decoder for the step $X_{t-1}\to X_t$ is the true posterior $p(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)$.

The learned $p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)$ is an approximation. Per-step KL gap accumulates over $T$ steps as ELBO slack.

Diffusion training is universal channel decoding at $T$ noise levels with shared parameters.

Successive Refinement Analogy

Rate–distortion successive refinement: nested codebooks describe a source at decreasing distortion.

Diffusion matches this structure layer by layer:

  • $X_T$: coarsest description (pure noise).
  • $X_{T-1}, \ldots, X_1$: progressively finer.
  • $X_0$: finest reconstruction.

Shared $\theta$ across $t$ amortizes one network across all refinement levels.

Recap

  • Master identity: $x_t = \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}x_0 + \sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\varepsilon$.
  • Three parameterizations: $x_0$, $\varepsilon$, score; same optimum.
  • Tweedie: ties posterior mean to the score by an affine rescaling.
  • $\varepsilon$-prediction: unit-variance target, well-conditioned, DDPM default.
  • Channel view: forward = noisy channel; reverse = decoding; chain = successive refinement.
