01

Forward Chain

Frozen encoder turning data into Gaussian noise

Setup: Variance-Preserving Steps

Schedule $\{\beta_t\}_{t=1}^T \subset (0,1)$. Set $\alpha_t = 1-\beta_t$, $\bar\alpha_t = \prod_{s\le t}\alpha_s$.

$$q(x_t\mid x_{t-1}) \;=\; \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(\sqrt{\alpha_t}\,x_{t-1},\; \beta_t I\bigr).$$

Each step contracts toward 0 and adds Gaussian noise.

Variance-preserving: unit variance in, unit variance out.

Closed Form: $q(x_t\mid x_0)$

Lemma.

$$q(x_t\mid x_0) \;=\; \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0,\;(1-\bar\alpha_t)I\bigr).$$

Induct: mean $\sqrt{\alpha_t\bar\alpha_{t-1}}x_0 = \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0$; variance $\alpha_t(1-\bar\alpha_{t-1})+\beta_t = 1-\bar\alpha_t$.

Master Reparameterization

For $\varepsilon\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I)$:

$$x_t \;=\; \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0 + \sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\varepsilon.$$

Jump from $(x_0, t)$ straight to $x_t$: no chain simulation in training.

As $t\to T$, $\bar\alpha_T\to 0$, so $x_T\approx\mathcal{N}(0,I)$.

02

Reverse Chain

Learned Gaussian kernels run the chain backwards

Generative Model

Start from prior $p(x_T)=\mathcal{N}(0,I)$, then reverse step by step:

$$p_\theta(x_{0:T}) \;=\; p(x_T)\prod_{t=1}^T p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t), \qquad p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t) = \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(\mu_\theta(x_t,t),\,\Sigma_t\bigr).$$

Sampling: draw $x_T$, then $x_{T-1},\ldots,x_0$ via the learned kernels.

Diffusion Is a Hierarchical VAE

  • Data $x_0$; latents $(x_1,\ldots,x_T)$.
  • Encoder $q(x_{1:T}\mid x_0)$: fixed, not learned.
  • Decoder $p_\theta(x_{0:T})$: learned Gaussian kernels.
  • Prior $p(x_T)=\mathcal{N}(0,I)$ over the deepest latent.
Frozen encoder: posterior collapse impossible.
03

True Reverse Conditional

Two Gaussians multiply; the square completes

Goal and Bayes

Want $q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t, x_0)$, the law of $x_{t-1}$ given both endpoints.

$$q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t, x_0) \;\propto\; \underbrace{q(x_t\mid x_{t-1})}_{\text{forward step}}\cdot\underbrace{q(x_{t-1}\mid x_0)}_{\text{closed form}}.$$

Both factors are Gaussian in $x_{t-1}$, so the product is Gaussian.

Complete the Square — Variance

Collect coefficients of $x_{t-1}^2$ in the exponent:

$$\frac{\alpha_t}{\beta_t} \;+\; \frac{1}{1-\bar\alpha_{t-1}} \;=\; \frac{1-\bar\alpha_t}{\beta_t(1-\bar\alpha_{t-1})}.$$

Posterior variance is the reciprocal:

$$\tilde\beta_t \;=\; \dfrac{1-\bar\alpha_{t-1}}{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\beta_t.$$

Complete the Square — Mean

Collect linear coefficient; divide by $1/\tilde\beta_t$:

$$\mu_q(x_t, x_0) \;=\; \frac{\sqrt{\bar\alpha_{t-1}}\,\beta_t}{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0 \;+\; \frac{\sqrt{\alpha_t}(1-\bar\alpha_{t-1})}{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,x_t.$$

Closed form.

$$q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t, x_0) \;=\; \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(\mu_q(x_t,x_0),\,\tilde\beta_t I\bigr).$$
04

ELBO Decomposition

$L_T + \sum L_t + L_0$ — what the model is asked to do

Apply Lecture 1 to the Hierarchy

Plug $p_\theta(x_{0:T})$ and $q(x_{1:T}\mid x_0)$ into the ELBO:

$$\log p_\theta(x_0) \;\ge\; \mathbb{E}_{q}\!\bigl[\log p_\theta(x_{0:T}) - \log q(x_{1:T}\mid x_0)\bigr].$$

Telescope and collect by index:

$$-\log p_\theta(x_0) \;\le\; \underbrace{D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q(x_T\mid x_0)\,\Vert\,p(x_T))}_{L_T} + \sum_{t=2}^{T} L_{t-1} + \underbrace{-\log p_\theta(x_0\mid x_1)}_{L_0}.$$

Boundary Terms: $L_T$ and $L_0$

$L_T$: prior matching

$D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(q(x_T\mid x_0)\,\Vert\,\mathcal{N}(0,I)\bigr)$.

Constant in $\theta$. Vanishes as $\bar\alpha_T\to 0$.

$L_0$: final reconstruction

$-\mathbb{E}_{q(x_1\mid x_0)}[\log p_\theta(x_0\mid x_1)]$.

Negligible for discrete pixel models; absorbed in practice.

Training signal lives in the interior terms.

Interior Term $L_t$ Is the Whole Game

For $t = 1,\ldots,T-1$:

$$L_t \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{q(x_t\mid x_0)}\!\bigl[D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t, x_0)\,\Vert\,p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)\bigr)\bigr].$$

Both arguments Gaussian; numerator known in closed form.

$L_t$ Becomes MSE on the Mean

Pin both covariances to $\tilde\beta_t I$. KL between same-covariance Gaussians:

$$D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(\mathcal{N}(\mu_q,\Sigma)\,\Vert\,\mathcal{N}(\mu_\theta,\Sigma)\bigr) \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}(\mu_q - \mu_\theta)^\top\Sigma^{-1}(\mu_q - \mu_\theta).$$
Training reduces to: predict the posterior mean $\mu_q(x_t, x_0)$ from $(x_t, t)$.

Three Ways to Parameterize $\mu_\theta$

  • Predict $x_0$: interpretable target.
  • Predict noise $\varepsilon$: DDPM default; well-conditioned across $t$.
  • Predict score $\nabla_x\log p_t(x)$: SDE perspective.
Lecture 3: derive the equivalences via Tweedie.

Recap

  • Forward: $q(x_t\mid x_0)=\mathcal{N}(\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}x_0,(1-\bar\alpha_t)I)$.
  • Reverse: learned Gaussian kernels $p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)$.
  • Hierarchical VAE: fixed encoder, deepest latent matched to $\mathcal{N}(0,I)$.
  • True reverse: Gaussian, $\mathcal{N}(\mu_q, \tilde\beta_t I)$, closed form.
  • ELBO: $L_T + \sum L_t + L_0$; interior terms are MSE on the mean.
