01

Motivation and Setup

Density ratios, samples-only access, the central problem

MI as Dependence Measure

Mutual information quantifies statistical dependence:

$$I(X;Y) = D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(p_{X,Y}\,\Vert\,p_X p_Y\bigr) = \mathbb{E}\!\left[\log\frac{p(X,Y)}{p(X)p(Y)}\right].$$

Key applications:

  • Representation learning: maximize $I(T;Y)$
  • Contrastive learning: maximize $I(T_1;T_2)$ across views
  • Information bottleneck: minimize $I(T;X)$ at fixed fidelity

Samples-Only Setting

Given i.i.d. samples $(x_i,y_i)\sim p_{X,Y}$, $i=1,\ldots,N$.

Shuffling pairs $(x_i,y_j)$ with $j\ne i$ gives empirical access to the product marginal. So we have samples from:

$$P = p_{X,Y}\quad\text{(joint)},\qquad Q = p_X p_Y\quad\text{(product of marginals)}.$$

$I(X;Y) = D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\,\Vert\,Q)$. Estimating KL from samples only.

Density-Ratio Framing

Define the density ratio:

$$r(x,y) \coloneqq \frac{p(x,y)}{p(x)p(y)},\qquad I(X;Y) = \mathbb{E}_{p_{X,Y}}[\log r(X,Y)].$$

If $r$ were known, MI is a single expectation under $p_{X,Y}$: estimable from samples.

Strategy: learn critic $T_\theta \approx \log r$ by maximizing a variational lower bound.

02

Barber–Agakov Bound

Variational lower bound via a conditional model $q(x\mid y)$

Barber–Agakov Bound

Theorem (Barber & Agakov, 2003).

For any conditional density $q(x\mid y)$ on the same alphabet:

$$I(X;Y) \ge H(X) + \mathbb{E}_{p(x,y)}[\log q(x\mid y)] \eqqcolon I_{\mathrm{BA}}(q).$$

Tight when $q(x\mid y) = p(x\mid y)$.

Barber & Agakov, "The IM Algorithm: A Variational Approach to Information Maximization", NeurIPS 2003.

BA Proof (1 of 2)

Start from $I(X;Y) = H(X) - H(X\mid Y)$ and expand the conditional entropy:

$$H(X\mid Y) = -\mathbb{E}_{p(x,y)}[\log p(x\mid y)].$$

Add and subtract $\log q(x\mid y)$:

$$H(X\mid Y) = -\mathbb{E}_{p(x,y)}[\log q(x\mid y)] - \mathbb{E}_{p(y)}\!\bigl[D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p(\cdot\mid y)\,\Vert\,q(\cdot\mid y))\bigr].$$

BA Proof (2 of 2)

Substitute back into $I(X;Y) = H(X) - H(X\mid Y)$:

$$I(X;Y) = H(X) + \mathbb{E}_{p(x,y)}[\log q(x\mid y)] + \mathbb{E}_{p(y)}D_{\mathrm{KL}}(\cdot).$$

Since $D_{\mathrm{KL}} \ge 0$:

$$I(X;Y) \ge H(X) + \mathbb{E}_{p(x,y)}[\log q(x\mid y)]. \quad\blacksquare$$

Gap is $\mathbb{E}_{p(y)}D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p(\cdot\mid y)\,\Vert\,q(\cdot\mid y)) \ge 0$. Closes when $q = p(\cdot\mid y)$.

BA in Practice

$H(X)$ is independent of $q$: a constant. So optimizing over $q$:

$$\max_q\; I_{\mathrm{BA}}(q) = H(X) + \max_q\;\mathbb{E}_{p(x,y)}[\log q_\theta(x\mid y)].$$

The inner term is just maximum likelihood of $X\mid Y$.

Train representations

Maximize $\mathbb{E}\log q_\theta(x\mid y)$: no MI machinery needed.

Report MI numbers

Also need $H(X)$, typically unknown. BA is not a plug-in estimator.

03

Donsker–Varadhan Bound

Dual KL representation, basis of MINE

Donsker–Varadhan Representation

Theorem (Donsker & Varadhan, 1983).

For any distributions $P, Q$ on a common space:

$$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\,\Vert\,Q) = \sup_T\;\bigl\{\,\mathbb{E}_P[T] - \log\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T]\,\bigr\}.$$

Supremum over measurable $T$ with finite expectations.

Apply with $P = p_{X,Y}$, $Q = p_X p_Y$: lower bound $I_{\mathrm{DV}}(T)$.

DV Bound on MI

Substituting $P = p_{X,Y}$, $Q = p_X p_Y$:

$$I(X;Y) \ge \mathbb{E}_P[T] - \log\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T] \eqqcolon I_{\mathrm{DV}}(T).$$

Tight at $T^\star(x,y) = \log r(x,y) + c$ for any constant $c$.

Learning the critic by maximizing $I_{\mathrm{DV}}$ recovers $\log r$ up to a constant.

DV Proof (1 of 2)

For any $T$ with $\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T] < \infty$, define a tilted distribution $G$ via:

$$\frac{dG}{dQ} = \frac{e^T}{\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T]}.$$

Compute $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\,\Vert\,G)$ by expanding the Radon-Nikodym log:

$$\begin{aligned}D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\,\Vert\,G) &= \mathbb{E}_P\!\left[\log\frac{dP/dQ}{e^T/\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T]}\right] \\ &= D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\,\Vert\,Q) - \mathbb{E}_P[T] + \log\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T].\end{aligned}$$

DV Proof (2 of 2)

Since $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\,\Vert\,G) \ge 0$:

$$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\,\Vert\,Q) \ge \mathbb{E}_P[T] - \log\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T]. \quad\blacksquare$$

Equality holds iff $P = G$, i.e., $T = \log(dP/dQ) + c$. For $P = p_{X,Y}$, $Q = p_X p_Y$:

$$T^\star(x,y) = \log r(x,y) + c.$$

Optimal critic is the log-density-ratio. Tightest possible bound when fully optimized.

MINE: Neural DV Estimator

Parameterize $T_\theta(x,y)$ by a neural network; approximate expectations by minibatches:

  • Positives: $(x_i, y_i) \sim p_{X,Y}$
  • Negatives: shuffle indices, $(x_i, y_j)$ with $j \ne i$

Gradient-ascend $I_{\mathrm{DV}}(T_\theta)$ over $\theta$.

Catch: $\log\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T]$ is dominated by the largest $T$ value. Variance grows exponentially in MI.

Belghazi et al., "MINE: Mutual Information Neural Estimation", ICML 2018.
04

NWJ Bound

Replace the log-partition with a linear surrogate

From DV to NWJ

For $u > 0$: $\log u \le u - 1$. Apply to the DV log-partition with $u = \mathbb{E}_Q[e^T]$:

$$\log\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T] \le \mathbb{E}_Q[e^T] - 1.$$

Substitute into $I_{\mathrm{DV}}$:

$$I_{\mathrm{DV}}(T) = \mathbb{E}_P[T] - \log\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T] \ge \mathbb{E}_P[T] - \mathbb{E}_Q[e^T] + 1.$$

Reparameterize $T \to T + 1$ to get the cleaner NWJ form.

NWJ Bound

Theorem (Nguyen, Wainwright & Jordan, 2010).

For any critic $T$:

$$I(X;Y) \ge \mathbb{E}_P[T] - \mathbb{E}_Q[e^{T-1}] \eqqcolon I_{\mathrm{NWJ}}(T).$$

Tight at $T^\star(x,y) = \log r(x,y) + 1$.

Nguyen, Wainwright & Jordan, "Estimating Divergence Functionals and the Likelihood Ratio", IEEE Trans. Inf. Theory 2010.

NWJ vs. DV: Variance

DV

Log-partition $\log\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T]$ is volatile: one large $T$ value dominates. Variance $\sim e^I / N$.

NWJ

Replaces $\log\mathbb{E}[e^T]$ with tractable $\mathbb{E}[e^{T-1}]$. More stable, but generally looser at finite-sample optima.

NWJ trades tightness for variance reduction. Same optimal critic, different finite-batch behavior.

Three Bounds Compared

BoundKey property
BAOptimal at $q = p(x\mid y)$; needs $H(X)$; reduces to MLE
DVOptimal at $T = \log r + c$; tightest; variance $\sim e^I/N$
NWJOptimal at $T = \log r + 1$; stable; looser at fixed critic class

All three share the same optimal critic $\log r$; differ in finite-sample behavior.

05

Bias / Variance Tradeoffs

High-MI regimes, finite-batch effects

High-MI Barrier

For strongly dependent pairs, $\log r$ has a long right tail. McAllester & Stratos (2020):

$$\mathrm{Var}\!\left[\text{empirical }\log\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T]\right] \sim \frac{e^{I(X;Y)}}{N}.$$

To estimate $I = 10$ nats: need $N \sim e^{10} \approx 22{,}000$ samples to control variance alone.

No fully sample-efficient estimator of high MI exists. This is an information-theoretic barrier, not a software bug.

Bivariate Gaussian Benchmark

$(X,Y)$ jointly Gaussian, correlation $\rho \in (-1,1)$, unit variances. Closed-form MI:

$$I(X;Y) = -\tfrac{1}{2}\log(1-\rho^2).$$

Low MI ($|\rho| \le 0.5$)

All estimators converge with $\sim 10^3$ samples.

High MI ($|\rho| = 0.99$)

DV diverges without huge batches; NWJ stable but biased low.

Choosing Your Bound

  • Optimization only: BA reduces to MLE. No MI numbers needed.
  • Stable contrastive objective: InfoNCE (next lecture). Bounded by $\log K$, very stable.
  • Tight numerical estimate at low-to-moderate MI: NWJ or DV with careful budget.
  • High MI ($I \gg 1$ nat): no estimator is reliable. Report an interval with a clear caveat.

Recap

  • Setup: samples from $P = p_{X,Y}$ and $Q = p_X p_Y$ only
  • Density-ratio critic: $T \approx \log r$, optimized via variational bound
  • BA: $I \ge H(X) + \mathbb{E}\log q(x\mid y)$, reduces to conditional MLE
  • DV: $I \ge \mathbb{E}_P[T] - \log\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T]$, tight, high variance (MINE)
  • NWJ: $I \ge \mathbb{E}_P[T] - \mathbb{E}_Q[e^{T-1}]$, stable, slightly looser
  • High-MI barrier: variance $\sim e^I/N$; reliable only at low-to-moderate MI
