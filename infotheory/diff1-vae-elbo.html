01

Generative Modeling

Latent-variable models, MLE, intractable integral

Latent-Variable Model

Prior $p(z) = \mathcal{N}(0,I)$. Decoder $p_\theta(x\mid z)$.

$$p_\theta(x) = \int p(z)\,p_\theta(x\mid z)\,dz.$$

Sample: draw $z\sim p(z)$, then $x\sim p_\theta(x\mid z)$.

Flexible decoder makes $p_\theta(x)$ intractable to compute.

Maximum Likelihood Training

Minimize average negative log-likelihood:

$$\theta^\star \in \arg\min_\theta\;\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^N -\log p_\theta(x_i).$$

Bottleneck: $\log p_\theta(x_i) = \log\int p_\theta(x_i,z)\,dz$.

Replace intractable log-marginal with a tractable lower bound.
02

The Variational Bound

Two derivations of the same inequality

Introduce a Variational Posterior

Encoder family $q_\phi(z\mid x)$. Rewrite the marginal:

$$p_\theta(x) = \mathbb{E}_{z\sim q_\phi(\cdot\mid x)}\!\left[\frac{p_\theta(x,z)}{q_\phi(z\mid x)}\right].$$

Take $\log$ and apply Jensen or the KL identity.

ELBO: Jensen Derivation

Derivation (concavity of log)

$\log\mathbb{E}[X]\ge\mathbb{E}[\log X]$. Apply to the importance-sampling identity:

$$\log p_\theta(x) \ge \mathbb{E}_{q_\phi}\!\left[\log\frac{p_\theta(x,z)}{q_\phi(z\mid x)}\right] =: \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ELBO}}(\theta,\phi;x). \;\blacksquare$$

Equality iff $q_\phi(z\mid x) = p_\theta(z\mid x)$.

ELBO: KL Derivation

Derivation (KL non-negativity)

Bayes: $p_\theta(z\mid x) = p_\theta(x,z)/p_\theta(x)$. Rearrange:

$$\log p_\theta(x) = \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ELBO}} + D_{\mathrm{KL}}\bigl(q_\phi(\cdot\mid x)\,\Vert\,p_\theta(\cdot\mid x)\bigr). \;\blacksquare$$

$D_{\mathrm{KL}} \ge 0$ gives the bound. The KL gap is the named slack.

Two Views, One Bound

$$\log p_\theta(x) = \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ELBO}} + \underbrace{D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_\phi\Vert p_\theta(\cdot\mid x))}_{\ge\,0} \ge \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ELBO}}.$$

Jensen view

Gap comes from concavity. Gap is opaque.

KL view

Gap is $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_\phi\Vert p_\theta(\cdot\mid x))$. Closing it means $q_\phi = p_\theta(\cdot\mid x)$.

03

Reconstruction + KL Decomposition

What the bound asks the model to do

ELBO Decomposition

Algebra

Expand $p_\theta(x,z) = p(z)p_\theta(x\mid z)$:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ELBO}} = \underbrace{\mathbb{E}_{q_\phi}[\log p_\theta(x\mid z)]}_{\text{reconstruction}} - \underbrace{D_{\mathrm{KL}}\bigl(q_\phi(\cdot\mid x)\,\Vert\,p(\cdot)\bigr)}_{\text{regularizer}}. \;\blacksquare$$

Rate vs. Distortion

Reconstruction

$\mathbb{E}_{q_\phi}[\log p_\theta(x\mid z)]$. High when decoder reproduces $x$ from encoded $z$.

Negative distortion.

KL Regularizer

$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_\phi(z\mid x)\Vert p(z))$. Penalizes information leakage from $x$ into $z$.

Rate (bits about $x$ in $z$).

ELBO is a variational rate-distortion functional.
04

VAE: Reparameterization

Gaussian encoder, the trick that makes gradients flow

Gaussian Encoder

Encoder: network outputs $\mu_\phi(x)$, $\sigma_\phi(x)$:

$$q_\phi(z\mid x) = \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(\mu_\phi(x),\operatorname{diag}(\sigma_\phi(x)^2)\bigr).$$

KL closed form vs. $\mathcal{N}(0,I)$ prior:

$$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_\phi\Vert p) = \tfrac{1}{2}\sum_j\bigl(\mu_{\phi,j}^2+\sigma_{\phi,j}^2-1-\log\sigma_{\phi,j}^2\bigr).$$

The Gradient Problem

Reconstruction term:

$$\mathbb{E}_{z\sim q_\phi(z\mid x)}[\log p_\theta(x\mid z)].$$

Expectation is over $q_\phi$, which depends on $\phi$.

Cannot swap $\nabla_\phi$ and $\mathbb{E}_{q_\phi}$ directly.

Fix: reparameterize so randomness is parameter-free.

Reparameterization Trick

Write $z = \mu_\phi(x) + \sigma_\phi(x)\odot\varepsilon$, $\;\varepsilon\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I)$.

$$\mathbb{E}_{z\sim q_\phi}[f(z)] = \mathbb{E}_{\varepsilon\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I)}\!\bigl[f\!\bigl(\mu_\phi(x)+\sigma_\phi(x)\odot\varepsilon\bigr)\bigr].$$

$\varepsilon$ is parameter-free: $\nabla_\phi$ passes through $\mathbb{E}_\varepsilon$.

Why Reparameterization Works

Lemma.

Let $z = g_\phi(\varepsilon)$ push $p_\varepsilon$ to $q_\phi$. For differentiable $f$:

$$\nabla_\phi\,\mathbb{E}_{q_\phi}[f(z)] = \mathbb{E}_{\varepsilon\sim p_\varepsilon}[\nabla_\phi f(g_\phi(\varepsilon))]. \;\blacksquare$$

First equality: change of variables. Second: $\nabla_\phi$ and $\mathbb{E}_\varepsilon$ commute because $p_\varepsilon$ does not depend on $\phi$.

Single sample $\varepsilon$ gives an unbiased gradient.

VAE Training Loop

Per data point $x$:

  1. Encoder: $\mu_\phi(x)$, $\sigma_\phi(x)$
  2. Sample: $\varepsilon\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I)$, $\;z = \mu_\phi(x)+\sigma_\phi(x)\odot\varepsilon$
  3. Decoder: $p_\theta(x\mid z)$
  4. Loss: $-\log p_\theta(x\mid z) + D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_\phi\Vert p)$
  5. Backprop through both networks

Failure Mode: Posterior Collapse

If decoder is too powerful: $q_\phi(z\mid x)\to p(z)$.

$$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_\phi\Vert p)=0 \iff q_\phi(z\mid x)=p(z) \implies z\perp x.$$

Latent carries no information about $x$.

Mitigations: KL annealing, free bits, weaker decoder.

Diffusion avoids this: fixed forward process forces $z$ to carry $x$.

Recap of Lecture 1

  • Latent-variable model: $p_\theta(x)=\int p(z)p_\theta(x\mid z)dz$, intractable
  • ELBO: $\log p_\theta(x)\ge\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ELBO}}$ via Jensen or KL
  • Decomposition: reconstruction minus rate
  • VAE: Gaussian encoder, reparameterization for pathwise gradients
  • Posterior collapse: diffusion sidesteps via fixed encoder
