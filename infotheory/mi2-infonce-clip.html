01

InfoNCE

A noise-contrastive lower bound on mutual information

InfoNCE Setup

One positive pair $(x,y) \sim p_{X,Y}$ and $K-1$ negatives $\{y_k^-\} \stackrel{\mathrm{i.i.d.}}{\sim} p_Y$, independent of $x$.

Critic $f(x,y)$ assigns a score to a pair. Define:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{InfoNCE}}(f) = -\mathbb{E}\!\left[\log\frac{e^{f(x,y)}}{e^{f(x,y)} + \sum_{k=1}^{K-1}e^{f(x,y_k^-)}}\right].$$

$K$-class softmax cross-entropy: classify the true $y$ among $K$ candidates.

InfoNCE Bound

Theorem (van den Oord et al., 2018).

For any critic $f$:

$$I(X;Y) \ge \log K - \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{InfoNCE}}(f).$$

Since $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{InfoNCE}} \ge 0$, the bound is at most $\log K$.

van den Oord, Li & Vinyals, "Representation Learning with Contrastive Predictive Coding", arXiv 2018.

The $\log K$ Ceiling

Even with the optimal critic and infinite samples, the bound saturates at $\log K$ when $I(X;Y) \gg \log K$:

$$0 \le \log K - \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{InfoNCE}} \le \log K.$$

Implication for estimation

To estimate $I = 10$ nats: need $K \ge e^{10} \approx 22{,}000$ negatives.

CLIP's solution

Batch size $K = 32{,}768$. Gives $\log K \approx 10.4$ nats.

InfoNCE Proof (1 of 2)

Use the optimal critic $f^\star(x,y) = \log r(x,y) + c(x)$. Substituting:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{InfoNCE}}(f^\star) = -\mathbb{E}\log\frac{r(x,y)}{r(x,y) + \sum_{k=1}^{K-1} r(x,y_k^-)}.$$

Note that $\mathbb{E}_{p(y_k^-)}[r(x,y_k^-)] = 1$ (density ratio integrates to 1 under $Q$).

InfoNCE Proof (2 of 2)

Apply Jensen's inequality on the convex $-\log$:

$$\begin{aligned}\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{InfoNCE}}(f^\star) &\ge \log\frac{\mathbb{E}[r(x,y)] + (K-1)}{\mathbb{E}\,r(x,y)} = \log K - I(X;Y) + o(1).\end{aligned}$$

Rearranging gives $I(X;Y) \ge \log K - \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{InfoNCE}}(f^\star)$.

The same bound holds for any $f$ (since $f^\star$ is optimal). $\quad\blacksquare$

Optimal InfoNCE Critic

The infimum of $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{InfoNCE}}$ is attained at any $f^\star$ with:

$$f^\star(x,y) = \log r(x,y) + c(x) = \log\frac{p(y\mid x)}{p(y)} + c(x).$$
  • Same log-density-ratio as DV's optimal critic
  • $f^\star(x,\cdot)$: log-conditional density up to $x$-dependent constant
  • $\mathrm{softmax}(f^\star)$ approximates the conditional $p(y\mid x)$

CLIP: Contrastive Image–Language Pretraining

InfoNCE deployed at web scale

CLIP Architecture

Two-encoder design trained jointly on 400M image–text pairs from the web:

Image $x$
→
Image encoder $g_\mu$
→
$g_\mu(x) \in \mathbb{R}^d$
Text $y$
→
Text encoder $h_\phi$
→
$h_\phi(y) \in \mathbb{R}^d$
Radford et al., "Learning Transferable Visual Models From Natural Language Supervision", ICML 2021.

The Separable Critic

CLIP uses a normalized inner product as the critic:

$$f_{\mu,\phi}(x,y) = \frac{1}{\tau}\,g_\mu(x)^\top h_\phi(y),\quad \|g_\mu(x)\| = \|h_\phi(y)\| = 1.$$

Computational

$N \times N$ similarity matrix: single matrix multiply, not $N^2$ joint forward passes.

Reusability

Each encoder reusable independently after training.

Temperature $\tau$: learned scalar, initialized at $\log(1/\tau) \approx 0$, learns up to $\approx 100$.

Symmetric CLIP Loss

Minibatch of $N$ pairs $\{(x_i,y_i)\}$. Similarity matrix $S_{ij} = f(x_i,y_j)$.

$$\mathcal{L}_{x\to y} = -\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i}\log\frac{e^{S_{ii}}}{\sum_{j}e^{S_{ij}}},\quad \mathcal{L}_{y\to x} = -\frac{1}{N}\sum_{j}\log\frac{e^{S_{jj}}}{\sum_{i}e^{S_{ij}}}.$$
$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{CLIP}} = \tfrac{1}{2}(\mathcal{L}_{x\to y} + \mathcal{L}_{y\to x}).$$

$N \times N$ Similarity Matrix

Diagonal: true (image, text) pairs. Should rank highest in their row and column.

Off-diagonal: contrastive negatives. Random shufflings within the batch.

Total: $N$ row-wise + $N$ column-wise classifications, optimized simultaneously.

image $i$ vs. text $j$

03

Zero-Shot Classification

Reusing the contrastive head, no task-specific training

CLIP as a Classifier

For a $K$-way classification task with labels $\{c_1,\ldots,c_K\}$:

  1. Build prompts: $y_k =$ "A photo of a $\{c_k\}$." for each label.
  2. Embed each prompt: $h_k = h_\phi(y_k)$.
  3. Embed the test image: $g = g_\mu(x)$.
  4. Predict $\hat c = \arg\max_k\; g^\top h_k$.

No retraining. No task-specific labels at training time. Classifier built from text alone.

Why Zero-Shot Works

InfoNCE training makes $g^\top h$ approximate $\log r(x,y) + c(x)$. For a label class:

$$g_\mu(x)^\top h_\phi(y_k) \approx \log\frac{p(y_k\mid x)}{p(y_k)} + \text{const}.$$

So $\arg\max_k\; g_\mu(x)^\top h_\phi(y_k)$ approximates the MAP class, up to the marginal $p(y_k)$.

Near-optimal Bayes classifier when label priors are roughly uniform across prompts.

Prompt Engineering Improves Accuracy

Text encoder trained on natural captions, not single labels. Richer prompts help:

Prompt styleImageNet top-1
Single-word ("dog")~60%
"A photo of a {label}."~68%
Ensemble of 80 templates~76%

For comparison, ResNet-50 supervised on ImageNet: ~76% top-1.

Radford et al., "Learning Transferable Visual Models From Natural Language Supervision", ICML 2021.

Practice

Bias / variance, scale, and what to use when

Bias / Variance Summary

BoundKey trade-off
BATight if $q$ is good; needs $H(X)$ for MI estimate
DVTight; variance $\sim e^I/N$ (explodes at high MI)
NWJLooser than DV; variance polynomial in $I$
InfoNCECeiling $\log K$; variance very stable

InfoNCE wins on training stability; DV/NWJ win on tight numerical estimation.

Why Batch Size Matters

InfoNCE bound: $\log K - \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{InfoNCE}}$. To reach $I$ nats, need $K \ge e^I$.

  • SimCLR (vision): $K \sim 4{,}096$, requires accelerator memory
  • MoCo: queue of $K \sim 65{,}000$ past negatives, decoupled from batch size
  • CLIP: $K = 32{,}768$ at full scale

Tightness gain saturates once $\log K \gg I$. Compute cost grows as $\mathcal{O}(K)$ per minibatch.

Choosing Your Bound

  • Scalable contrastive training: InfoNCE. Always.
  • Tight numerical estimate at low/moderate MI: NWJ with careful neural critic.
  • Good conditional model $q(y\mid x)$: BA, just maximize likelihood.
  • Very high MI ($I \gg 5$ nats): no estimator is reliable. Report $\log K$ saturation as lower bound.

Recap

  • InfoNCE: $K$-way softmax, one positive and $K-1$ negatives; bound $I \ge \log K - \mathcal{L}$
  • Optimal critic: $f^\star = \log r + c$, same as DV
  • $\log K$ ceiling: biased low at high MI, variance very stable
  • CLIP: separable critic, symmetric InfoNCE on $N\times N$ similarity matrix
  • Zero-shot: text encoder defines classifier; matches supervised ResNet-50 on ImageNet
  • Practice: InfoNCE for training, NWJ for tight estimation, BA for likelihoods
