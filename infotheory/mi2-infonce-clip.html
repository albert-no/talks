01

InfoNCE

A noise-contrastive lower bound on mutual information

InfoNCE: setup

One positive pair $(x,y)\sim p_{X,Y}$ and $K-1$ negatives $\{y_k^-\}_{k=1}^{K-1}\stackrel{\text{i.i.d.}}{\sim}p_Y$, independent of $x$.

Critic $f(x,y)$ assigns a score to a pair. Define

$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{InfoNCE}}(f)\;=\;-\mathbb{E}\!\left[\log\dfrac{e^{f(x,y)}}{e^{f(x,y)}+\sum_{k=1}^{K-1}e^{f(x,y_k^-)}}\right].$$

This is a $K$-class softmax cross-entropy: classify the true $y$ among $K$ candidates.

The InfoNCE bound

For any critic $f$:

$$I(X;Y)\;\ge\;\log K-\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{InfoNCE}}(f).$$

Right-hand side is at most $\log K$ (since $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{InfoNCE}}\ge 0$), so InfoNCE saturates: the bound cannot exceed $\log K$ regardless of the true MI.

Estimating MI larger than $\log K$ requires more negatives. CLIP's batch size of 32k gives $\log K\approx 10.4$ nats — far above what DV/NWJ can stably estimate.

InfoNCE proof

Proof

Use the optimal critic $f^\star(x,y)=\log r(x,y)+c(x)$ for any $x$-dependent constant $c$. Plug in:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{InfoNCE}}(f^\star)=-\mathbb{E}\log\dfrac{r(x,y)}{r(x,y)+\sum_k r(x,y_k^-)}.$$

Use $\mathbb{E}_{p(y_k^-)}r(x,y_k^-)=\int p(y_k^-)\frac{p(x,y_k^-)}{p(x)p(y_k^-)}dy_k^-=1$. By Jensen's inequality on the convex $-\log$:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{InfoNCE}}(f^\star)\ge \log\!\dfrac{\mathbb{E}_p[r(x,y)+\sum_k r(x,y_k^-)]}{\mathbb{E}\,r(x,y)}=\log(1+(K-1))=\log K-I(X;Y)+o(1).$$

Rearrange: $I(X;Y)\ge\log K-\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{InfoNCE}}(f^\star)$, and the same bound holds for any $f$. $\;\blacksquare$

The optimal InfoNCE critic

The infimum of $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{InfoNCE}}$ is attained at any $f^\star$ with

$$f^\star(x,y)\;=\;\log r(x,y)+c(x)\;=\;\log\dfrac{p(y\mid x)}{p(y)}+c(x).$$

Equivalent statements:

  • $f^\star$ is the log-density-ratio — same as DV's optimal critic.
  • $f^\star(x,\cdot)$ is the log-conditional density up to an $x$-dependent constant.
  • The softmax $\mathrm{softmax}(f^\star)$ approximates the conditional $p(y\mid x)$.

The $\log K$ ceiling

The minimum of $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{InfoNCE}}$ is the entropy of the uniform distribution over $K$ classes minus the conditional entropy — bounded above by $\log K$.

$$0\;\le\;\log K-\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{InfoNCE}}\;\le\;\log K.$$

Even with $f=f^\star$ and infinite samples, the bound saturates at $\log K$ when $I(X;Y)\gg\log K$. To estimate $I=10$ nats: need $K\ge e^{10}\approx 22000$ negatives.

InfoNCE is a biased estimator at high MI — bounded below by $\max(0,I-\log K)$, but optimization-friendly.

CLIP: Contrastive Image–Language Pretraining

InfoNCE deployed at web scale

The CLIP recipe

Radford et al., "Learning Transferable Visual Models From Natural Language Supervision", ICML 2021

The separable critic

CLIP factors the critic into two encoders — image $g_\mu(x)\in\mathbb{R}^d$ and text $h_\phi(y)\in\mathbb{R}^d$ — and uses a normalized inner product:

$$f_{\mu,\phi}(x,y)\;=\;\dfrac{1}{\tau}\,g_\mu(x)^\top h_\phi(y),\qquad \|g_\mu(x)\|=\|h_\phi(y)\|=1.$$

Two payoffs:

  • Computational: $N\times N$ similarity matrix is a single matrix multiplication, not $N^2$ joint forward passes.
  • Reusability: trained encoders are reusable independently — e.g. the image encoder for vision tasks, the text encoder for retrieval.

Temperature $\tau$ is a learned scalar (CLIP initializes $\log(1/\tau)\approx 0$, learns up to $\sim 100$).

The symmetric CLIP loss

Minibatch of $N$ image–text pairs $\{(x_i,y_i)\}_{i=1}^{N}$. Compute the $N\times N$ similarity matrix $S_{ij}=f(x_i,y_j)$.

$$\mathcal{L}_{x\to y}=-\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}\log\dfrac{\exp(S_{ii})}{\sum_{j=1}^{N}\exp(S_{ij})},\qquad \mathcal{L}_{y\to x}=-\frac{1}{N}\sum_{j=1}^{N}\log\dfrac{\exp(S_{jj})}{\sum_{i=1}^{N}\exp(S_{ij})}.$$

Symmetric total loss:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{CLIP}}\;=\;\tfrac{1}{2}(\mathcal{L}_{x\to y}+\mathcal{L}_{y\to x}).$$

The $N\times N$ similarity matrix

Row $i$: image $x_i$ vs. every caption $y_1,\ldots,y_N$.

Column $j$: caption $y_j$ vs. every image $x_1,\ldots,x_N$.

Diagonal entries are the true pairs — should be highest in their row and column.

Off-diagonals are contrastive negatives: random shufflings within the batch.

Total: $N$ row-wise classifications $+$ $N$ column-wise classifications. CLIP optimizes both simultaneously.

image index $i$  ×  text index $j$

03

Zero-Shot Classification

Reusing the contrastive head with no task-specific training

How CLIP becomes a classifier

For a $K$-way classification task with labels $\{c_1,\ldots,c_K\}$:

  1. Build prompts: $y_k=$ "A photo of a {$c_k$}." for each label $c_k$.
  2. Embed each prompt: $h_k=h_\phi(y_k)$.
  3. Embed the test image: $g=g_\mu(x)$.
  4. Predict $\hat c=\arg\max_k g^\top h_k$.

No retraining, no task-specific labels at training time. The classifier is built from text alone.

Why does zero-shot work?

InfoNCE training makes $g^\top h$ approximate $\log r(x,y)+c(x)$, the log-density-ratio between joint and product. For a label class:

$$g_\mu(x)^\top h_\phi(y_k)\;\approx\;\log\dfrac{p(y_k\mid x)}{p(y_k)}+\text{const}.$$

So $\arg\max_k g_\mu(x)^\top h_\phi(y_k)$ approximates the MAP class up to the marginal $p(y_k)$ — nearly the optimal Bayes classifier when label priors are roughly uniform across the prompts.

Prompt engineering improves accuracy

The text encoder was trained on natural captions, not single labels. Single-word prompts ("dog") give weaker classification than full sentences ("A photo of a dog").

CLIP paper reports zero-shot ImageNet accuracy:

  • Single-word prompts: $\approx 60$ %.
  • "A photo of a {label}.": $\approx 68$ %.
  • Ensemble of 80 prompt templates: $\approx 76$ %.

For comparison, ResNet-50 supervised on ImageNet itself: $\approx 76$ % top-1. CLIP matches it without seeing a single ImageNet label.

Why batch size matters

The InfoNCE bound is $\log K-\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{InfoNCE}}$. To estimate true MI of $I$ nats, we need at least $K\ge e^{I}$ negatives.

Practical choices:

  • SimCLR (vision): $K\sim 4{,}096$ — needs accelerator memory.
  • MoCo: queue of past negatives ($K\sim 65{,}000$) decoupled from batch size.
  • CLIP: $K=32{,}768$ at full scale.

Why not $K\to\infty$? Compute cost is $\mathcal{O}(K)$ per minibatch, and the bound's tightness gain saturates once $\log K\gg I$.

Choosing your bound

  • You want a scalable contrastive objective for representation learning → InfoNCE. Always.
  • You want to estimate MI numerically at low/moderate values → NWJ with a careful neural critic.
  • You have a good conditional model $q(y\mid x)$ → BA — just maximize likelihood.
  • You suspect MI is very high ($I\gg 5$ nats) → no estimator is reliable. Report $\log K$ saturation as a lower bound and stop.

Recap of Lecture 2

  • InfoNCE: $K$-way softmax classifier with one positive and $K-1$ negatives. Lower bound $I\ge\log K-\mathcal{L}$.
  • Optimal critic: $f^\star=\log r+c$, same as DV.
  • $\log K$ saturation: InfoNCE cannot exceed $\log K$. Bias-low at high MI, variance very stable.
  • CLIP: separable critic via two encoders, symmetric InfoNCE on the $N\times N$ similarity matrix.
  • Zero-shot classification: reuse text encoder to define a classifier — matches supervised ResNet-50 on ImageNet.
  • Practice: InfoNCE for training, NWJ for tight numerical estimation, BA for likelihoods.
