Masked Diffusion at a Glance

Start from a fully masked sequence; iteratively unmask tokens in any order.

$$x_T = [\texttt{M}]^N \;\xrightarrow{\text{denoise}}\; x_{T-1} \;\xrightarrow{\text{denoise}}\; \cdots \;\xrightarrow{\text{denoise}}\; x_0$$

Forward: progressively mask tokens.

Reverse: predict masked from visible context.

Two Key Inductive Biases

Bidirectional attention

Every token sees every other. No causal mask.

Shared weights couple forward and reverse directions.

Any-order training

Random masking exposes every ordering.

Learns order-agnostic representations.

New control surfaces: decoding order, step count, masking strategy. New failure modes: EOS overflow, safety gaps, dependency errors.

Recent Progress in the Field

From principled training to competitive LLM-scale systems

SEDD: Score Entropy

Novel loss for discrete diffusion: directly parameterizes the concrete score (ratio of transition probabilities).

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{SE}} = \mathbb{E}_{t,x_0,x_t}\!\left[\sum_{y \neq x_t} s_\theta(y \mid x_t, t) - \log s_\theta(x_0 \mid x_t, t)\right]$$
  • Principled connection to continuous diffusion score matching
  • First discrete diffusion competitive on language modeling
  • Foundation for all subsequent scaling work
Lou et al., "Discrete Diffusion Modeling by Estimating the Ratios of the Data Distribution", ICML 2024.

RADD: Absorbing Diffusion = Any-Order AR

Clean-data conditionals reveal that absorbing diffusion and any-order autoregressive modeling share the same conditional family.

Bridge result: optimal absorbing-diffusion denoiser = any-order AR model.
  • Unifies two seemingly different paradigms
  • Justifies AR-style training tricks for diffusion
  • Clean theoretical foundation for subsequent scaling
Ou et al., "Absorption Diffusion Revisited", 2024.

LLaDA: Diffusion at 8B Scale

First diffusion LLM trained at 8B parameter scale with competitive general-purpose performance.

  • Masked diffusion objective, large-scale training data
  • Instruction-tuned variant (LLaDA-8B-Instruct)
  • Competitive with AR models on math, code, QA
Favorable scaling laws: dLLM quality tracks compute.
Nie et al., "LLaDA: Large Language Diffusion with mAsking", 2025.

Rainbow Padding

The EOS Overflow Problem

Instruction-tuned dLLMs: as allocated sequence length grows, responses paradoxically shorten.

  • Responses collapse into streams of <eos> tokens
  • Severity increases with generation budget
  • Affects math, code, and general tasks

Expected

Theansweris42eospadpad

EOS overflow

Theeoseoseoseoseos
Rainbow Padding

Root Cause: Dual Role of <eos>

Standard instruction-tuning pads sequences with <eos>, giving it a dual role: termination signal and padding token.

$$p(\texttt{eos} \mid \text{position } i) \uparrow \;\text{as}\; i \to N$$
  • EOS dominates later positions in training data
  • Model learns strong positional bias toward EOS
  • Confidence-based decoding selects EOS early
  • EOS propagates backward; response collapses
Rainbow Padding

Solution: Cyclic Distinct Pad Tokens

Replace repeated <eos> padding with a cycling sequence of distinct pad tokens, reserving one <eos> for true termination.

Before

responseeoseoseoseos

After (Rainbow)

responseeospad₁pad₂pad₃
  • Decouples termination from padding
  • Distributes probability mass; no single token dominates
As few as 7 distinct pad tokens resolve the issue.

A2D

The Enlarged Safety Attack Surface

dLLMs' any-order generation: a fundamentally different safety challenge.

  • Harmful spans appear at arbitrary positions
  • Template-based prefilling attacks (DIJA) bypass response-level refusals
  • Standard AR safety alignment fails: >80% attack success rate on baseline dLLMs
AR safety relies on refusal at the beginning of a response. dLLMs generate in any order, so initial refusal can be bypassed.
A2D

A2D: Any-Order, Any-Step Defense

Train dLLMs to emit an [EOS] refusal at any masked position when harmful content is encountered.

$$\text{A2D}: \; p_\theta(\texttt{[EOS]} \mid x_t, \text{pos}_i) \uparrow \;\text{if harmful}$$
  • Align at token level, not response level
  • Train under randomized masking: robust to any decoding order
  • Use [EOS] probability as real-time safety monitor
A2D

Three Innovations

Real-time monitoring

[EOS] probability as internal safety signal. Threshold at first step enables early rejection.

19.3× faster termination

FITS attack defense

Fill-in-the-sentence: embed one masked sentence inside a harmful response. A2D blocks it.

~0% attack success

No over-refusal

0% false positives on XSTest. Benign prompts correctly handled.

0% false positive

dgMARK

Watermarking for Diffusion LLMs

AR watermarking: bias next-token distribution; requires sequential generation. Doesn't apply to dLLMs.

dLLMs generate tokens in arbitrary order. Need a new paradigm.

Key observation: practical dLLMs are sensitive to unmasking order, opening a new watermarking channel.

AR watermarking

Bias next-token distribution. Sequential only.

dgMARK

Steer unmasking order. Works with any-order generation.

dgMARK

Decoding-Guided Watermarking

  • Define a binary hash function mapping tokens to {0, 1}
  • Rank candidate positions by decoding score
  • Steer unmasking toward positions satisfying a parity constraint
  • Detect via elevated parity-matching statistics
Never reweights probabilities: changes only which positions unmask first.
  • Plug-and-play with any decoding strategy
  • Sliding-window detector: robust to insertion, deletion, substitution, paraphrasing
Hong, No, "dgMARK", arXiv:2601.22985, 2025.
Reversal Curse

The Reversal Curse

AR models trained on "A is B" fail catastrophically on "B is A."

Autoregressive

Trains: "Tom Cruise's mother is Mary Lee Pfeiffer"

Fails: "Mary Lee Pfeiffer's son is ___"

Masked diffusion

Same data. Reversal curse significantly mitigated.

Our question: if not the training objective alone, what explains the mitigation?
Reversal Curse

Architecture + Training Dynamics

Mitigation arises from architectural structure and its interaction with training, not the objective alone.

Mechanism 1: weight sharing

Shared $W_Q, W_K, W_V$ couple forward and reverse attention patterns.

$$\alpha_{A \to B} = \text{softmax}(q_A^\top k_B)$$

Mechanism 2: gradient alignment

Forward and reverse gradients align:

$$\nabla_\theta \mathcal{L}_{\text{fwd}} \cdot \nabla_\theta \mathcal{L}_{\text{rev}} > 0$$
A gradient step reducing forward loss also reduces reverse loss.
Reversal Curse

Why AR Models Can't Do This

Causal (AR) transformer

  • Causal mask blocks $B \to A$ attention
  • Weight sharing cannot couple directions
  • Forward training gives zero reverse signal

Bidirectional transformer

  • No causal mask: both directions active
  • Weight sharing creates coupling
  • Forward training implicitly helps reverse
Both masked objective AND bidirectional architecture are needed; architecture is the critical piece.
Shin, Kim, Lee, Jeon, No, "Reversal Curse in Masked Diffusion Models", arXiv:2602.02133, 2025.
DAPD

The Parallel Decoding Challenge

Promise: dLLMs can unmask multiple tokens per step, enabling faster generation.

Problem: each step provides only token-wise marginals. Parallel unmasking ignores inter-token dependencies.

Naive parallel decoding: unmask tokens independently, ignoring dependencies. Errors accumulate; quality degrades.

Example: "The [M] [M] is blue." Tokens "big" and "sky" are strongly dependent. Unmasking both simultaneously can produce incoherent text.

DAPD

Dependency-Aware Parallel Decoding

Use self-attention to build a dependency graph; unmask independent sets in parallel.

  • Extract attention weights between masked positions
  • Build dependency graph: edge = strong interaction
  • Find independent set: non-adjacent nodes
  • Unmask independent tokens in parallel; repeat
Training-free: no auxiliary models, no retraining. Fully plug-and-play.
Kim, Jeon, Jeon, No, "DAPD", arXiv:2603.12996, 2025.

Practical Guidance

Training

  • Use Rainbow Padding for instruction tuning
  • A2D: token-level safety is non-optional
  • AR safety methods are insufficient

Decoding

  • DAPD respects inter-token dependencies
  • Unmasking order matters more than expected
  • Confidence-based decoding needs EOS mitigation

Deployment

  • dgMARK: watermarking without quality loss
  • Monitor [EOS] probability for real-time safety
  • Expect different failure modes than AR models

Key Takeaways

  • dLLMs are real. SEDD to LLaDA 2.0: theory to competitive 7B+ systems in under two years.
  • Different behavior. Bidirectional + masked training yields unique inductive biases, both beneficial and dangerous.
  • New control surfaces. Robustness, safety, watermarking, decoding all need dLLM-specific solutions.
  • Architecture matters. Reversal curse mitigation comes from weight sharing, not the objective.
  • Solutions exist today. Rainbow Padding, A2D, dgMARK, DAPD are all plug-and-play.
