01

The Variance Preserving Process

Scaled noise so variance does not explode

Why Variance Preserving?

  • VE process: variance $\sigma_0^2 + n\sigma^2 \to \infty$.
  • Terminal distribution depends on data variance.
  • Prefer a fixed terminal, ideally $\mathcal{N}(0,1)$.
  • Trivial to sample from.

Shrink $X^{(n-1)}$ before adding noise: signal decays, noise accumulates to unit variance.

VP Forward Process

Fix schedule $\{\beta_n\}_{n=1}^N$ with $0 < \beta_n < 1$:

$$X^{(n)} = \sqrt{1-\beta_n}\,X^{(n-1)} + \sqrt{\beta_n}\,Z^{(n)},\quad Z^{(n)} \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1)\ \text{i.i.d.}$$
  • Equivalently, $X^{(n)}\,|\,X^{(n-1)} \sim \mathcal{N}\!\left(\sqrt{1-\beta_n}\,X^{(n-1)},\; \beta_n\right)$.
  • If $\mathrm{Var}(X^{(n-1)}) = 1$, then $\mathrm{Var}(X^{(n)}) = (1-\beta_n) + \beta_n = 1$.

Exercise. Verify VP preserves variance and find $\mathrm{Var}(X^{(N)})$ as $N \to \infty$.

$n$ Steps in One Shot

Define $\bar{\alpha}_n = \prod_{i=1}^n (1-\beta_i)$. Unrolling the recursion:

$$X^{(n)} \;\stackrel{(d)}{=}\; \sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_n}\,X^{(0)} + \sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_n}\,Z,\quad Z \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1).$$
  • Training at any noise level: single Gaussian draw.
  • As $\bar{\alpha}_n \to 0$: signal fades, noise approaches $\mathcal{N}(0,1)$.

Convergence: MGF Proof

Using independence of $X^{(n-1)}$ and $Z^{(n)}$:

$$\begin{aligned} M_{X^{(n)}}(t) &= \mathbb{E}\bigl[e^{t\sqrt{1-\beta_n}\,X^{(n-1)}}\bigr]\,e^{\beta_n t^2 / 2} \\ &\;\;\vdots \\ &= \mathbb{E}\bigl[e^{t\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_n}\,X^{(0)}}\bigr]\,e^{(1-\bar{\alpha}_n)\,t^2 / 2}. \end{aligned}$$

As $\bar{\alpha}_n \to 0$: first factor $\to 1$, second $\to e^{t^2/2}$.

Terminal $X^{(N)} \xrightarrow{d} \mathcal{N}(0,1)$, independent of data law.

02

DDPM Reverse Model

Gaussian approximation via Tweedie

Reverse Conditional, VP Version

By the Bayes-plus-Tweedie argument (Lecture 1), for small $\beta_n$:

$$X^{(n-1)}\,|\,X^{(n)} \;\approx\; \mathcal{N}\bigl(\mu(X^{(n)}, n),\; \beta_n\bigr),$$

where the mean is:

$$\mu(X^{(n)}, n) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-\beta_n}}\left(X^{(n)} + \beta_n\,\frac{\partial}{\partial x}\log P_{X^{(n)}}(X^{(n)})\right).$$
Ho, Jain & Abbeel, "Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models", NeurIPS 2020.

What the Network Learns

Replace unknown score by neural approximation $s_\theta(X^{(n)}, n)$:

$$\mu_\theta(X^{(n)}, n) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-\beta_n}}\left(X^{(n)} + \beta_n\,s_\theta(X^{(n)}, n)\right).$$

Sampling uses:

$$p_\theta(X^{(n-1)}\,|\,X^{(n)}) = \mathcal{N}\bigl(\mu_\theta(X^{(n)}, n),\; \beta_n\bigr).$$

Loss for $s_\theta$: derived from maximum likelihood.

03

Variational Lower Bound

Jensen, Markov identity, three-term decomposition

The Generative Model

We want to maximize the marginal $p_\theta(X^{(0)})$ on data — first we must compute it.

Reverse chain ($p(X^{(N)}) = \mathcal{N}(0,1)$, $X^{(i:j)} = (X^{(i)},\ldots,X^{(j)})$):

$$\begin{aligned} p_\theta\bigl(X^{(0:N)}\bigr) &= p\bigl(X^{(N)}\bigr)\prod_{n=1}^{N} p_\theta\bigl(X^{(n-1)}\,|\,X^{(n)}\bigr), \\ p_\theta\bigl(X^{(0)}\bigr) &= \int p_\theta\bigl(X^{(0:N)}\bigr)\,dX^{(1:N)}. \end{aligned}$$

Marginal: intractable integral.

Why Maximize the Marginal?

MLE on data $\equiv$ closing the KL gap to the data law:

$$\mathbb{E}_{X^{(0)}\sim q}\bigl[-\log p_\theta(X^{(0)})\bigr] \;=\; D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(q(X^{(0)})\,\Vert\,p_\theta(X^{(0)})\bigr) + H(q).$$

Entropy $H(q)$ is constant in $\theta$; only the KL term moves.

Closing the marginal gap = matching the data distribution.

Likelihood to VLB

Insert forward joint $q(X^{(1:N)}\,|\,X^{(0)}) = \prod_{n=1}^{N} q(X^{(n)}\,|\,X^{(n-1)})$ in num/denom — the $q$'s cancel — then apply Jensen:

$$\begin{aligned} -\log p_\theta\bigl(X^{(0)}\bigr) &= -\log \int q\bigl(X^{(1:N)}\,|\,X^{(0)}\bigr)\,\frac{p_\theta(X^{(0:N)})}{q(X^{(1:N)}\,|\,X^{(0)})}\,dX^{(1:N)} \\ &= -\log \mathbb{E}_q\!\left[\frac{p_\theta(X^{(0:N)})}{q(X^{(1:N)}\,|\,X^{(0)})}\,\middle|\,X^{(0)}\right] \\ &\leq \mathbb{E}_q\!\left[-\log \frac{p_\theta(X^{(0:N)})}{q(X^{(1:N)}\,|\,X^{(0)})}\,\middle|\,X^{(0)}\right]. \end{aligned}$$

A Markov Rewrite

Forward chain is Markov: $X^{(n)}$ depends only on $X^{(n-1)}$.

$X^{(0)}$ $\to$ $X^{(1)}$ $\to$ $\cdots$ $\to$ $X^{(n-1)}$ $\to$ $X^{(n)}$ $\to$ $\cdots$ $\to$ $X^{(N)}$

Bayes inside the chain conditional on $X^{(0)}$ ($n \geq 2$):

$$q\bigl(X^{(n)}\,|\,X^{(n-1)}\bigr)\,q\bigl(X^{(n-1)}\,|\,X^{(0)}\bigr) = q\bigl(X^{(n-1)}\,|\,X^{(n)}, X^{(0)}\bigr)\,q\bigl(X^{(n)}\,|\,X^{(0)}\bigr).$$

Exercise. Prove this identity from the chain rule.

Three-Term Decomposition

Apply the Markov identity inside the bound:

$$\mathbb{E}_q\!\left[\underbrace{-\log\frac{p(X^{(N)})}{q(X^{(N)}|X^{(0)})}}_{L_N}\; +\sum_{n=2}^{N}\underbrace{\biggl(-\log\frac{p_\theta(X^{(n-1)}|X^{(n)})}{q(X^{(n-1)}|X^{(n)}, X^{(0)})}\biggr)}_{L_{n-1}}\; \underbrace{-\log p_\theta(X^{(0)}|X^{(1)})}_{L_0}\right].$$

Three groups: prior match ($L_N$), reverse-step KL ($L_{n-1}$, summed), boundary ($L_0$).

$L_N$: Match the Prior

$$L_N \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{X^{(0)}\sim q}\Bigl[D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(q(X^{(N)}\,|\,X^{(0)})\,\Vert\,p(X^{(N)})\bigr)\Bigr].$$
  • Measures proximity of the forward endpoint to the Gaussian prior.
  • No $\theta$ dependence: constant from the optimizer's view.

$L_N \to 0$ by VP schedule design.

Ignored during training.

$L_{n-1}$: Match Each Reverse Step

$$L_{n-1} \;=\; \mathbb{E}_q\Bigl[D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(q(X^{(n-1)}\,|\,X^{(n)}, X^{(0)})\,\Vert\,p_\theta(X^{(n-1)}\,|\,X^{(n)})\bigr)\Bigr].$$
  • Both sides are Gaussian: KL available in closed form.
  • The actual training signal — every reverse step.
  • $L_0 = \mathbb{E}_q[-\log p_\theta(X^{(0)}\,|\,X^{(1)})]$: boundary term, negligible, dropped.

Exercise. Show $D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(\mathcal{N}(\mu_0,\sigma^2 I)\,\Vert\,\mathcal{N}(\mu_1,\sigma^2 I)\bigr) = \tfrac{1}{2\sigma^2}\|\mu_1-\mu_0\|^2$.

Target Posterior Is Gaussian

Conditional forward marginal (closed form, from one-shot rule):

$$q\bigl(X^{(n)}\,|\,X^{(0)}\bigr) \;=\; \mathcal{N}\bigl(\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_n}\,X^{(0)},\; 1-\bar{\alpha}_n\bigr).$$

Hence the reverse posterior is also Gaussian:

$$q\bigl(X^{(n-1)}\,|\,X^{(n)}, X^{(0)}\bigr) \;=\; \mathcal{N}\bigl(\mu_n(X^{(n)}, X^{(0)}),\; \beta_n\bigr),$$

where $\mu_n$ uses the conditional score $\partial_x \log q(X^{(n)}\,|\,X^{(0)})$.

Exercise. Derive the posterior. Hint: all factors are Gaussian given $X^{(0)}$.

KL for Matched-Variance Gaussians

Both distributions have variance $\beta_n$: KL reduces to squared-mean mismatch:

$$L_{n-1} = \frac{1}{2\beta_n}\,\bigl\|\mu_n(X^{(n)}, X^{(0)}) - \mu_\theta(X^{(n)}, n)\bigr\|^2.$$

Using $\partial_x \log q(X^{(n)}\,|\,X^{(0)}) = -(X^{(n)} - \sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_n}X^{(0)})/(1-\bar{\alpha}_n)$:

$$L_{n-1} = \frac{\beta_n}{2(1-\beta_n)}\left\|\frac{\partial}{\partial X^{(n)}}\log q(X^{(n)}\,|\,X^{(0)}) - s_\theta(X^{(n)}, n)\right\|^2.$$

$\epsilon$-Prediction Reparameterization

Write $X^{(n)} \stackrel{(d)}{=} \sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_n}\,X^{(0)} + \sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_n}\,\epsilon_n$. Reparameterize:

$$s_\theta(X^{(n)}, n) = -\,\frac{\epsilon_\theta(X^{(n)}, n)}{\sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_n}}.$$

Per-step loss becomes noise matching:

$$L_{n-1} = \frac{\beta_n}{2(1-\beta_n)(1-\bar{\alpha}_n)}\left\|\epsilon_n - \epsilon_\theta\!\bigl(\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_n}\,X^{(0)} + \sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_n}\,\epsilon_n,\; n\bigr)\right\|^2.$$

$\epsilon_\theta$ predicts the Gaussian noise mixed into $X^{(0)}$. Plain regression.

04

Training and Sampling

$\epsilon$-prediction loss in practice

Training Algorithm

DDPM Training — repeat until converged
  1. Sample $X^{(0)} \sim p_{\text{data}}$.
  2. Sample $n \sim \text{Unif}\{1, \ldots, N\}$.
  3. Sample $\epsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1)$.
  4. Form $X^{(n)} = \sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_n}\,X^{(0)} + \sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_n}\,\epsilon$.
  5. Gradient step on $\tilde{\lambda}_n\,\bigl\|\epsilon - \epsilon_\theta(X^{(n)}, n)\bigr\|^2$.

Original DDPM: $\tilde{\lambda}_n = 1$ (unweighted).

Sampling Algorithm

DDPM Sampling
  1. Sample $X^{(N)} \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1)$.
  2. For $n = N, N-1, \ldots, 1$:
    1. Sample $Z^{(n)} \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1)$.
    2. $X^{(n-1)} = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-\beta_n}}\!\left(X^{(n)} - \dfrac{\beta_n}{\sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_n}}\,\epsilon_\theta(X^{(n)}, n)\right) + \sqrt{\beta_n}\,Z^{(n)}$.
  3. Return $X^{(0)}$.

Three Approximation Errors

Gaussian reverse

Requires small $\beta_n$.

Large steps break the approximation.

Imperfect score

$\epsilon_\theta$: finite capacity, finite data.

Errors compound over steps.

Prior mismatch

$X^{(N)} \to \mathcal{N}(0,1)$ requires $\bar{\alpha}_N \to 0$.

Finite $N$: residual gap.

Lecture Recap

  • VP forward: $X^{(n)} = \sqrt{1-\beta_n}\,X^{(n-1)} + \sqrt{\beta_n}\,Z^{(n)}$. Unit variance preserved.
  • Terminal: converges to $\mathcal{N}(0,1)$ when $\bar{\alpha}_n \to 0$ (MGF proof).
  • Reverse: approximately Gaussian, mean given by the score function.
  • VLB: $L_N$ (constant) $+\ \sum L_{n-1}$ (matching) $+\ L_0$ (boundary).
  • Matching term collapses to $\epsilon$-prediction regression.
