From Discrete to Continuous

VE and VP as limits of a stochastic differential equation

VE as an SDE

Take $X_{t} = X_{t-\Delta t} + \sqrt{\beta}\,Z_t$, $Z_t \sim \mathcal{N}(0,\Delta t)$. Let $\Delta t \to 0$:

$$dX_t = \sqrt{\beta}\,dZ_t.$$

$dZ_t$: increment of standard Brownian motion, $Z_t - Z_{t-\Delta t} \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \Delta t)$.

Integrated: $X_t = X_0 + \sqrt{\beta}\,Z_t$. Matches VE single-step corruption.

VP as an SDE

VP recursion: $X_t = \sqrt{1-\beta_n}\,X_{t-\Delta t} + \sqrt{\beta_n}\,Z_t$. Set $\beta_n = \beta_t\,dt$, Taylor-expand $\sqrt{1-\beta_t\,dt} \approx 1 - \tfrac{\beta_t\,dt}{2}$:

$$dX_t = -\frac{\beta_t}{2}\,X_t\,dt + \sqrt{\beta_t}\,dZ_t.$$

Linear Ornstein–Uhlenbeck SDE: deterministic pull toward zero plus white noise. Continuous-time DDPM.

General Forward SDE

Both VE and VP fit:

$$dX_t = f(X_t, t)\,dt + g(t)\,dZ_t.$$

VE

$f = 0$, $g(t) = \sqrt{\beta}$.

VP

$f(X_t, t) = -\tfrac{\beta_t}{2}X_t$, $g(t) = \sqrt{\beta_t}$.

Euler–Maruyama discretization: $X_{k+1} = X_k + \Delta t\,f(X_k, k\Delta t) + g(k\Delta t)\sqrt{\Delta t}\,Z_k$.

The Solution Is a Distribution

An SDE defines a stochastic process via the Itô integral:

$$X_t = X_0 + \int_0^t f(X_s, s)\,ds + \int_0^t g(s)\,dW_s.$$

For diffusion models, we need only the marginal densities $\{p_t\}_{t\in[0,T]}$ and their evolution from $p_0$ to $p_T$.

02

Fokker–Planck Equation

The PDE that governs $p_t$

Fokker–Planck, 1D

Theorem (Fokker–Planck).

For $dX_t = f(X_t, t)\,dt + g(t)\,dZ_t$, the marginal density $p_t(x)$ satisfies:

$$\partial_t\,p_t(x) = -\partial_x\bigl(f(x,t)\,p_t(x)\bigr) + \frac{g^2(t)}{2}\,\partial_x^2\,p_t(x).$$

A deterministic PDE for the probability density. Derived by testing against smooth functions and integrating by parts.

Derivation: Evolve Expectations

Let $\phi$ be smooth with compact support. Over small $\varepsilon$: $X_{t+\varepsilon} \approx X + \varepsilon f + \sqrt{\varepsilon}\,g\,Z$, $Z\sim\mathcal{N}(0,1)$. Taylor-expand $\phi$ to second order:

$$\phi(X_{t+\varepsilon}) \approx \phi(X) + \phi'(X)\,(\varepsilon f + \sqrt{\varepsilon}\,g\,Z) + \tfrac{1}{2}\phi''(X)\,\varepsilon g^2 Z^2 + O(\varepsilon^{3/2}).$$

Derivation: Collect Surviving Terms

Expectation kills $\sqrt{\varepsilon}\,\phi'(X) g Z$ (mean zero) and replaces $Z^2$ by $1$:

$$\partial_t\,\mathbb{E}[\phi(X_t)] = \mathbb{E}_{X \sim p_t}\!\left[\phi'(X)\,f(X, t) + \tfrac{1}{2}\phi''(X)\,g^2(t)\right].$$

Integrate by parts, use $p_t(\pm \infty) = 0$:

$$\int \phi(x)\,\partial_t p_t(x)\,dx = \int \phi(x)\bigl[-\partial_x(f\,p_t) + \tfrac{g^2}{2}\,\partial_x^2 p_t\bigr]\,dx.$$

Holds for all test functions $\phi$: integrands match pointwise. This is Fokker–Planck.

03

Anderson's Reverse SDE

A backward SDE with the same marginals

Anderson's Theorem

Theorem (Anderson, 1982).

For the forward SDE $dX_t = f(X_t, t)\,dt + g(t)\,dZ_t$, $X_0 \sim p_0$, the reverse-time SDE

$$d\bar{X}_t = \bigl(f(\bar{X}_t, t) - g^2(t)\,\partial_x \log p_t(\bar{X}_t)\bigr)\,dt + g(t)\,d\bar{Z}_t,\quad \bar{X}_T \sim p_T,$$

has the same marginals as the forward process: $\bar{X}_t \stackrel{(d)}{=} X_t$ for every $t \in [0,T]$.

Anderson, "Reverse-time diffusion equation models," Stochastic Processes and their Applications 1982.

Proof Setup

Let $\{q_t\}$ be the marginals of $Y_t = \bar{X}_{T-t}$. By Fokker–Planck on $Y_t$:

$$\partial_t q_t(y) = \partial_y\!\Bigl[\bigl(f(y, T-t) - g^2(T-t)\,\partial_y \log p_{T-t}(y)\bigr)\,q_t(y)\Bigr] + \frac{g^2(T-t)}{2}\,\partial_y^2 q_t(y).$$

A time-change $t \mapsto T-t$ gives the PDE for the marginals $\bar{p}_t$ of $\bar{X}_t$.

Key Step: $p_t$ Solves the Reverse PDE

Plug $\bar{p}_t = p_t$, expand the score-shift term, use $(\partial_x \log p_t)\,p_t = \partial_x p_t$:

$$\begin{aligned} &-\partial_x\!\Bigl[\bigl(f - g^2\,\partial_x \log p_t\bigr)\,p_t\Bigr] - \tfrac{g^2}{2}\,\partial_x^2 p_t \\ &\quad= -\partial_x(f\,p_t) + g^2\,\partial_x(\partial_x p_t) - \tfrac{g^2}{2}\,\partial_x^2 p_t \\ &\quad= -\partial_x(f\,p_t) + \tfrac{g^2}{2}\,\partial_x^2 p_t \;=\; \partial_t p_t. \end{aligned}$$

$p_t$ satisfies the reverse PDE. By uniqueness, $\bar{p}_t = p_t$.

Why This Matters for Generation

Anderson's theorem is the continuous-time analog of the Bayes-based reverse step from Lecture 1: to reverse diffusion, need the score $\partial_x \log p_t$.

Generation: sample $\bar{X}_T \sim p_T$, run the reverse SDE with drift $f - g^2 s_\theta$ and diffusion $g$. DDPM sampling is the Euler–Maruyama discretization.

04

Score Matching

Learning the score without knowing the marginals

Natural Loss Is Intractable

Train $s_\theta$ against the true score at every $t$:

$$\mathcal{L}(\theta) = \int_0^T \lambda(t)\,\mathbb{E}_{X_t}\!\left[\bigl\|s_\theta(X_t, t) - \partial_x \log p_t(X_t)\bigr\|^2\right]dt.$$

Samples from $p_t$ are easy (run forward). The function $p_t$ and its gradient are not.

Rewrite the marginal gradient as an expectation over conditionals $p_{t\mid 0}$ that are known in closed form.

Score Matching Theorem

Theorem (Vincent, 2011).

Up to a $\theta$-independent constant:

$$\mathcal{L}(\theta) = \int_0^T \lambda(t)\,\mathbb{E}_{X_0}\!\left[\mathbb{E}_{X_t\,|\,X_0}\!\left[\bigl\|s_\theta(X_t, t) - \partial_x \log p_{t\mid 0}(X_t\,|\,X_0)\bigr\|^2\right]\right] dt + C.$$

Conditional density $p_{t\mid 0}$: known in closed form (noise kernel). For VP: Gaussian with trivial score.

Vincent, "A connection between score matching and denoising autoencoders," Neural Computation 2011.

Proof: Score as Conditional Expectation

$$\begin{aligned} \partial_{X_t}\log p_t(X_t) &= \frac{\partial_{X_t}\,p_t(X_t)}{p_t(X_t)} \\ &= \int \partial_{X_t}\!\log p_{t\mid 0}(X_t\,|\,X_0)\cdot\frac{p_0(X_0)\,p_{t\mid 0}(X_t\,|\,X_0)}{p_t(X_t)}\,dX_0 \\ &= \mathbb{E}_{X_0\,|\,X_t}\!\bigl[\partial_{X_t}\!\log p_{t\mid 0}(X_t\,|\,X_0)\bigr]. \end{aligned}$$

Step 2 uses log-derivative trick; step 3 is Bayes.

Proof: Plug into the Loss

Expand squared norm, focus on cross term, use the score identity:

$$\begin{aligned} &\mathbb{E}_{X_t}\!\left[\bigl\|s_\theta(X_t, t) - \partial_{X_t}\log p_t(X_t)\bigr\|^2\right] \\ &\quad = \mathbb{E}_{X_0}\!\mathbb{E}_{X_t\,|\,X_0}\!\left[\|s_\theta - \partial_{X_t}\log p_{t\mid 0}(X_t\,|\,X_0)\|^2\right] + C'. \end{aligned}$$

Absorbing $\|\partial_{X_t}\log p_t\|^2$ into the constant completes the proof.

$\epsilon$-Reparameterization

For VP the noise kernel is Gaussian: $X_t \stackrel{(d)}{=} \gamma_t\,X_0 + \sigma_t\,\epsilon$, $\epsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I)$. Conditional score:

$$\nabla\log p_{t\mid 0}(X_t\,|\,X_0) = -\frac{\epsilon}{\sigma_t}.$$

Reparameterize $s_\theta(X_t, t) = -\epsilon_\theta(X_t, t)\,/\,\sigma_t$. Loss becomes noise prediction:

$$\tilde{\mathcal{L}}(\theta) = T\,\mathbb{E}_{X_0,\,\epsilon,\,t}\!\left[\frac{\lambda(t)}{\sigma_t^2}\,\bigl\|\epsilon_\theta(\gamma_t X_0 + \sigma_t\epsilon, t) - \epsilon\bigr\|^2\right].$$

Example: Ornstein–Uhlenbeck

For $dX_t = -\beta\,X_t\,dt + \sigma\,dW_t$:

$$p_{t\mid 0}(X_t\,|\,X_0) = \mathcal{N}\!\left(e^{-\beta t}\,X_0,\;\frac{\sigma^2}{2\beta}(1 - e^{-2\beta t})\right).$$

$\gamma_t = e^{-\beta t}$, $\sigma_t^2 = \tfrac{\sigma^2}{2\beta}(1-e^{-2\beta t})$. Score kernel closed form.

$\epsilon_\theta$ trained instead of $s_\theta$: stable scale across time. Equivalent up to factor $\sigma_t$.

Multi-Dimensional Extension

For $\mathbf{X} \in \mathbb{R}^d$, isotropic $g(t) = \rho(t)\,I$:

  • Fokker–Planck: $\partial_t p_t = -\nabla_{\mathbf{x}}\cdot (f p_t) + \tfrac{\rho^2}{2}\Delta p_t$.
  • Anderson: reverse SDE uses gradient score $\nabla_{\mathbf{x}}\log p_t$.
  • Score matching: holds verbatim with $\nabla_{\mathbf{X}_t}\log p_{t\mid 0}$.

DDPM and Score Matching: Same Loss

With DDPM reparameterization $\epsilon_\theta = \sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_n}\,s_\theta$, the DDPM VLB:

$$\mathcal{L}(\theta) = \sum_{n=1}^{N}\tilde{\lambda}_n\,\mathbb{E}\!\left[\bigl\|\epsilon - \epsilon_\theta\bigl(\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_n}X^{(0)} + \sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_n}\epsilon,\; n\bigr)\bigr\|^2\right]$$

is the discretization of the score-matching objective.

DDPM is discrete-time score matching on the VP SDE, trained via $\epsilon$-prediction.

Lecture Recap

  • VE and VP are limits of $dX_t = f\,dt + g\,dZ_t$.
  • Marginal densities $p_t$ evolve by Fokker–Planck.
  • Anderson's theorem: reverse-time SDE with matching marginals via the score.
  • Intractable loss $\|s_\theta - \nabla \log p_t\|^2$ equals tractable score-matching loss using known kernel $p_{t\mid 0}$.
  • $\epsilon$-parameterization: noise regression. DDPM is a special case.
