01

Discrete to Continuous

VE and VP as limits of a stochastic differential equation

VE as an SDE

Step size $\Delta t \to 0$ in $X_{t} = X_{t-\Delta t} + \sqrt{\beta}\,Z_t$ with $Z_t \sim \mathcal{N}(0,\Delta t)$:

$$dX_t = \sqrt{\beta}\,dZ_t.$$

$dZ_t$: standard Brownian increment, variance $\Delta t$.

Integrated form $X_t = X_0 + \sqrt{\beta}\,Z_t$ matches VE single-step corruption.

VP as an SDE

VP recursion with $\beta_n = \beta_t\,dt$ and $\sqrt{1-\beta_t\,dt} \approx 1 - \tfrac{\beta_t\,dt}{2}$:

$$dX_t = -\frac{\beta_t}{2}\,X_t\,dt + \sqrt{\beta_t}\,dZ_t.$$

Linear Ornstein–Uhlenbeck SDE.

Drift pulls toward zero; Brownian noise injects variance.

Continuous-time DDPM.

General Forward SDE

Both VE and VP are special cases of:

$$dX_t = f(X_t, t)\,dt + g(t)\,dZ_t.$$

VE

Drift $f = 0$.

Diffusion $g(t) = \sqrt{\beta}$.

VP

Drift $f(X_t, t) = -\tfrac{\beta_t}{2}X_t$.

Diffusion $g(t) = \sqrt{\beta_t}$.

The Solution Is a Distribution

Itô integral form:

$$X_t = X_0 + \int_0^t f(X_s, s)\,ds + \int_0^t g(s)\,dW_s.$$

Diffusion training needs only the marginal densities $\{p_t\}_{t\in[0,T]}$.

Goal: track how $p_t$ evolves from $p_0$ to $p_T$.

02

Fokker–Planck Equation

The PDE that governs $p_t$

Fokker–Planck, 1D

Theorem (Fokker–Planck).

For $dX_t = f(X_t, t)\,dt + g(t)\,dZ_t$, the marginal $p_t(x)$ satisfies:

$$\partial_t\,p_t(x) = -\partial_x\bigl(f(x,t)\,p_t(x)\bigr) + \frac{g^2(t)}{2}\,\partial_x^2\,p_t(x).$$

Deterministic PDE for the density.

Drift advects mass; diffusion spreads it.

Proof Setup

Let $\phi$ be smooth with compact support.

Track $\partial_t \mathbb{E}[\phi(X_t)]$ via two routes; equate them.

Small-step Euler approximation:

$$X_{t+\varepsilon} \approx X_t + \varepsilon\,f(X_t,t) + \sqrt{\varepsilon}\,g(t)\,Z,\qquad Z\sim\mathcal{N}(0,1).$$

Proof Outline

Target identity (holds for every test $\phi$):

$$\int \phi(x)\,\partial_t p_t(x)\,dx = \int \phi(x)\bigl[-\partial_x(f p_t) + \tfrac{g^2}{2}\,\partial_x^2 p_t\bigr]\,dx.$$
  1. Step 1. Taylor-expand $\phi(X_{t+\varepsilon})$ to second order.
  2. Step 2. Take expectation; collect surviving terms.
  3. Step 3. Integrate by parts; match integrands.

Step 1: Taylor Expand $\phi$

Expand $\phi(X_{t+\varepsilon})$ around $X_t$ to order $\varepsilon$:

$$\phi(X_{t+\varepsilon}) \approx \phi(X_t) + \phi'(X_t)\bigl(\varepsilon f + \sqrt{\varepsilon}\,g\,Z\bigr) + \tfrac{1}{2}\phi''(X_t)\,\varepsilon\,g^2\,Z^2 + O(\varepsilon^{3/2}).$$

Three orders contribute: $\sqrt{\varepsilon}$ term linear in $Z$, $\varepsilon$ drift, $\varepsilon$ noise variance.

Exercise. Verify each term, including boundary conditions $p_t(\pm\infty) = 0$ used in Step 3.

Step 2: Collect Surviving Terms

Expectation kills $\sqrt{\varepsilon}\,\phi'(X_t)\,g\,Z$ since $\mathbb{E}[Z]=0$.

Replace $Z^2$ by $1$. Subtract $\phi(X_t)$, divide by $\varepsilon$:

$$\partial_t\,\mathbb{E}[\phi(X_t)] = \mathbb{E}_{X \sim p_t}\!\left[\phi'(X)\,f(X, t) + \tfrac{1}{2}\phi''(X)\,g^2(t)\right].$$

Step 3: Integrate by Parts

Write expectations as integrals against $p_t$.

Move derivatives off $\phi$ using $p_t(\pm\infty)=0$:

$$\int \phi(x)\,\partial_t p_t(x)\,dx = \int \phi(x)\bigl[-\partial_x(f\,p_t) + \tfrac{g^2}{2}\,\partial_x^2 p_t\bigr]\,dx.$$

Identity in every $\phi$: integrands match pointwise.

This is Fokker–Planck.

Proof Recap

Single chain, test-function side:

$$\begin{aligned} \partial_t\,\mathbb{E}[\phi(X_t)] &\stackrel{(1)}{=}\; \mathbb{E}\!\left[\phi'(X_t)\,(\varepsilon f + \sqrt{\varepsilon}gZ) + \tfrac{1}{2}\phi''(X_t)\,\varepsilon g^2 Z^2\right]\big/\varepsilon \\ &\stackrel{(2)}{=}\; \mathbb{E}_{X\sim p_t}\!\left[\phi'(X)\,f + \tfrac{g^2}{2}\,\phi''(X)\right] \\ &\stackrel{(3)}{=}\; \int \phi(x)\bigl[-\partial_x(fp_t) + \tfrac{g^2}{2}\partial_x^2 p_t\bigr]\,dx \\ &\stackrel{(\ast)}{=}\; \int \phi(x)\,\partial_t p_t(x)\,dx. \end{aligned}$$
03

Anderson's Reverse SDE

A backward SDE with the same marginals

Anderson's Theorem

Theorem (Anderson, 1982).

For forward $dX_t = f(X_t, t)\,dt + g(t)\,dZ_t$ with $X_0 \sim p_0$, the reverse SDE

$$d\bar{X}_t = \bigl(f(\bar{X}_t, t) - g^2(t)\,\partial_x \log p_t(\bar{X}_t)\bigr)\,dt + g(t)\,d\bar{Z}_t,\quad \bar{X}_T \sim p_T$$

has the same marginals: $\bar{X}_t \stackrel{(d)}{=} X_t$ for every $t$.

Exercise. Extend to the isotropic multi-dim case $g(t) = \rho(t)\,I$. Which 1D steps survive?

Anderson, "Reverse-time diffusion equation models," Stochastic Processes and their Applications 1982.

Proof Setup

Let $\bar{p}_t$ be the marginal of $\bar{X}_t$.

Strategy: write the PDE that $\bar{p}_t$ must satisfy (Fokker–Planck applied to the reverse SDE, with time reversal).

Then check that the forward marginal $p_t$ solves it.

Uniqueness gives $\bar{p}_t = p_t$.

Proof Outline

Target: PDE equality at every $t$.

$$\partial_t p_t \;\stackrel{?}{=}\; -\partial_x\!\Bigl[\bigl(f - g^2\,\partial_x \log p_t\bigr) p_t\Bigr] - \tfrac{g^2}{2}\,\partial_x^2 p_t.$$
  1. Step 1. Time-reverse Fokker–Planck on $\bar{X}_t$.
  2. Step 2. Plug $\bar{p}_t = p_t$ and use $(\partial_x\log p_t)\,p_t = \partial_x p_t$.
  3. Step 3. Match against forward FP; conclude by uniqueness.

Step 1: Reverse-Time FP

Let $Y_t = \bar{X}_{T-t}$ run forward in $t$. Apply Fokker–Planck to $Y_t$:

$$\partial_t q_t(y) = \partial_y\!\Bigl[\bigl(f(y, T{-}t) - g^2(T{-}t)\,\partial_y \log p_{T{-}t}(y)\bigr)\,q_t(y)\Bigr] + \tfrac{g^2(T{-}t)}{2}\,\partial_y^2 q_t(y).$$

Change of variable $t \mapsto T - t$ gives the PDE for $\bar{p}_t$.

Step 2: $p_t$ Solves the Reverse PDE

Plug $\bar{p}_t = p_t$. Use $(\partial_x\log p_t)\,p_t = \partial_x p_t$:

$$\begin{aligned} &-\partial_x\!\Bigl[\bigl(f - g^2\,\partial_x \log p_t\bigr)\,p_t\Bigr] - \tfrac{g^2}{2}\,\partial_x^2 p_t \\ &\quad= -\partial_x(f\,p_t) + g^2\,\partial_x^2 p_t - \tfrac{g^2}{2}\,\partial_x^2 p_t \\ &\quad= -\partial_x(f\,p_t) + \tfrac{g^2}{2}\,\partial_x^2 p_t. \end{aligned}$$

The right-hand side is forward Fokker–Planck, equal to $\partial_t p_t$.

Proof Recap

$p_t$ matches both ends of the reverse PDE:

$$\begin{aligned} \partial_t p_t &\stackrel{(\text{forward FP})}{=}\; -\partial_x(f\,p_t) + \tfrac{g^2}{2}\,\partial_x^2 p_t \\ &\stackrel{(2)}{=}\; -\partial_x\!\Bigl[\bigl(f - g^2\,\partial_x\log p_t\bigr) p_t\Bigr] - \tfrac{g^2}{2}\,\partial_x^2 p_t \\ &\stackrel{(1)}{=}\; \text{reverse-FP RHS for } \bar{p}_t. \end{aligned}$$

Uniqueness of solutions: $\bar{p}_t = p_t$.

Why Anderson Matters

Continuous-time analog of Lecture 1's Bayes-based reverse step.

Reverse drift uses the score $\partial_x \log p_t$.

Generation recipe. Sample $\bar{X}_T \sim p_T$. Run reverse SDE with drift $f - g^2 s_\theta$ and diffusion $g$.

DDPM sampling is the Euler–Maruyama discretization.

04

Score Matching

Learning the score without knowing the marginals

Natural Loss Is Intractable

Match $s_\theta$ against the true score at every $t$:

$$\mathcal{L}(\theta) = \int_0^T \lambda(t)\,\mathbb{E}_{X_t}\!\left[\bigl\|s_\theta(X_t, t) - \partial_x \log p_t(X_t)\bigr\|^2\right]dt.$$

Forward sampling gives $X_t \sim p_t$ for free.

The marginal density $p_t$ and its gradient are not available.

Score Matching Theorem

Theorem (Vincent, 2011).

Up to a $\theta$-independent constant:

$$\mathcal{L}(\theta) = \int_0^T \lambda(t)\,\mathbb{E}_{X_0}\,\mathbb{E}_{X_t\,|\,X_0}\!\left[\bigl\|s_\theta(X_t, t) - \partial_x \log p_{t\mid 0}(X_t\,|\,X_0)\bigr\|^2\right] dt + C.$$

Conditional density $p_{t\mid 0}$: known noise kernel.

For VP the kernel is Gaussian; its score is closed form.

Vincent, "A connection between score matching and denoising autoencoders," Neural Computation 2011.

Proof Setup

Two ingredients link marginal score and conditional score.

Marginal density via the kernel:

$$p_t(X_t) = \int p_0(X_0)\,p_{t\mid 0}(X_t\,|\,X_0)\,dX_0.$$

Bayes posterior:

$$p_{0\mid t}(X_0\,|\,X_t) = \frac{p_0(X_0)\,p_{t\mid 0}(X_t\,|\,X_0)}{p_t(X_t)}.$$

Proof Outline

Target identity:

$$\mathbb{E}_{X_t}\!\bigl[\|s_\theta - \partial \log p_t\|^2\bigr] \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{X_0}\,\mathbb{E}_{X_t\,|\,X_0}\!\bigl[\|s_\theta - \partial \log p_{t\mid 0}\|^2\bigr] + C'.$$
  1. Step 1. Score as conditional expectation of the kernel score.
  2. Step 2. Expand squared norm; match cross terms.
  3. Step 3. Absorb the $\theta$-independent residual into $C'$.

Step 1: Score as Conditional Expectation

Differentiate the marginal under the integral; apply log-derivative; recognize Bayes:

$$\begin{aligned} \partial_{X_t}\log p_t(X_t) &= \frac{\partial_{X_t}\,p_t(X_t)}{p_t(X_t)} \\ &= \int \partial_{X_t}\!\log p_{t\mid 0}(X_t\,|\,X_0)\cdot\frac{p_0(X_0)\,p_{t\mid 0}(X_t\,|\,X_0)}{p_t(X_t)}\,dX_0 \\ &= \mathbb{E}_{X_0\,|\,X_t}\!\bigl[\partial_{X_t}\!\log p_{t\mid 0}(X_t\,|\,X_0)\bigr]. \end{aligned}$$

Step 2: Match the Cross Term

Expand the squared norm. The cross term, by Step 1:

$$\mathbb{E}_{X_t}\!\bigl[s_\theta\cdot \partial \log p_t\bigr] \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{X_0}\,\mathbb{E}_{X_t\,|\,X_0}\!\bigl[s_\theta\cdot \partial \log p_{t\mid 0}\bigr].$$

Squared marginal score is a $\theta$-independent term; absorb it into $C'$.

Squared conditional score plus the cross term reassemble into the conditional squared norm:

$$\mathbb{E}_{X_t}\!\bigl[\|s_\theta - \partial \log p_t\|^2\bigr] = \mathbb{E}_{X_0}\,\mathbb{E}_{X_t\,|\,X_0}\!\bigl[\|s_\theta - \partial \log p_{t\mid 0}\|^2\bigr] + C'.$$

Proof Recap

Single chain from intractable marginal score to tractable kernel score:

$$\begin{aligned} \mathbb{E}_{X_t}\!\bigl[\|s_\theta - \partial\log p_t\|^2\bigr] &\stackrel{(1)}{=}\; \mathbb{E}\!\bigl[\|s_\theta\|^2 - 2\,s_\theta\cdot \mathbb{E}_{X_0|X_t}[\partial\log p_{t|0}] + \|\partial\log p_t\|^2\bigr] \\ &\stackrel{(2)}{=}\; \mathbb{E}_{X_0}\mathbb{E}_{X_t|X_0}\!\bigl[\|s_\theta\|^2 - 2\,s_\theta\cdot \partial\log p_{t|0}\bigr] + C' \\ &\stackrel{(3)}{=}\; \mathbb{E}_{X_0}\mathbb{E}_{X_t|X_0}\!\bigl[\|s_\theta - \partial\log p_{t|0}\|^2\bigr] + C''. \end{aligned}$$

VP Noise Kernel (Recall)

For VP the kernel is Gaussian:

$$X_t \;\stackrel{(d)}{=}\; \gamma_t\,X_0 + \sigma_t\,\epsilon,\qquad \epsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I).$$

Conditional score reads off directly:

$$\partial_x \log p_{t\mid 0}(X_t\,|\,X_0) \;=\; -\,\frac{\epsilon}{\sigma_t}.$$

$\epsilon$-Reparameterization

Abstract form: $s_\theta(X_t, t) \approx -\,\dfrac{\epsilon}{\sigma_t}$.

Substitute $s_\theta(X_t, t) = -\,\dfrac{\epsilon_\theta(X_t, t)}{\sigma_t}$:

$$\tilde{\mathcal{L}}(\theta) = T\,\mathbb{E}_{X_0,\,\epsilon,\,t}\!\left[\frac{\lambda(t)}{\sigma_t^2}\,\bigl\|\epsilon_\theta(\gamma_t X_0 + \sigma_t\epsilon,\; t) - \epsilon\bigr\|^2\right].$$

Loss is plain noise regression.

Example: Ornstein–Uhlenbeck

For $dX_t = -\beta\,X_t\,dt + \sigma\,dW_t$ the kernel is closed form:

$$p_{t\mid 0}(X_t\,|\,X_0) = \mathcal{N}\!\left(e^{-\beta t}\,X_0,\;\frac{\sigma^2}{2\beta}(1 - e^{-2\beta t})\right).$$

Read off $\gamma_t = e^{-\beta t}$ and $\sigma_t^2 = \tfrac{\sigma^2}{2\beta}(1-e^{-2\beta t})$.

Multi-Dimensional Extension

For $\mathbf{X} \in \mathbb{R}^d$ with isotropic $g(t) = \rho(t)\,I$:

  • Fokker–Planck: $\partial_t p_t = -\nabla\!\cdot (f\,p_t) + \tfrac{\rho^2}{2}\,\Delta p_t$.
  • Anderson: reverse drift uses gradient score $\nabla\log p_t$.
  • Score matching: 1D proof runs component by component.

DDPM Is Discrete Score Matching

DDPM reparameterization: $\epsilon_\theta = \sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_n}\,s_\theta$. The VLB collapses to:

$$\mathcal{L}(\theta) = \sum_{n=1}^{N}\tilde{\lambda}_n\,\mathbb{E}\!\left[\bigl\|\epsilon - \epsilon_\theta\bigl(\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_n}X^{(0)} + \sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_n}\epsilon,\; n\bigr)\bigr\|^2\right].$$

DDPM = discrete-time score matching on the VP SDE.

Both train via $\epsilon$-prediction.

Lecture Recap

  • VE and VP are limits of $dX_t = f\,dt + g\,dZ_t$.
  • Marginals $p_t$ evolve by Fokker–Planck.
  • Anderson: reverse SDE matches forward marginals via the score.
  • Score matching: marginal-score loss equals kernel-score loss.
  • $\epsilon$-parameterization: noise regression. DDPM is a special case.
