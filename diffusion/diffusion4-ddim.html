01

What the DDPM Loss Actually Sees

Why we have freedom to redesign the forward process

A closer look at the DDPM loss

The $\epsilon$-prediction training loss per step is

$$L_{n-1} = \tilde{\lambda}_n\,\mathbb{E}_{X^{(0)},\,\epsilon}\!\left[\bigl\|\epsilon - \epsilon_\theta\bigl(\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_n}\,X^{(0)} + \sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_n}\,\epsilon,\; n\bigr)\bigr\|^2\right].$$

The only place the forward process enters is through the single-time marginal:

$$q\bigl(X^{(n)}\,|\,X^{(0)}\bigr) = \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_n}\,X^{(0)},\;(1-\bar{\alpha}_n)\,I\bigr).$$

The joint law $q(X^{(1:N)}\,|\,X^{(0)})$ never appears in the loss.

The freedom this gives us

Any forward process with

$$q(X^{(n)}\,|\,X^{(0)}) = \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_n}\,X^{(0)},\;(1-\bar{\alpha}_n)\,I\bigr)$$

yields the same $\epsilon$-prediction loss, and therefore the same trained network.

We can redesign the joint to make the corresponding reverse process useful in a new way. In particular, we can drop the Markov assumption.

DDIM: keep the same marginals, build a non-Markovian joint, and use a reverse process whose stochasticity is tunable down to zero.

02

DDIM's Non-Markovian Forward

Same marginals, tunable stochasticity

DDIM forward distribution

Fix a schedule $\{\bar{\alpha}_n\}$ (same as DDPM) and an arbitrary sequence $\{\sigma_n \geq 0\}$. Define

$$\begin{aligned} q(X^{(N)}\,|\,X^{(0)}) &= \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_N}\,X^{(0)},\;(1-\bar{\alpha}_N)\,I\bigr), \\[4pt] q(X^{(n)}\,|\,X^{(n+1)}, X^{(0)}) &= \mathcal{N}\!\left(\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_n}\,X^{(0)} + \frac{\sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_n - \sigma_{n+1}^2}}{\sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_{n+1}}}\,\bigl(X^{(n+1)} - \sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_{n+1}}\,X^{(0)}\bigr),\;\sigma_{n+1}^2\,I\right). \end{aligned}$$

The second conditional is non-Markovian: it depends on both the future step $X^{(n+1)}$ and the clean signal $X^{(0)}$.

Marginals match DDPM

One checks inductively that

$$q(X^{(n)}\,|\,X^{(0)}) = \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_n}\,X^{(0)},\;(1-\bar{\alpha}_n)\,I\bigr)$$

for every $n$, regardless of the choice of $\{\sigma_n\}$. In particular:

  • Any $\epsilon$-prediction network trained under DDPM is directly usable in DDIM.
  • The training cost is identical. Only the sampler differs.

Intuition: the DDIM joint carefully transports mass so that the single-time marginals coincide with the DDPM Markov chain, even though the couplings are different.

The deterministic choice $\sigma_n \equiv 0$

Setting $\sigma_n = 0$ makes the forward conditional Dirac:

$$q(X^{(n)}\,|\,X^{(n+1)}, X^{(0)}) = \delta\!\left(\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_n}\,X^{(0)} + \sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_n}\cdot\frac{X^{(n+1)} - \sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_{n+1}} X^{(0)}}{\sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_{n+1}}}\right).$$

Given $X^{(0)}$ and one later step, every other step is determined. The chain still has the same marginals as DDPM, but with zero fresh randomness between steps.

The interpolation between $\sigma_n = \sqrt{\beta_n \cdot (1-\bar\alpha_n)/(1-\bar\alpha_{n+1})}$ (DDPM-like) and $\sigma_n = 0$ (deterministic) is continuous.

03

DDIM Sampling

From $\epsilon_\theta$ to samples, with tunable noise

Training: nothing changes

DDIM trains the same $\epsilon$-prediction network as DDPM. Given $X^{(0)}$, draw $\epsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I)$ and $n \sim \text{Unif}$, form

$$X^{(n)} = \sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_n}\,X^{(0)} + \sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_n}\,\epsilon,$$

and regress $\epsilon_\theta(X^{(n)}, n)$ against $\epsilon$.

Training is Markov, because the loss only depends on single-time marginals. Sampling is where the non-Markovian structure pays off.

Predicted clean signal

At sampling time, invert the noise equation to estimate $X^{(0)}$ from the current state:

$$\hat{X}^{(0)} = \frac{X^{(n+1)} - \sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_{n+1}}\,\epsilon_\theta(X^{(n+1)}, n+1)}{\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_{n+1}}}.$$

This is just the $\epsilon$-prediction rearranged. It tells us where the network thinks the clean sample lies, given the noisy one.

DDIM reverse update

Plug $\hat{X}^{(0)}$ into the DDIM forward conditional to define the sampler:

$$p_\theta(X^{(n)}\,|\,X^{(n+1)}) = q\bigl(X^{(n)}\,|\,X^{(n+1)}, \hat{X}^{(0)}\bigr).$$

Explicitly,

$$X^{(n)} = \sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_n}\,\hat{X}^{(0)} + \sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_n-\sigma_{n+1}^2}\,\epsilon_\theta(X^{(n+1)}, n+1) + \sigma_n\,Z^{(n)},\quad Z^{(n)}\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I).$$

Three pieces: a direction toward the data (via $\hat{X}^{(0)}$), a direction along the noise, and optional fresh noise.

Sampling algorithm

DDIM Sampling
  1. Sample $X^{(N)} \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I)$.
  2. For $n = N{-}1, N{-}2, \ldots, 0$:
    1. Compute $\hat{X}^{(0)} = \bigl(X^{(n+1)} - \sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_{n+1}}\,\epsilon_\theta(X^{(n+1)}, n+1)\bigr)/\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_{n+1}}$.
    2. Optionally sample $Z^{(n)}\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I)$ (skip if $\sigma_n = 0$).
    3. Set $X^{(n)} = \sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_n}\,\hat{X}^{(0)} + \sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_n-\sigma_{n+1}^2}\,\epsilon_\theta(X^{(n+1)}, n+1) + \sigma_n\,Z^{(n)}$.

DDPM sampling is the special case with the DDPM-matching $\sigma_n$. The deterministic case $\sigma_n \equiv 0$ drops step (b) entirely.

$\sigma_n \equiv 0$: a probability flow ODE

When $\sigma_n = 0$, the reverse step is deterministic. Taking the continuous-time limit yields the probability flow ODE:

$$\frac{dX_t}{dt} = f(X_t, t) - \frac{g^2(t)}{2}\,\nabla_{\mathbf{x}} \log p_t(X_t),$$

whose flow has the same marginals $\{p_t\}$ as the forward SDE. DDIM with $\sigma_n = 0$ is a specific discretization of this ODE for VP diffusion.

Stochastic forward process, deterministic generative process. Same marginals. The score still does all the work.

04

Consequences of Determinism

Why people care about DDIM in practice

What you get for free

Fewer sampling steps

Because the reverse step is deterministic, one can take large strides on the index axis. 50 steps often match 1000-step DDPM quality.

Inversion

A deterministic generator can be run backwards: given an image, recover the latent noise that produced it. Used for editing and attribution.

Latent interpolation

Interpolating noise latents yields smooth, meaningful paths in image space. With a Markov sampler this is noisy and inconsistent.

All three hinge on the same underlying fact: $X^{(0)}$ is a deterministic function of $X^{(N)}$ under $\sigma_n = 0$.

DDPM or DDIM?

DDPM samplerDDIM sampler
ForwardMarkovNon-Markovian
Reverse variance$\sigma_n$ fixed$\sigma_n$ free (including $0$)

In modern diffusion tooling the two are the same model served by different solvers. Which one runs at inference time is a configuration flag, not a retraining decision.

Lecture recap

  • The DDPM $\epsilon$-prediction loss depends only on $q(X^{(n)}\,|\,X^{(0)})$, never on the joint.
  • DDIM preserves that marginal while replacing the Markov forward with a non-Markovian one parameterized by $\{\sigma_n\}$.
  • With $\sigma_n \equiv 0$ the reverse is deterministic: it discretizes the probability-flow ODE.
  • Training is shared. Sampling is a solver choice.
  • Practical wins: fewer steps, invertibility, clean latent interpolation.

  2. Show that when $\sigma_n^2 = \beta_n\,(1-\bar{\alpha}_n)/(1-\bar{\alpha}_{n+1})$, the DDIM reverse update reduces to the DDPM reverse update from Lecture 2.
  3. Show that the $\sigma_n = 0$ DDIM update is a valid explicit discretization of the probability-flow ODE $\dfrac{dX_t}{dt} = f(X_t, t) - \tfrac{g^2(t)}{2}\,\nabla \log p_t(X_t)$ for VP diffusion.
