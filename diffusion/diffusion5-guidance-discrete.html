Classifier Guidance

Bayes decomposition and the $\omega$-scaled reverse SDE

Conditional Generation

So far: sample from $P_{\mathbf{X}}$. In practice: want $P_{\mathbf{X}\,|\,Y}$ for label or prompt $Y$.

Naive approach: train a separate conditional score $s_\theta(\mathbf{x}\,|\,Y=y)$ per label. Expensive, does not scale.

Classifier guidance: reuse the pretrained unconditional score, steer with a small auxiliary classifier.

Dhariwal & Nichol, "Diffusion Models Beat GANs on Image Synthesis", NeurIPS 2021.

Bayes Decomposition of Conditional Score

From Bayes' rule and taking $\nabla_{\mathbf{x}} \log$:

$$\nabla_{\mathbf{x}} \log P_{\mathbf{X}\,|\,Y}(\mathbf{x}\,|\,y) = \nabla_{\mathbf{x}} \log P_{\mathbf{X}}(\mathbf{x}) + \nabla_{\mathbf{x}} \log P_{Y\,|\,\mathbf{X}}(y\,|\,\mathbf{x}).$$

$P_Y(y)$ drops (no $\mathbf{x}$ dependence). Conditional score = unconditional score + classifier gradient.

Classifier on Noisy Inputs

Sampling operates on noisy state $\mathbf{X}^{(n)}$, so train a classifier that works at that noise level:

$$c_\phi(\mathbf{X}^{(n)}, y, n) \;\approx\; P_{Y\,|\,\mathbf{X}^{(n)}}(y\,|\,\mathbf{X}^{(n)}).$$

Regular classifier with noise index $n$ as extra input. Trained on noisy images from the forward process.

Classifier-Guided Reverse SDE

Replace unconditional score by the Bayes-decomposed conditional, amplified by $\omega > 1$:

$$d\bar{\mathbf{X}}_t = \Bigl(f - g^2\bigl(\nabla\log p_t(\bar{\mathbf{X}}_t) + \omega\,\nabla \log c_\phi(\bar{\mathbf{X}}_t, y, t)\bigr)\Bigr)dt + g\,d\bar{\mathbf{Z}}_t.$$

Larger $\omega$: sharper, more label-consistent samples. Trades diversity for fidelity.

Inpainting

Conditioning on a fixed subset of the signal

Setup

$\Omega$: pixel-subsampling operator. $Y = \Omega(X^{(0)})$: observed pixels. $\bar{\Omega}$: complementary pixels to generate.

Let $Z^{(t)} = \bar{\Omega}(X^{(t)})$. It follows its own SDE on the complement:

$$dZ^{(t)} = \bar{\Omega}(f)\bigl(Z^{(t)}, t\bigr)\,dt + \bar{\Omega}(g)(t)\,d\bar{W}^{(t)}.$$

Brownian motion decomposes cleanly: coordinate-wise independent.

Approximating the Conditional Score

Want $p_t\bigl(Z^{(t)}\,|\,\Omega(X^{(0)}) = Y\bigr)$. Condition on the noisy version of the observation, use a single-sample approximation:

$$p_t\bigl(Z^{(t)}\,|\,\Omega(X^{(0)}) = Y\bigr) \;\approx\; p_t\bigl(Z^{(t)}\,|\,\hat{\Omega}(X^{(t)})\bigr),$$

where $\hat{\Omega}(X^{(t)})$: noise-corrupted version of $Y$ at time $t$, generated by running the forward process on observed pixels.

Inpainting Sampler

Score-Guided Inpainting
  1. Sample $X^{(N)} \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I)$ on the full image.
  2. For $n = N, N{-}1, \ldots, 1$:
    1. Sample $\epsilon_n \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I)$; form $\zeta_n = \beta_n\,Y + \gamma_n\,\epsilon_n$ (noised observation at level $n$).
    2. Run one reverse-SDE step using $\bar{\Omega}\bigl(s_\theta([\bar{Z}^{(n)}; \zeta_n], n)\bigr)$ as guidance on generated pixels.

Classifier-Free Guidance

Guidance without an extra classifier

Why Drop the Classifier?

Classifier guidance: second network, trained on noisy inputs, evaluated at every sampling step. Expensive and hard to train in high-noise regime.

Key identity:

$$\nabla \log p_t(\mathbf{x}) + \omega\,\nabla \log p_t(y\,|\,\mathbf{x}) \;\equiv\; \omega\,\nabla \log p_t(\mathbf{x}\,|\,y) + (1-\omega)\,\nabla \log p_t(\mathbf{x}).$$

Classifier-free guidance: one model provides both conditional and unconditional scores.

Ho & Salimans, "Classifier-Free Diffusion Guidance", NeurIPS Workshop 2021.

A Dual-Role Network

Train $\epsilon_\theta(\mathbf{X}^{(n)}, y, n)$ where $y$ is the label or null token $\varnothing$:

  • $\epsilon_\theta(\mathbf{X}^{(n)}, y, n) \approx$ conditional noise under label $y$.
  • $\epsilon_\theta(\mathbf{X}^{(n)}, \varnothing, n) \approx$ unconditional noise.

Training trick: replace $y$ by $\varnothing$ with probability $p_\text{drop} \approx 0.1$–$0.2$. Network learns both in a single pass.

CFG Sampling Rule

At inference, combine two forward passes:

$$\tilde{\epsilon}(\mathbf{X}^{(n)}, y, n) \;=\; \omega\,\epsilon_\theta(\mathbf{X}^{(n)}, y, n) - (\omega - 1)\,\epsilon_\theta(\mathbf{X}^{(n)}, \varnothing, n),\qquad \omega > 1.$$

Use $\tilde{\epsilon}$ in place of $\epsilon_\theta$ in any DDPM / DDIM sampler.

Push toward label-consistent mode (weight $\omega$), pull back with unconditional (weight $\omega-1$). Net: label pull sharpened by $\omega$.

CFG in Practice

Design levers

  • Label-drop probability $p_\text{drop}$
  • Guidance scale $\omega$ at sampling
  • Null-token embedding (learnable)

Trade-offs

  • Larger $\omega$: sharper, less diverse
  • Two forward passes per step: $2\times$ cost
  • No auxiliary model to train or serve

Discrete Diffusion

When data lives on $\{1, \ldots, K\}$ instead of $\mathbb{R}^d$

What Breaks in the Discrete Case

Continuous diffusion relies on adding Gaussian noise. Discrete data (tokens, categories) does not admit addition.

Natural substitute: random transitions. Switch $x$ to a different value with some probability. State distribution converges to a fixed stationary law.

Discrete diffusion: "additive noise" replaced by "stochastic transitions." Forward, reverse, score, loss: same recipe.

Forward: Transition Matrices

Let $p_t \in [0,1]^K$ be the probability vector of $X_t$. Transition matrix $Q_t \in \mathbb{R}^{K\times K}$ with $Q_t(y, x) = \Pr(X_{t+1} = y\,|\,X_t = x)$:

$$p_{t+1} = Q_t\,p_t.$$

For a valid noising process: $p_t \to \pi$ for a fixed $\pi$ regardless of $p_0$.

Two standard choices: uniform and absorbing (masking).

Uniform vs. Absorbing Transitions

Uniform

Stay with prob $1-\epsilon$; otherwise jump uniformly to another token.

Stationary: uniform on $\{1,\ldots,K\}$.

Absorbing (mask)

Non-mask tokens get replaced by $[\text{M}]$ at rate $\epsilon$; mask stays mask.

Stationary: all mass on $[\text{M}]$.

Continuous-Time ODE on Probability Vectors

Take $\Delta t \to 0$: discrete updates become a linear ODE:

$$\frac{dp_t}{dt} = Q_t\,p_t,\qquad p_0 = p_\text{data}.$$

Reverse rate matrix (for $x \ne y$):

$$\overline{Q}_t(y, x) = \frac{p_t(y)}{p_t(x)}\,Q_t(x, y),\qquad \overline{Q}_t(x, x) = -\sum_{y \ne x}\overline{Q}_t(y, x).$$

Diagonal fixed by mass conservation. Novel quantity: the ratio $p_t(y)/p_t(x)$.

The Discrete Score Is a Ratio

Continuous analog of the score $\nabla_{\mathbf{x}} \log p_t(\mathbf{x})$: the marginal ratio:

$$s(x, t) = \bigl(p_t(1)/p_t(x),\; p_t(2)/p_t(x),\; \ldots,\; p_t(K)/p_t(x)\bigr)^\top \in [0, \infty)^K.$$

Learned by a neural network $s_\theta(x, t)$.

Continuous: gradient of log density. Discrete: ratio of densities between states. Same role, different object.

Score Entropy Loss

Squared loss on $s_\theta - p_t(y)/p_t(x)$ fails: accepts negative estimates. Lou et al. (2024) propose score entropy:

$$\mathcal{L}_\text{SE}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}_{x_t \sim p_t}\!\left[\sum_{y \ne x_t} w_{x_t, y}\!\left(s_\theta(x_t, t)_y - \frac{p_t(y)}{p_t(x_t)}\,\log s_\theta(x_t, t)_y + C\!\left(\frac{p_t(y)}{p_t(x_t)}\right)\right)\right],$$

with $C(a) = a(\log a - 1)$. The $\log s_\theta$ term forces positivity. Tractable denoising variant $\mathcal{L}_\text{DSE}$ uses $p_{t\,|\,0}$, analogous to score matching.

Lou, Meng & Ermon, "Discrete Diffusion Modeling by Estimating the Ratios of the Data Distribution", ICML 2024 (Best Paper).

Practical Sequence Space

Real text: $\mathcal{X} = \{1, \ldots, K\}^L$. Direct transition matrix: $K^L \times K^L$ entries, intractable.

Factored assumption: each token perturbed independently. This implies the Hamming-sparsity condition:

$$Q_t(\mathbf{y}, \mathbf{x}) = 0\quad\text{whenever}\quad d_\text{H}(\mathbf{y}, \mathbf{x}) \geq 2.$$

Reverse process only needs $s_\theta$ on Hamming-distance-1 pairs.
